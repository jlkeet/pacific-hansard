MR T. VAVIA: Thank you, Mr Chairman. Kia Orana to you this afternoon. I rise to give my support to the Clause 13, included in the Bill before us. It states in Clause 13, Sub-Section (1), “No nuclear wastes or other radioactive matter in whatsoever form or condition shall be transported by a vessel or platform into Cook Islands waters except as specified under Article IV of the London Dumping Convention.” This also includes the airspace of the Cook Islands. In fact, it is very interesting to note, Mr Chairman, that it will be illegal to transport nuclear waste on the seabed and even under the subsoil below the Cook Islands waters. I feel this Clause is a tough one with not allowing any nuclear or radioactive waste materials to be transported or shifted through our waters and even our airspace. This Clause is very different from Clauses 3 and 4. Those Clauses dealt with the discharge of wastes into our ocean. This Clause specifies and concentrates that, no nuclear waste or radioactive matter is to be transported through our airspace, on our seabed or subsoil below our waters.
I wish to congratulate the Minister for introducing this Clause into the Bill before the House in order that we Cook Islanders are protected in the future. I’d like to emphasise the fact that any amount of contamination of nuclear waste, minor or large, in our waters will be lethal and will take years to decompose. We are all aware of the end result should something like this occur. As the Minister of Education had said earlier, one day we will see the benefits of what we are influencing in this House today. Right now, I congratulate the Minister for incorporating this most important Section into the Bill. This Bill however, is rather silent on particular vessels should they come through our waters in times of war. But the Bill applies to our time of peace today. If there is going to be a war, we will deal with that matter at the appropriate time. In the meantime, I am glad that this Section has been included in the Bill. Thank you, Mr Chairman.
