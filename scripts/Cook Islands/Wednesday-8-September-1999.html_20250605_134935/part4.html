It may be large in number as opposed to those amounts allocated to other Ministries but, is it sufficient to efficiently and effectively service the needs of our Outer Islands. Now that question, Mr Chairman, will be addressed in my effort to find them a solution to satisfy the needs of the Outer Islands.
You know, Mr Chairman, yesterday in this House, the Honourable Member for the island of Rakahanga, Papa Pupuke Robati asked a couple of questions. You know, one of the questions he raised was why is it that the island of Manihiki is without a doctor? Or why is it that the island of Rakahanga is without a doctor? You know, Mr Chairman, if I am compelled to answer those two questions, I will try to find an answer that would see the easy way out. One of those answers that I would give to get the easy way out is to tell him, that because of the population ratio. The island of Rakahanga does not need a doctor. Or is it because the other answer that I would give is that because there is no doctor’s residence on the island. Therefore Rakahanga cannot have a doctor. Or a third possible answer would be, Mr Chairman, because the mosquitoes are abundant in Rakahanga. Therefore no doctor would like to go there.
Now an issue would then come, Mr Chairman, whether I, as Minister responsible for Outer Islands Development, is giving a truthful answer or whether I, Mr Chairman as a Member of Parliament in this House would be misleading the House. I think the answer would be that although a doctor is not readily available at this point in time, we would need to reassess, re-evaluate and review all those needs pertaining to the welfare of our people in the Outer Islands. I do hope, Mr Chairman, that at the end of my presentation, Members of Parliament from the Outer Islands will agree for us to sit down, put our thinking caps on for in-depth consultation. Having done those consultations, Mr Chairman, we would all reach common grounds whereby some policies and guidelines would be developed and to point us in the right direction.

With Cabinet approval, I have appointed a Task Force comprising of the following people: Mr Tai Herman, Mayor of Aitutaki; Mr Roger Malcolm, Mayor of Atiu; Taukea Raui, Chief Executive Officer for Mauke; Orthaniel Tangianau, Chief Executive Officer of Mangaia and Anna Katoa representative from the Island of Manihiki. The Task Force, Mr Chairman, is commissioned to evaluate and review the needs of the Outer Islands. A period of up to six weeks has been anticipated for the completion of this task and that a draft report is to be submitted to the Mayoral Forum scheduled to take place on 18th October 1999 in Tongareva. When the Report is tabled at the next Mayoral Forum in Tongareva, the Mayors will be given the opportunity to discuss the contents of the Report in full. No doubt we will all reach an agreement to finalise the paper which will eventually provide some policies and guidelines for the Devolution Process to proceed further. At that meeting, I am requesting input from Government Representatives and Members of Parliament from the Outer Islands. Until these steps are carried out, all Government assets and machinery will remain under the management and control of the Ministry of Outer Islands Development.
