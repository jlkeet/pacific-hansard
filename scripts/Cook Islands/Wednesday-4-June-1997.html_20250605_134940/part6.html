Income Tax Bill Second Reading.
The Associate Minister of Finance Papamama Pokino.
HON. P. POKINO: Mr Speaker,
I move:
That the Income Tax Bill be read a Second time
Mr Speaker, Even though this Bill is thicker than the previous one, most of the matters incorporated in it are just a continuation or are taken from the old Income Tax Act. There are a few additions that have been included merely to improve and update the old Act. I feel that the most noteworthy inclusion, Mr Speaker, is the provision for $6,000 exemption from your pay before your income is assessed for Income Tax. Secondly, the other important area is the ceiling, the tiers, this is the level of payment you are required to pay tax on. For example, Mr Speaker, from memory from 0 to $10,000 you are required to pay 20% tax. From $10,001 to $20,000 I think the figure is 25%, and from $20,0001 to above that is 30%.
Beside those major improvements there are other minor ones which I do not need to go through. But I want to reiterate that for this Bill to be effective it must be considered in combination with the VAT. Once again may I repeat, I feel that we have thrashed the debate on the Principles and the Merits of the tax package. Therefore, I do not anticipate any prolonged debate on the Principles and Merits of the Income Tax Bill.
Thank you Mr Speaker.

A country that has no resources that we can manipulate to extract revenue for the development of our country. We have no alternative but to initiate a taxation system in order for Government to generate revenue to pay its public bills.
Just a few minutes ago, Mr Speaker, we passed the taxation that is initiated for mainly business people. Now we are talking of a Bill that will impose upon our people, Income Tax. However, as I said the other day, the two bills have to be taken in consideration - its importance has got to compromise each other. Under this new Income Tax Bill, we will be seeing exemptions that have been highlighted by some speakers of up to the amount of $6,000 ie) for the first $6,000 that you earn you will not be paying any taxes on that amount. Thereafter, a tier system of taxing will be placed on any income that you collect or accumulate after the $6,000 that you originally earn.
However, there are other areas that people have been querying, as to when the tax will apply and how the tax would apply. One area Mr Speaker, is that of insurance. We are aware, Mr Speaker, from our session in camera with the experts that the insurance will be exempted. That included, will have a total exemption figure of up to $5,000. The other money that is common to our people is that derived from superannuation. When you are contributing to super this will be taxable. That means if you are contributing to super you will be paying tax. But the beauty about that, is that when you retire and are entitled to your superannuation, during that time you collect your superannuation you are not paying any tax at all.
I want to highlight those areas, Mr Speaker, because these two issues, the insurance and superannuation are issues that our people are very much interested to know, as to whether they are taxed or not.
Also under this new Bill, Mr Speaker, are the same exemptions given to spouses and children, similar to the old Act. That means at the end of the year, from the money that is spent, you can claim some benefit on a child or your wife or husband that does not work or earn money at all.
There is also the issue of donations, Mr Speaker, that are exempted from business as well as individuals. You can donate money up to the amount of $5,000 to any organisation, namely to sport, the church, and also to village committees. At the end of the financial year, you can claim that $5,000 back from the Tax Department. This
