Income Tax Amendment (No.2) Bill : Second Reading. The Honourable Minister, Pa Pokino.
HON. P. POKINO: Thank you Mr speaker. Mr Speaker, I move:
That the Income Tax Amendment (No.2) Bill be read a Second time
I don’t think it will take us long to debate over this Bill, Mr Speaker, because it is only a very short Bill. The reason why this is brought in is, as we know it is an amendment to the Income Tax Bill which was enacted on the 1st July. Yes, there is a change in the Income Tax law which came into force on the 1st July. From the beginning of this year to the end of June we found that there was a difference, to do with the tax imposed upon workers. From the 1st July going on to the end of December we know there is a little difference to do with the tax imposed upon the people. We know in the past that the Income Tax imposed upon the people was consistent right through from the beginning of the year to the end of the year, but for this Amendment that we have before the House today there is a difference from the beginning of the year to the end

of June. There is also a difference from the 1st July to the end of December. So the purpose of the Amendment is to even out the payment of tax from the beginning of the year to the end of the year. So really, Mr Speaker, this Amendment applies to this year only. After the 31st December it will become void. So that is why I said it is a short simple amendment and it should not invite any debate whatsoever.
The idea is so that some workers don’t get charged excessively by the tax department or they don’t receive a refund in excess from the tax department. It is to even out the payment of tax. So really that is the purpose of the Amendment, Mr Speaker. Thank you.
