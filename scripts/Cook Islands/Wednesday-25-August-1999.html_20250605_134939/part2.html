MR SPEAKER took the Chair at 1.00 p.m.
MR SPEAKER: May we all be seated. Kia Orana. Greetings to all Honourable Members this afternoon and I would like to welcome you all. Before I call upon the Leader of the House, I would like to say, whatever I do in here I will try to do as honestly as I can. I am hoping that we have an enjoyable time here at our meetings.
Now I will call upon the Leader of the House, the Honourable Deputy Prime Minister.
HON. N. GEORGE: Thank you Mr Speaker. To you Mr Speaker and the Clerk of the House and to all your staff members, Kia Orana. To the Prime Minister Dr Joseph Williams and all Members of Cabinet and Members of the Coalition Caucus, Kia Orana. To the Leader of the Opposition Dr Maoate, his Deputy and Caucus Members, Kia Orana. Mr Speaker, I also, lastly, extend a warm greeting to the people of this country. Kia Orana in the name of our Lord.
Mr Speaker, in order for us to proceed with our Order of the Day, I move:
That Standing Order 21 be suspended in order that the day for the Ceremonial Opening of Parliament be postponed until such future date.
The reason Mr Speaker is that there is one vacancy in the House. It would be futile to have a Ceremonial Opening if there is a vacancy. It is the intention of Government that when the By-Election is completed and when there are 25 members in the House we will consider a date for the Ceremonial Opening. I am quite sure that with a clear explanation like that there will be no need for further comment. Thank you Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Honourable Deputy Prime Minister. I call for a Seconder to the Motion?
Seconded by the Honourable Member for Tamarua, Mii Parima.
Are there any further speakers? The Leader of the Opposition, the Honourable Dr Maoate.
HON. DR T. MAOATE: Thank you Mr Speaker. Mr Speaker Kia Orana on this special day. To the Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, Cabinet Ministers, Sir Geoffrey, and Members of Government, Kia Orana. To my colleagues in the Opposition, Deputy Leader and Members in the Opposition, to all our people listening in, and a special one to our friend the Financial Secretary. Kia Orana to all of you. And of course, to your staff members, Mr Speaker.
