OPENING PRAYER
MR SPEAKER (T.TURA): Please be seated. I call the House to order. Kia Orana
to all of us this afternoon, Kia Orana in the Name of our Lord Jesus Christ.
To the Prime Minister and to your Deputy and all Members of your Cabinet and
Government, Kia Orana. To the Leader of the Opposition and all your Members, Kia
Orana. To all our family and friends in the Gallery who had joined us this afternoon
for our special occasion, we welcome you to this Honourable House.
Our first business of the House today is the swearing-in of our new Member of
Parliament. Like I said in Maori, Honourable Members, the first item on our business
paper today is the swearing-in of the new Member for Manihiki, the Honourable Akaiti
Puna, pursuant to Standing Order 32. May I, on behalf of all Members of Parliament,
congratulate Mrs Puna on her successful election to this Honourable House.
I now invite Mrs Puna to rise and present herself at the Table to be sworn in as the new
Member for Manihiki.
Mrs Akaiti Puna, by the powers vested in me as Speaker of the House, I hereby invite
you to take the Oath of Allegiance as prescribed by Article 30 of the Constitution of the
Cook Islands.
(Swearing-in of a new Member of Parliament)
MR SPEAKER: Honourable Members, that completes the swearing-in of our new
Member this afternoon. Congratulations Mrs Puna and may you have a joyful and
productive journey throughout your term in Parliament.
It is my honour now to say, that Parliament is hereby suspended until 3.00 p.m. to
enable the newly sworn Member to spend time with her family, followed by
refreshments.
Sitting suspended at 1.12 p.m.

The question is to the Minister of Culture, if he could explain to this Honourable House,
I believe not many Members are aware of what is happening. In town, there are some

MR SPEAKER: Before the Minister answers the question, I would like to
acknowledge the Mama in the Public Gallery for your presence in Parliament, you are

HON. G. ANGENE: Kia Orana everyone in the Name of our Lord, our Prime Minister
and your Deputy, Ministers and everyone here today, Mr Speaker, thank you very
much.
Honourable Member for Matavera, thank you for your question. I believe your question
is not to intimidate me but rather a very good question directly for me. Thank you also
to one of my special guest who is in the Gallery here today, it is good that you are here
today so you can witness what goes on in here today.
The actual question being asked is, what exactly is this house that I am building on the
grounds, namely the Constitution Park? This is not the Constitution Honourable
Member, and because I am the Minister of Culture, Maori traditions, the name of the
place is called Ta’ua Terevete. Mr Speaker, I was known as Mr Action Man, but it is
now changed to Mr Terevete, hence why this place is called Ta’ua Terevete, a place for
the people to enjoy.
The purpose of this house is for the people to use when it comes to special celebrations.
Firstly, when the time comes for the parade (float), we all know we get squeezed under
marquees and it is very hot, this is why it was built for.
Secondly, anybody in the Cook Islands can do their Prayer Service here, they can bring
their Church here. Otherwise, anybody wanting to use it for wedding purposes,
birthday, Zumba, and it would be suitable for ladies like Selina and other Members of
Parliament for them to use. Whoever wishes to plan any activities for the people, it is
the reason why this house is being built, Honourable Member.
Lastly Mr Speaker, to the Government, our Prime Minister and Ministers who had
supported this request, because this place is not ours, it is for the VIP and for the people.
That is why the Government of today supported this, because it would be given to the

MR W. HEATHER: Thank you Mr Speaker. My question is to our Prime Minister,
Minister of Police. Mr Speaker, in the past few days, we have seen a tremendous
increase of pills or drugs, because one of my children was jailed for taking one of these
drugs. They went out at night to enjoy themselves and were caught up with the wrong
crowd and were given some unknown substances to take, in which they took without
realising the ramifications behind it. This is a concern to the family. This is a concern
to the people of the village that I speak of.
So I would like to ask you, the Prime Minister, what is happening at the borders? Are
these being controlled and if there is any investigations in place to how these pills or
drugs are being imported into the country?
MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Prime Minister, you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker, thank you very much to the Honourable
Member for this question.
I know Mr Speaker that our Police, working with our border agencies, have a very
comprehensive programme to try to combat illegal substance, illegal drugs and
contraband coming into our country. Over the last year, the number of flights and the
number of ships that were coming into our country, the number of yachts that were
coming into our country has been drastically reduced. I think this would have gone
some way to reduce any avenue for these illegal substances to be able to come into our
country. However, Mr Speaker, with the opening now of our borders again, we can see
that there will be an increase, not only in the flights, but also in the number of people
coming into our country.
Therefore, we need to make sure that our border agencies, our law enforcement
agencies are up to speed and with their enforcement and with their detection of any
illegal substances coming into our country. I have no doubt Mr Speaker, that this will
be something that I will be discussing with our new Police Commissioner who have
just arrived into our country over the weekend. May I take this time to congratulate Mr
James Keenan who has taken on the role of Police Commissioner of the Cook Islands.
I am not aware of the personal circumstances of the Honourable Member with his
family member but it will be something that I will follow up with the Police to find out
what the situation is with this person. Thank you Mr Speaker.

My question is actually going out to the Minister of Justice. Just recently, a young

be given a hyphenated name. A combination of both the mothers and fathers name.

right that they have has been outlined in our Constitution. So, my question here is, what
steps had been taken by you as Minister and also the Ministry of Justice to change this
outdated practice that is not even supported by law in this country?

In response to the question by the Honourable Member from Titikaveka, yes, we have
seen on television and in our local newspaper too, questions raised by the Leader of the
Opposition.
The Head of Ministry of Justice is looking into the Birth and Registration at ways in
which such problems can be rectified. There are processes in place
The Head of Ministry of Justice is trying to sort this out because we have our own
process when we register names for our children. This has been on for so long, so it is
time that this process is carefully looked at and is dealt with properly so that it may
accommodate some of the weaknesses that our people are experiencing. We all know
that we have processes when it comes to registering your child, and you can have them
registered under your parents’ name, or a combination of both your mother and father,
but there is a process as outlined in our laws. However, this is being reviewed in order
to alleviate the burdens our people are faced with. Thank you.
