ELECTION PETITIONS- STATEMENT BY THE ACTING SPEAKER

Mr ACTING SPEAKER- Honorable Members in accordance with section 221 of the Organic Law on National and Local Level Government Elections, I present the copy of the National Court Order which served as a Court of Disputed Returns; EP38/2022 dated 24 January 2022 in the Matter between Ms Delilah Weka Gore and Mr Henry Amuli in which the Court ordered that it is hereby declared that grounds, 3 & 4 of the petition are upheld under section 212 (1) of the Organic Law on the National and Local Government Election.

It is declared that the first respondent who was returned as the elected Member for Sohe Open Electorate was not duly elected pursuant to section 212 (If) of the Organic Law on National and Local Level Government Election.

It is declared the election of the first respondent for the the Sohe Open Electorate declared on 5th August 2022 in the 2022 National General Elections is absolutely void pursuant to section 212 (1h) of the Organic Law on National and Local Level Government Elections.

The second respondent is hereby ordered to conduct a by-election for the SoheÂ­ Open Electorate forthwith pursuant to section 212 (3) of the Organic Law on National and Local Level Government Elections.

The first respondent shall subject to any specific order for cost made during the course of the proceedings; may the petitioner's cost of the petition. The registrar shall forthwith refund to the petitioner the security for cost deposited under section 209 of the Organic Law on National and Local Government Election. The registrar shall under section 221 of the Organic Law on National and Local Level Government Elections promptly forward to the Clerk of the National Parliament a copy of the order.
