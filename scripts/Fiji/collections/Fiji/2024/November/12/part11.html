SECRETARY-GENERAL.- Honourable Members, I now call for nominations for the
Speaker of the Parliament of the Republic of Fiji.
I call upon the honourable Prime Minister and Minister for Foreign Affairs, Climate Change,
Environment, Civil Service, Information, Public Enterprises and Veteran Affairs for his nomination.
HON. S.L. RABUKA.- I move:
That the honourable, Mr. Justice Filimone Jitoko, do take the Chair as Speaker of
Parliament.
HON. V. NAUPOTO.- Madam Secretary-General, I second the nomination.
SECRETARY-GENERAL.- I now invite the honourable Prime Minister to give a brief
account of the nominee’s credentials.
HON. S.L. RABUKA.- Madam Secretary-General, the honourable Deputy Prime Ministers,
honourable Ministers and Assistant Ministers, honourable Leader of the Opposition, honourable
Members of Parliament, members of the public who may be watching us via television and livestream
or listening to their radios. Ni sa bula vinaka and a very good morning to you all.
Madam Secretary-General, I rise in accordance with Standing Order 17(4) to propose the
name of Mr. Justice Filimone Jitoko as the new Speaker to preside over this august House. Mr.
Justice Jitoko was born on 3rd October, 1949, in Kabara, Lau, to his parents, Mr. Isireli Rarawa and
Mrs. Leba Wainise. He is a proud husband of Mrs. Elina Senirewa Jitoko, and they are doting parents
of two sons and two daughters.
From his humble beginnings at a District School in Kabara, and later attending Ratu
Kadavulevu School and Queen Victoria School, Mr. Justice Jitoko has become a distinguished
Pacific Jurist, a diplomat and an academic. He is an outstanding son of Fiji. A keen sportsman, he
was a member of the Fiji Secondary Schools Rugby Team in 1969. Prior to that, he captained the
Queen Victorian School First XV Rugby Team, as well as the school cricket, hockey and athletics
team.
Mr. Justice Jitoko read law at the Victoria University of Wellington from 1972 to 1976. While
there, he was a member of the cricket and rugby team of the University. He was admitted to the New
Zealand Bar and the Bar of the High Court of Fiji in 1978.
He has had a storied legal career as a State Law Officer, rising to the position of Solicitor-
General and later, stints as Resident Magistrate and Puisne Judge of the High Court of Fiji. Mr.
Justice Jitoko also served as the Legal Advisor to the Bose Levu Vakaturaga from 1988 to1994.
The apex of his professional career, most recently, is as President of the Fiji Court of Appeal and
prior to that, as Chief Justice of the Republic of Nauru.
As a Diplomat, Madam Secretary-General, he served as Fiji’s Ambassador to the Court of St.
James in the United Kingdom and also was accredited to the Holy See, Denmark and Israel. He also
served as Counsellor at our Permanent Mission to the United Nations in New York and at our
Embassy to the United States in Washington D.C.
As a senior and trustworthy civil servant, Mr. Justice Jitoko served as Secretary to Cabinet.
He also briefly joined Academia as a Senior Lecturer at the School of Islands and Oceans as well as
the School of Law in the University of the South Pacific.

On Community Service, Madam Secretary-General, another significant highlight is Mr.
Justice Jitoko’s fiduciary service to his vanua and community in giving back to the nation. Mr.
Justice Jitoko has served as Chairman of the Kabara Development Corporation from 1992 to 1996,
as well as the Ratu Mara Education Trust Board. He had also served as Governor on the Adi Cakobau
School Board of Governors and President of Fiji Cricket Association as well as the Queen Victoria
School Old Boy’s Club.
In conclusion, Madam Secretary-General, Mr. Justice Jitoko as an eminent Jurist, illustrious
Diplomat, senior Civil Servant and Academic, is well regarded and well respected. I have, no doubt,
that his sense of fairness and commitment to uphold the parliamentary democracy in this land at all
times will make him an excellent holder of this High Office. He will bring outstanding leadership,
communication and strategic thinking credentials and gravitas to the Office of the Speaker in guiding
parliamentary debate and ensuring that the people of this nation participate in their governance.
The Coalition Government has an ambitious and intricate legislative agenda and his brilliant
qualities as a Jurist will ensure that Parliament make laws that are user-friendly, coherent and
navigable. As Lord Bingham had said, the law should be, and I quote: “accessible and so far as
possible intelligible, clear and predictable.”
Mr. Justice Jitoko’s distinguished national, regional and international service as an
outstanding Jurist, illustrious Diplomat and Academic makes him eminently suitable to take the Chair
as Speaker of this august House. Therefore, Madam Secretary-General, I commend his name to the
House for election as our Speaker.
SECRETARY-GENERAL.- I now call upon the honourable Leader of the Opposition for his
nomination.
HON. I.B. SERUIRATU.- Madam Secretary-General, I move:
That Mr. Ruveni Nadalo do take the Chair of Parliament as Speaker.
HON. A.A. MAHARAJ.- Madam Secretary-General, I as a stand-alone and Independent
Member of Parliament, second the name of Mr. Ruveni Nadalo to be the Speaker of Parliament.
SECRETARY-GENERAL.- I now invite the honourable Leader of the Opposition to give a
brief account of the nominee’s credentials.
HON. I.B. SERUIRATU.- Madam Secretary-General, I rise in accordance with Standing
Order 17(4) as well and as the Leader of the Opposition, I have announced our nomination for
Speaker of Parliament, Mr. Ruveni Nadalo, who was a former Deputy Speaker of Parliament as well
during the term of Parliament from 2014 to 2018.
Honourable Members, Mr. Ruveni Nadalo is a proud son of Batiri, Malomalo, Nadroga to the
late Isikeli Nadalo. A long serving member of the National Federation Party, who inspired Mr.
Nadalo’s interest in politics at a very tender age and Mr. Nadalo was raised in one of the most
politically active families in Fiji then and grew up instilled in the values of public service and
commitment to serving the interest of the people of Fiji. His late father left behind a legacy as a
champion for multiracial representation and regional interest which profound the influence of Mr.
Nadalo in his approach to leadership. He is also a distinguished leader in the vanua, particularly in
his tikina of Malomalo in Nadroga, and has also served as the Chairman of the Nadroga/Navosa
Provincial Council from the years 2019 to 2022. He has demonstrated during his term as Deputy
Speaker of Parliament the capability to handle parliamentary affairs. He has also during his term as

SECRETARY-GENERAL.- Honourable Members, are there any further nominations?
Thank you. Honourable Members, since there is more than one nomination, and pursuant to
Standing Order 17(7), the Parliament is required to undertake a roll call vote. Pursuant to Standing
Order 17(8), the Secretary-General will ask each honourable Member separately how the honourable
Member wishes to vote. Each honourable Member upon being called will give his or her vote stating
the name of the person for whom the honourable Member wishes to vote for. Pursuant to Standing
Order 17(10), and after tallying the results, the Secretary-General will announce the results and
declares the person receiving the greatest number of votes to be elected. Please kindly take further
note that the roll call vote will be undertaken in alphabetical order of surnames.
