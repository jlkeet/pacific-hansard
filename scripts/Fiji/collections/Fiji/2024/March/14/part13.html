HON. PROF. B.C. PRASAD.- Mr. Speaker, Sir, by way of background, as per the
establishment of the Fiji Development Bank (FDB) Act in 1966, it is important for me to, once again,
highlight the function of the Bank and, that is, to facilitate and stimulate the promotion and
development of natural resources, transportation and other industries and enterprises in Fiji and in
the discharge of these functions, the Bank shall give a special consideration and priority to the
economic development of the rural and agricultural sectors of the economy of Fiji. Furthermore, Sir,
the Bank is under the supervision of the Reserve Bank of Fiji, and this requires the Bank to provide
a six-monthly report to the Minister of Finance.
Also, as required under section 2(1) of the Financial Management Act which states that
subject to any other Act that the Government may guarantee the financial liability of an entity in
respect of a loan or otherwise but only if the giving of the guarantee is approved by Parliament. So
by way of background, this is why this guarantee comes to Parliament.
This Government guarantee, Mr. Speaker, Sir, the request from the Bank is to:

I want to also state, Mr. Speaker, Sir, that the original intention of the Bank was to ensure that
the Bank actually took additional risks in providing financing to sectors which, perhaps traditionally,
will not be the ones that commercial banks and other financial institutions would like to lend.

compared to $692 million in the 2022 Financial Year. So, that is a significant change, Mr. Speaker,
Sir.

instruments - promissory notes, short term borrowings and RBF financing facility.

again this year. Given that Parliament only approves to guarantee FDB borrowings for the next 12
months period, which is a strategy, I think, also put in place to stringently monitor the risk associated
with these borrowings to minimise the risk of exposure to Government.
In light of the figures highlighted, the outstanding guaranteed borrowings, Mr. Speaker, Sir,
has been declining over the years. Every time we have been coming to Parliament, the amount has
been declining. For instance, the total approved Government Guarantee for FDB borrowings for the
past five years has accumulated to a sum of $920 million, and as of 31st December 2023, only 25
percent - $234.21 million of this accumulated approved guarantee is outstanding by which the Debt
Management Unit in the Ministry of Finance and Strategic Planning is closely monitoring to ensure
that associated fiscal risks are mitigated at all times.
Mr. Speaker, Sir, the Bank’s current average lending rate and cost of borrowing as of 31st
December, 2023 is 5.67 percent and 1.23 percent respectively. Additionally, the Bank accommodates
and delivers a variety of Government’s socio-economic and directed lending policies, therefore,
request that it continues to be assisted by the provision of guarantees. I think that is the rationale
behind the guarantees as well.
Mr. Speaker, Sir, the Bank’s outstanding liabilities guaranteed by the Government, as
mentioned earlier, as of 31st January 2024 amounts to $227.6 million, of which bond stood at $45.5
million - 21 percent; term deposits at $65.1 million - 29 percent; and RBF import substitution and
export finance provision at $117.02 million, which is 50 percent.
