COMMUNICATIONS FROM THE SPEAKER
Welcome
I welcome all honourable Members to today’s sitting and all those joining us in the gallery
and those watching the proceedings on television and the internet. Thank you for taking interest in
the proceedings of your Parliament.
Parliament – ICT Upgrade of Conferencing System
For the information of all honourable Members, there are only a few works left to complete
the overall ICT upgrade of the conferencing system in our Chambers. I have been advised that these
will be completed after this sitting and will be fully functioning at the next April sitting. These
includes the interpretation (captioning) and broadcasting of the vernacular (translation). You may
notice that you are not provided with any listening devices since of this fact that there is no final
connection. It had just arrived after being bought from a New Zealand firm and that should be ready
as I have stated in the next sitting.
Translations – Hon. I. Vasu and Hon I. Tuiwailevu
With that effect, I have also received notification from the honourable Minister for iTaukei
Affairs and Culture, Heritage and Arts and the honourable Assistant Minister for iTaukei Affairs that
they will deliver their responses in the vernacular. I can confirm that I have also received the English
translations of their responses, and these will be included in the Daily Hansard for today. Whatever
has been said in this Parliament, you will find after a few seconds being livestreamed in the television,
whether it is said in the vernacular, both iTaukei and Hindi, or is said in English. That is what you
will see on our television.

Appointment of Opposition Whip
