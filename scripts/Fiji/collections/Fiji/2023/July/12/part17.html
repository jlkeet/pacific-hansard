On Dairy, Mr. Speaker, Sir, Fiji still imports around $99 million worth of dairy products. The
annual demand for liquid milk is 24 million litres and we are producing only 29 percent of the milk
in the formal sector. Moreover, this is worsened by poor genetics, old infrastructure and poor hygiene

the rise in the production cost.

restructure of the dairy industry was done more than 12 years back and it has not been completed.

dairy sector, which will have a significant impact on the dairy industry. This is a very crucial
intervention for the industry that had suffered badly from the losses of good breeds of cattle due to
Bovine Tuberculosis and Brucellosis re-emergence in 2014.

with the supply of supplementary feed on a one-third and two-third basis.
Under the Dairy Extension Programme, activities such as Improvement to Dairy Access Farm

farmers to increase their milk production.

consultant, who will set up a qualifier’s exam for veterinarians from other Universities so that they

consultations with academic institutions like the University of the South Pacific (USP), FNU,
University of Fiji and other institutions around the region, to address needs from different agriculture
sectors.

Mr. Speaker, Sir, these impacts contribute to Fiji’s flash flooding, heavy silted waterways and

community’s land and freshwater aquifer.

during storm surges.
