COMMUNICATION FROM THE SPEAKER
Welcome
I welcome all honourable Members to today’s official opening of Parliament. I also welcome
our guests seated in the gallery, and those watching the live broadcast and the livestreaming of
today’s proceedings from the comfort of their homes, offices and electronic devices, thank you for
your continued interest.
Today is another special occasion in the parliamentary calendar as it marks the official
opening of the first session of the third term of the Fijian Parliament. The State Opening will be
officiated by His Excellency the President, Turaga na Tui Macuata, Ratu Wiliame Katonivere.
Gratitude – Former Speaker
Honourable Members, before I proceed, I wish to take this opportunity to thank and
acknowledge my predecessor, His Excellency Ratu Epeli Nailatikau for his great service in this
honourable Chair and to Parliament. I wish him well in his future endeavours.
Resolution – Business Committee
During the Business Committee meeting on Friday, 27th January, 2023, the Business
Committee resolved and agreed through vote that Standing Order 28, to some extent Standing Orders
29 and 30 be waived so as to allow the following:
1.
Enable the honourable Members of Parliament to speak in the vosa vaka-Viti, or Hindi as
apart from English, so as to allow them to express themselves fully and make meaningful
contributions on issues of national importance;
2. Allow the translations to be reflected in the Hansard Report; and
3. Allow the Speaker to pray the way he wishes to pray and will not be offended if someone
else prays the way they pray.
Honourable Members, when a Member of Parliament speaks in the vosa vaka-Viti or Hindi
languages, the translations can be from the vernacular to English. These translations can be heard by
