ADOPTION OF STANDING ORDERS
HON. RO F.Q. TUISAWAU.- Mr. Speaker, I move:
That pursuant to Section 71 of the Constitution, the Standing Orders of the Parliament
of the Republic of Fiji, published and gazetted on 1st April, 2019, be adopted by Parliament.
HON. S.R. DITOKA.- Mr. Speaker, Sir, I beg to second the motion.
HON. RO F.Q. TUISAWAU.- Mr. Speaker, Sir, this motion is purely procedural in nature.
The Standing Orders lays out the rules for the order and conduct of business and the proceedings of
Parliament.
Following the administration of oath or affirmation earlier today, it is envisaged that the
Parliamentary Standing and Select Committees will meet soon and, therefore, it is pertinent that
Parliament adopts the current Standing Orders so as to allow Parliament and its Committees to start
and continue with business accordingly.
Mr. Speaker, Section 71 of the Constitution provides, and I quote:
“Parliament may make standing orders and rules for the order and conduct of
business and proceedings in Parliament and its committees.”
Standing Order 128(2) also further provides, and I quote:
“Immediately following the first sitting of Parliament after each election, the Standing
Orders Committee shall be automatically convened to –
(a)
(b)
review the Standing Orders; and
report to Parliament within 14 sitting days with recommended amendments to the
Standing Orders if any, in which case, on the third sitting day after the tabling of the
report, the Speaker must put the question ‘That the amendments recommended by the
Standing Orders Committee … be adopted by Parliament’.”
Mr. Speaker, in order for the Standing Order 128(2) to take place, Parliament on its first
sitting after a general election (which is today) must adopt the Standing Orders in its current form. I,
therefore, seek support from all honourable Members for this motion.
