The response to the Question by honourable J.R. Vocea is as follows:
I thank the honourable Member for his question. A priority to Government is increasing home
ownership. It also encompasses emergency support to families who require grant assistance to rebuild
their uninsured homes that have been destroyed by a disaster that is unforeseen and considered a crisis.
This is considering the aftermath of the incident which can be detrimental physically and
psychologically to family members.
The Housing Assistance to Fire Victims programme was introduced in 2015 targeted towards
families who have had their homes destroyed in the cause of an accidental fire. Families that are assisted
through this programme are low and middle income earning households with a total annual income of
$50,000 and below. The grant payable to eligible households is up to
$5,000. In 2020-2021 financial year, this assistance was extended to rural and maritime areas.
