RABUKA.- Madam Secretary-General, the honourable Deputy Prime Ministers, honourable Ministers and Assistant Ministers, honourable Leader of the Opposition, honourable Members of Parliament, members of the public who may be watching us via television and livestream or listening to their radios. Ni sa bula vinaka and a very good morning to you all. Madam Secretary-General, I rise in accordance with Standing Order 17(4) to propose the name of Mr. Justice Filimone Jitoko as the new Speaker to preside over this august House. Mr. Justice Jitoko was born on 3rd October, 1949, in Kabara, Lau, to his parents, Mr. Isireli Rarawa and Mrs. Leba Wainise. He is a proud husband of Mrs. Elina Senirewa Jitoko, and they are doting parents of two sons and two daughters. From his humble beginnings at a District School in Kabara, and later attending Ratu Kadavulevu School and Queen Victoria School, Mr. Justice Jitoko has become a distinguished Pacific Jurist, a diplomat and an academic. He is an outstanding son of Fiji. A keen sportsman, he was a member of the Fiji Secondary Schools Rugby Team in 1969. Prior to that, he captained the Queen Victorian School First XV Rugby Team, as well as the school cricket, hockey and athletics team. Mr. Justice Jitoko read law at the Victoria University of Wellington from 1972 to 1976. While there, he was a member of the cricket and rugby team of the University. He was admitted to the New Zealand Bar and the Bar of the High Court of Fiji in 1978. He has had a storied legal career as a State Law Officer, rising to the position of Solicitor- General and later, stints as Resident Magistrate and Puisne Judge of the High Court of Fiji. Mr. Justice Jitoko also served as the Legal Advisor to the Bose Levu Vakaturaga from 1988 to1994. The apex of his professional career, most recently, is as President of the Fiji Court of Appeal and prior to that, as Chief Justice of the Republic of Nauru. As a Diplomat, Madam Secretary-General, he served as Fiji’s Ambassador to the Court of St. James in the United Kingdom and also was accredited to the Holy See, Denmark and Israel. He also served as Counsellor at our Permanent Mission to the United Nations in New York and at our Embassy to the United States in Washington D. C. As a senior and trustworthy civil servant, Mr. Justice Jitoko served as Secretary to Cabinet. He also briefly joined Academia as a Senior Lecturer at the School of Islands and Oceans as well as the School of Law in the University of the South Pacific.

SECRETARY-GENERAL.- Honourable Members, are there any further nominations? Thank you. Honourable Members, since there is more than one nomination, and pursuant to Standing Order 17(7), the Parliament is required to undertake a roll call vote. Pursuant to Standing Order 17(8), the Secretary-General will ask each honourable Member separately how the honourable Member wishes to vote. Each honourable Member upon being called will give his or her vote stating the name of the person for whom the honourable Member wishes to vote for. Pursuant to Standing Order 17(10), and after tallying the results, the Secretary-General will announce the results and declares the person receiving the greatest number of votes to be elected. Please kindly take further note that the roll call vote will be undertaken in alphabetical order of surnames.
