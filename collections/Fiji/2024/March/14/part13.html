PRASAD.- Mr: Speaker, Sir, by way of background, as per the establishment of the Fiji Development Bank (FDB) Act in 1966, it is important for me to, once again, highlight the function of the Bank and, that is, to facilitate and stimulate the promotion and development of natural resources, transportation and other industries and enterprises in Fiji and in the discharge of these functions, the Bank shall give a special consideration and priority to the economic development of the rural and agricultural sectors of the economy of Fiji. Furthermore, Sir, the Bank is under the supervision of the Reserve Bank of Fiji, and this requires the Bank to provide a six-monthly report to the Minister of Finance. Also, as required under section 2(1) of the Financial Management Act which states that subject to any other Act that the Government may guarantee the financial liability of an entity in respect of a loan or otherwise but only if the giving of the guarantee is approved by Parliament. So by way of background, this is why this guarantee comes to Parliament. This Government guarantee, Mr. Speaker, Sir, the request from the Bank is to: 1. 2. 3. 4. continue the facilitation of the Government’s policy by lending to essential sectors of the economy, particularly resource-based sectors including; agriculture, mining and quarrying, manufacturing, transport, communications, storage, small and micro enterprises, wholesale and retail, hotels, sustainable energy from fossils to renewable sources and professional and business services which will assist the disadvantaged community to earn a decent livelihood. lend to the resource-based sector that will assist in the development of the agriculture sector and assist the development of the Fijian economy in the long run. continue provision of finance that can support the growth of the nation’s economy and improve the standard of living for the people, as well as improving their social economic status by assisting them with their needs in making the correct investments. ensure that the Bank’s borrowings are guaranteed by the Government, therefore, reducing the perceived risks associated with such borrowings. This will make the Bank’s instruments more attractive to investors and should reduce the cost of borrowing to the Bank. I want to also state, Mr. Speaker, Sir, that the original intention of the Bank was to ensure that the Bank actually took additional risks in providing financing to sectors which, perhaps traditionally, will not be the ones that commercial banks and other financial institutions would like to lend. If I could just give you some figures, Mr. Speaker, Sir, in the last financial year, the Bank disbursed about $40 million worth of loan to assist in the MSME and agriculture sectors alone. As pointed out by the honourable Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Trade, Co-operatives and Small and Medium Enterprises and Communications, the FDB has also introduced some new innovative projects for the SME and MSME sectors, particularly for women, and he gave the statistics. Women’s loan portfolio represents about 28.6 percent of the Bank’s overall loan portfolio, which is quite impressive. As the honourable Deputy Prime Minister has said, it represents about $3 million and about 1,300 accounts. Also, just to give you some figures and put it for record that the Bank has recorded a substantial decrease in Non-Performing Loans (NPLs) by 30.2 percent; four times more, compared to the previous financial year and the NPL was $152.3 million. The Non-Performing Loan value as at June 2022 has now reduced to $116.94 million in June 2023. I think that has been the case overall. Just quickly clarifying the point raised by the honourable Leader of the Opposition in terms of the Household Income and Expenditure Survey Report, I think the media also confused us on what was in the Report. What the report said, Mr. Speaker, Sir, was 75 percent of those who were in poverty and not 75 percent of the total iTaukei population. It is the total which was below poverty line. Of those who were in poverty, 75 percent of them were from the iTaukei community. I see a lot of people saying that 75 percent of the iTaukei population are living in poverty, that is incorrect, it is 75 percent of those who were already in poverty. I agree with the honourable Leader of the Opposition and honourable Deputy Prime Ministers that SMEs, even in developed countries, the largest number of employment is created in the SMEs micro enterprises, small businesses, and they contribute significantly to the GDP. The honourable Prime Minister was saying to me earlier that FDB’s role, and I agree with him, is to also take a little bit more risk and expand its portfolio in terms of supporting SMEs, MSMEs and those in the agriculture sector. I know, Mr. Speaker, Sir. My first job was with FDB many years ago and the late former Prime Minister, Mr. Laisenia Qarase, was the Managing Director. Part of our responsibility in the section I worked in was basically to go and identify these very small micro enterprises and we were looking at loans of $1,000; $2,000; and $3,000. What we found at that time was that those very small ones were actually very good. They were able to repay their loans on time and it created a lot more economic activity in those areas. So, I agree with all the honourable Members and with the honourable Prime Minister that we need to focus on SMEs and, particularly, in areas where the Bank could take a bigger risk in lending. In conclusion, Mr. Speaker, Sir, I want to thank the FDB Board and Management. I think we have a very competent Board, and I am pretty sure that with the new strategic plan going forward, the Bank will serve its intention of promoting those focused sectors that we have identified earlier in our presentations.
