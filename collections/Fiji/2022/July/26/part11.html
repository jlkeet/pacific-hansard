MR. SPEAKER.- Honourable Members, the floor is now open for debate on the motion.
HON. V.R. GAVOKA.- Yes, Mr. Speaker. Where do you have the time to do all these? We
have the Consequential Bills and we were given six Bills yesterday to be debated and now you have this.
How are we allocating time for all these, Mr. Speaker? I am at a loss to understand how we are doing all
these things - these are laws, Mr. Speaker. Are we going to make time for this, Mr. Speaker, because the
way I see it, the time is all taken up already and we do not know how. Are there other Bills coming in
tomorrow?
Mr. Speaker, you must make some sense out of our Parliament. This is really unforgiveable - the
way they just bring Bills to be passed this week. Please, can you tell us how we are going to allocate time
to do this, Mr. Speaker, because right now, we see that the programme is congested already? I mean, this
is very perfunctory, very casual in the way they handle legislation in this country, Mr. Speaker.
MR. SPEAKER.- Honourable Members, let me make it very clear, time in this House is dictated

take the floor at this time?
