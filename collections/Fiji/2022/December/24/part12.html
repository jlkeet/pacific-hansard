ELECTION OF PRIME MINISTER
HON. V.R. GAVOKA.- I move:
That honourable Sitiveni Ligamamada Rabuka do take the position of Prime Minister
of the Republic of Fiji.
HON. PROF. B.C. PRASAD.- Mr. Speaker, Sir, I second the motion.
MR. SPEAKER.- Honourable Members, are there any further nominations?
HON. A.A. MAHARAJ.- Mr. Speaker, Sir, I move:
That candidate number 234, honourable Josaia Voreqe Bainimarama, having collected
136,829 votes in the 2022 General Elections and who is the Leader of the FijiFirst Party who
won the greatest number of seats to lead the nation as Prime Minister for the next four years.
HON. I.B. SERUIRATU.- Mr. Speaker, Sir, I beg to second the motion.
MR. SPEAKER.- Honourable Members, since there is more than one nomination, we shall
proceed with the casting of votes by secret ballot.
[Ballot papers distributed to Members by chamber attendants]
Pursuant to Standing Order 13(4), I call upon honourable Viliame Gavoka and honourable
Alvick Maharaj to come forward and act as scrutineers and observe the count at the table.
[Ballot papers collected and counted in the presence of scrutineers]
Honourable Members, the results of the vote are as follows:
1. Honourable Sitiveni Ligamamada Rabuka - 28 votes.
2. Honourable Josaia Voreqe Bainimarama - 27 votes.
Having received more than half of all the Members votes, I now declare the honourable
Sitiveni Ligamamada Rabuka as an elected Prime Minister of the Parliament of the Republic of Fiji.
(Acclamation)

MR. SPEAKER.- Honourable Members, pursuant to Standing Order 13, Parliament will now
suspend proceedings so as to allow the honourable Prime Minister and his Cabinet to proceed to State
House to take before His Excellency the President the Oath of Allegiance of Office which the
President must administer. When that is concluded, honourable Members, then Parliament will
resume to complete the remaining items on today’s Order Paper. The Secretary-General will notify
all honourable Members through the Whips the resumption time so as to conclude today’s items on
the Order Paper.
Honourable Members, we now suspend Parliament.
The Parliament adjourned at 12.39 p.m.
