ELECTION OF SPEAKER
HON. A.A. MAHARAJ.- Madam Secretary-General, I move that:
His Excellency Ratu Epeli Nailatikau do take the Chair as Speaker of Parliament.
HON. I.B. SERUIRATU.- I second the motion.
HON. A.A. MAHARAJ.- The honourable Prime Minister, the honourable Ministers,
honourable Members of Parliament, members of the public who are watching from the comfort of
their home; yadra, ram ram, salaam and good morning to you all. I have much pleasure in
introducing my nominee, Ratu Epeli Nailatikau. He was born on 5th July,1941 and is married to Adi
Koila Nailatikau with one son and one daughter. His career started in 1962 when he joined the Royal
Fiji Military Forces (RFMF). He served as the Commander of the RFMF from 1982 to 1987.
Ratu Epeli Nailatikau was the President of the Republic of Fiji from 2009 to 2015. His first
appointment was from 5th November, 2009 to 11th November, 2012. He was then re-appointed as
President of the Republic of Fiji from 12th November, 2012 to 11th November, 2015. He has had a
long and distinguished career in the Military, Diplomatic Services and Government.
From 2001 to 2006, he served as the Speaker of the House of Representatives. Ratu Epeli
Nailatikau was also the Chair of the Parliamentary Appropriation Committee and the House
Committee. He served as a Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Foreign Affairs from July 2000
to September 2001. On 5th October, 2006 he was appointed as the Ambassador in the Ministry of
Foreign Affairs. On 8th January, 2007, he was appointed as the Minister for Foreign Affairs and
External Trade, after which he served as the Minister for Indigenous Affairs, Provincial Development

SECRETARY-GENERAL.- I now call upon the honourable Professor Biman Prasad for his
nomination.
HON. PROF. B.C. PRASAD.- I move that:
Honourable Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu do take the Chair as Speaker of Parliament.
HON. RO F.Q. TUISAWAU.- I beg to second the motion.
HON. PROF. B.C. PRASAD.- Madam Secretary-General, Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu, the
Turaga Bale na Tui Cakau, is my high chief as well, as I come from Dreketi and he is the Head of
the Tovata Confederacy. Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu is no stranger to Parliament and Fiji’s political
landscape. He first entered Parliament after May 1999 General Elections, being re-elected five times
until 2018. Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu also served as a senior Cabinet Minister until 2006.
From December 2020, until the General Elections, Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu served as the
Leader of the Opposition. He is well versed with parliamentary procedures and his sense of fairness
and desire to uphold parliamentary democracy at all times is well regarded and respected. We are
sure and confident that he will bring to the high office of the Speaker the same wisdom and authority
that has become an embodiment of his chiefly role.
Before I conclude, may I also say that Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu’s late father, Ratu Glanville
Lalabalavu was also an NFP Senator. Today, I am very pleased to move Ratu Naiqama Lalabalavu’s
name as Speaker and I commend him to Parliament.
SECRETARY-GENERAL.- Honourable Members, since there is more than one nomination,
we shall proceed with the casting of votes by secret ballot, and I call on the Chamber Attendants to
distribute the ballot papers to all honourable Members.
Honourable Members, you are requested to write the name of the person whom you wish to
vote for on the ballot paper and fold it so that the name written on it cannot be seen.

(Acclamation)
MR. SPEAKER.- Honourable Members of Parliament and all those tuning in from the
comfort of their homes, as your elected Speaker, first and foremost, I thank the Almighty God for
this great honour bestowed upon me. I must sincerely thank the honourable Members of Parliament
for the trust and confidence placed on me to serve this august Parliament as your Speaker.
Honourable Members, allow me to congratulate you again individually and collectively for
your successful elections to this august Parliament. I also take this opportunity to acknowledge the
support afforded to me by my family, and like all families, they are a pillar of strength and a never-
ending source of support and motivation and I thank you most sincerely for your unwavering support
through the years.
I must also sincerely thank the Secretary-General to Parliament for her team of dedicated staff
who, under immense pressure and public scrutiny, have heeded the call of duty to ensure that all the
necessary services of Parliament are up to the professional standards demanded by such a higher
office. I dearly acknowledge their steadfast dedication to ensure the necessary services and processes
of Parliament are given due consideration without fear or favour.
Honourable Members, the Chair is not a prestigious or glamorous one, but one that is
burdened with the high level of responsibility and due diligence since it requires a constant devotion
to the roles and principles of parliamentary democracy. I commend the Fijian Elections Office and
the Electoral Commission for their successful completion of the Election process that has afforded
all Fijians the right to vote for their representative in Parliament, and I must thank all Fijians who
had rightfully and dutifully exercised this right.
Whilst we may have many new and young Members entering Parliament for the first time, I
also notice the reduction of the number of women representatives. From the largest ever
representation of 10 women MPs in 2018 to a low of six in 2022. I sincerely hope that this is an
important challenge that we, as decision makers and as a nation, can collectively strive to rectify.
But I must acknowledge the efforts of the six brave women who have successfully made it to a space
that is already dominated by men. We must also pay all due respect to the courageous women who
had put up their hands to be candidates but were unable to join their colleagues in Parliament.

Whilst we could have done better, it is still truly a remarkable demonstration of confidence
of our often-conservative society, especially when women are seen as serious decision makers and
very important national leaders. At this juncture, I would like to acknowledge the contribution of
our development partners who, despite the challenges of the pandemic-ridden world, have given their
support towards the strengthening of parliamentary democracy in Fiji. I sincerely thank the
continuous support and assistance of the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) through
the Fiji Parliament Support Project, and I extend my utmost gratitude to the Governments of

commitment to helping us build and strengthen our democratic institution. They include the Inter-

Honourable Members, to conclude, it is with great eagerness and energy that I look forward
to serving in this role as Speaker, especially to work with you to ensure that our Parliament is
effective and relevant to the needs of our people. May God bless us all and may God bless Fiji.
