protecting consumer rights and promoting fair competition amongst Fijian businesses. The period
of report under review carried great significance to the organisation and it was the year when the Fiji
Commerce Commission was officially rebranded as Fijian Competition and Consumer Commission.

period under review which included stakeholders’ engagement, ongoing organisational restructure
and greater inspection, et cetera.

still being received by FCCC on various issues such as trader non-compliance and landlord and tenant
issues.

collaborations with various stakeholders such as Citizens Constitutional Forum (CCF), Financial
Intelligence Unit (FIU), Ministry of Commerce, Trade, Tourism and Transport (MCTTT),
Department of Co-operatives and the Ministry of iTaukei Affairs, as well as preparing and
disseminating information packages regularly for all new and existing businesses on various
requirements under the Act would be beneficial.
