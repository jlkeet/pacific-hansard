And of course, that somewhat shrunk very quickly so that the question back then.

The question was whether we should continue to fund this Vote Item. We will recall that there was a presentation by the personnel in the Development Authority at the time and the message to us was that they were embarking on a new marketing strategy with the hope that in due course some revenue will come out of it. We haven’t in the last couple years, we really haven’t seen the fruits of the work of this authority.

I see that the Minister and Cabinet probably have the same faith because they have agreed to increase the budget by $40,000.
This support is given for the continuation of the marketing strategy of this Authority. But I urge the Minister to encourage the Authority to file its Report and to file its statements of accounting and hopefully the accounts are unqualified. As the PAC committee has reminded this, it is this Parliament that approves the Budget and the Ministries responsibility is to ensure that it reports to this Parliament as to how that money has been spent.
Of course, if we do not get a Report from this Authority by the next financial year it will be extremely difficult to support this allocation going forward. Those are my comments Mr Minister.

HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you Mr Speaker. I would like to thank the Leader of the Opposition who has spoken strongly and in support of this sector.
Mr Speaker, our Financial Services can play a crucial role in our economy, providing valuable financial services to international clients and generating revenues for our country. Our industry is considered well regulated in compliant with international rules and expectations which is of credit to our Government and those who take part in the industry by putting in place a comprehensive regulatory framework that adheres to global standards and best practice.
This will not only ensure that our Offshore Financial Services providers are subject to strict anti- money laundering Regulations but it also helps prevent reputational risk to our country.
The Financial Service industry is a key employer for professionals in the Cook Islands and provides our next generation a range of career options on island.
