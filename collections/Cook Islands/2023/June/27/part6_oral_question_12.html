Oral Question 12

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker. Thank you very much to the Member of Matavera. There are two questions in there. The first one is asking about how Board members appointed onto the Boards? And the second one is in relation to the Mana Uira o Araura Board (Aitutaki Power Board). In terms of the Board appointments, the Government and Investment Corporations a number of years ago amended the legislation to allow people to put their names forward for consideration as Board members on all of our SOE Boards. This has allowed the Board of the Investment Corporation to have a wider pool of good qualified people to serve as Board members on our SOE Boards. And I have to say that the performance and calibre of our Boards now across all of our SOE’s are much higher than they have been in previous years.
For the Mana Uira o Araura Board in Aitutaki, this was established a number of years ago. Previously, the power in Aitutaki was run as pretty much as most of the outer islands are run and administered by the Island Administration. But we could see that Aitutaki’s power use and the economy was changing and growing with increased use of power and much more commercial application of power than it was seen in the rest of the outer islands. But it was not till the application of the solar project to Aitutaki to the value of significant billions of dollars of assets that we have invested in providing power for Aitutaki that it was decided the best way to be able to accommodate and manage this increased asset value was to create a Board for the Aitutaki power supply. That is why this Board was established and made up of members of the community from Aitutaki because not only were they now responsible for significant millions of dollars of assets but also in Stage 2.
