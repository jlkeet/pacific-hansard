Oral Question 1

MR. V. TUPA: Thank you Mr Speaker. I stand before you to give my support to this Vote Item for the Business Trade and Investment Board and all the work that will be carried out under this Vote Item.
I was thinking of asking a question to the Minister of BTIB during the Oral Questions, however, we are now debating this Appropriation now. When I look at Output 1, it says:
Ensure that the revolving fund is sustainable and encourage and support local business.
Mr Speaker, I had a look at the programs under the appropriations for the years 2019/2020/2021/2022.
Under Program 1 for those years, it says:
Agriculture Development Fund and also under this program is Sustainable Export Fund.
Why I am looking at the past Appropriations is at the allocation for the Vanilla Fund. Therefore, the question that I was going to ask the Minister was whether anything has been done for this setup, especially the soft loan of the Agriculture fund. During the COVID pandemic era, we know that 3 percent were allocated to those who take out loans under BTIB. When we were actually hit by COVID, the Government dropped that to 1 percent. Today, I believe our people are taking out loans and this applies to the Outer Islands on the allocations of the Appropriation.
I am happy Mr Speaker that BTIB has reintroduced this program so our people can take out loans to develop and improve their businesses. I know with the influence of the Minister, he was able restore the allocations in this yearâ€™s Appropriation. I want to thank the Minister Albert Nicholas on finding solutions since the COVID outbreak to help our people by reintroducing this allocation. However, what I am looking at now is the BTIB Board; perhaps the Minister can review the value and qualifications of their Board Members in providing the required assistance for our people.
Mr Speaker, I give my support for this Vote Item for BTIB. Thank you.

MRS. T. MATAPO: Thank you Mr Speaker. Firstly, I give my support for BTIB. Secondly, I would also like to thank the BTIB trainers for the workshops held in the Outer Islands especially in Mangaia. I support the contents of their booklet because I went to their workshop and inside it, it states, The Significant achievements.

The trainers were happy at the introduction phase of the workshop and during the follow up phase afterwards. In the past, after a workshop, that is it, there are no follow-up.
These days, we see a change, which aligns to what they are saying about this Ministry; to support those that attends the workshops. We learnt the breakdowns on appropriations when managing a small business. I believe that assistance is required for this area in the Outer Islands. Therefore, I am support the allocations for this Vote Item, especially the support provided for small businesses in the Outer Islands. My request is for this to continue and for those needing assistance in the future. Therefore, I wanted to thank Mama Teroro, Papa Seta and Mama Rima for the work you did in Mangaia. Surely, carrying out these tasks by our people when opening up small businesses can be demanding because of lack of funding but resources are available on the island.
This is why I support this Vote Item. Thank you.
