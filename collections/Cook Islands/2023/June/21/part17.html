MR. V. TUPA: Thank you Mr Speaker. I stand before you to give my support to this Vote Item for the Business Trade and Investment Board and all the work that will be carried out under this Vote Item.
I was thinking of asking a question to the Minister of BTIB during the Oral Questions, however, we are now debating this Appropriation now. When I look at Output 1, it says:
Ensure that the revolving fund is sustainable and encourage and support local business.
Mr Speaker, I had a look at the programs under the appropriations for the years 2019/2020/2021/2022.
Under Program 1 for those years, it says:
Agriculture Development Fund and also under this program is Sustainable Export Fund.
Why I am looking at the past Appropriations is at the allocation for the Vanilla Fund. Therefore, the question that I was going to ask the Minister was whether anything has been done for this setup, especially the soft loan of the Agriculture fund. During the COVID pandemic era, we know that 3 percent were allocated to those who take out loans under BTIB. When we were actually hit by COVID, the Government dropped that to 1 percent. Today, I believe our people are taking out loans and this applies to the Outer Islands on the allocations of the Appropriation.
I am happy Mr Speaker that BTIB has reintroduced this program so our people can take out loans to develop and improve their businesses. I know with the influence of the Minister, he was able restore the allocations in this year’s Appropriation. I want to thank the Minister Albert Nicholas on finding solutions since the COVID outbreak to help our people by reintroducing this allocation. However, what I am looking at now is the BTIB Board; perhaps the Minister can review the value and qualifications of their Board Members in providing the required assistance for our people.
Mr Speaker, I give my support for this Vote Item for BTIB. Thank you.

HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you Mr Speaker. I note the comments and the views of the Honourable Member across the Floor and I must say that I totally concur with her views.
But may I share with this Honourable House Mr Speaker, what I truly believe is the spirit of BTIB’s role. Whether it is legislated like this, but this is what I believe, what its role is. I believe, Mr Speaker the role of BTIB is to open its doors and our doors to foreign investment and at the same time, safeguarding the interest and businesses of our own people.
And we so have challenges Mr Speaker, like the Honourable Member has shared just earlier. But may I share with us that those challenges are challenges because we have hit certain brick walls in our regulations or policies that are put in place pretty much to safeguard our own people.
But I agree Mr Speaker, we are moving into the future and we must find common ground for both the foreign investor and the local partnership. Mr Speaker, BTIB has three core outputs as outlined by the Development Investment Act 1995/96 and these core outputs, Mr Speaker are foreign investments, business development and trade. And may I touch on these outputs Mr Speaker.
Our foreign investment, Mr Speaker, we have currently 118 foreign investors in country and this is 12 percent reduction Mr Speaker, from the start of this financial year and that reduction Mr Speaker is a result of the awarding of PRs that we had last year. As you are probably aware Mr Speaker that once you are awarded a PR status, you no longer fall under foreign investment.
And so, that is part of the cause of the 12 percent reduction Mr Speaker. As we are also aware that there are only two islands in the Cook Islands that have predominantly foreign investors, which is Rarotonga and Aitutaki.
