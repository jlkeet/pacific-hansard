Oral Question 3

HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you Mr Speaker. Good afternoon to you and thank you very much to the question from the Member of Matavera. Mr Speaker, the Ministry of Education has one of the highest staffing level all over the Cook Islands. At present, we have 405 registered staff within the Ministry.
One of the biggest challenges for not only the Ministry of Education right across the board is the recruitment and staffing level of each Government Ministry. While we are worried about 27 teachers within the Ministry of Education, the Ministry of Health is looking for about 80 staff to fulfil their role.
So, in terms of recruitment, the recruitment process for the Ministry of Education is always ongoing. We have heard all the complaints and concerns of our people moving overseas to New Zealand and Australia; they are there to seek greener pastures.
We have heard the Prime Minister say that we are third behind Australia and New Zealand in terms of the Minimum Wage rate, so in comparison with every other Pacific island, while we are the third within the region in terms of Minimum Wage, we will always have this challenge of our young people migrating.
We are now in the situation where we are importing teachers from overseas. Some from Fiji, some from Samoa and some, from Tonga. The Deputy Principal for Tereora now is from Fiji. This is not to look down on our friends from overseas, but this is to send a strong message to our people that we are truly short of staff within the Ministry.
I have met with the Minister of Education for Fiji, Honourable Aseri Radrodro and when I asked him that we have a shortage of teachers here in the Cook Islands, his straight answer was, “how many do you want? Thirty or 40? What level of teachers would you want? Secondary, Primary or Early Childhood? Would you want single teachers or married teachers?” Therefore, for the information of our people, the help from our friends if we look at our regional relations, we will get help if we ask for those help.
If you look at what we are doing locally, we do have our ongoing USP extension studies where some of our teachers are currently undertaking educational courses through extension. Some are undertaking their teacher’s application course with New Zealand. However, that still does not resolve the shortage that we have in the Ministry of Education. It is best for our Ministry to work with the Form 7 or Year 13 students of our Tereora College and to recruit as many as they can from these students that are in Year 13.
