Oral Question 4

MR T. HEATHER: Thank you Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to all of us in the Name of the Lord and to all our people listening and watching our Parliament.
I thank the Honourable Minister of ICI for his honest answer to the question that has been asked. May I just explain Mr Speaker that I do not intend to tell a story, this is however a true question that I would like to ask the Minister.
It is reported in our newspaper this morning on the Report of an appropriation of $400,000 on this Vote Item. I believe that we undertook many research in finding ways of how we can improve and protect our coastal lines and today, we are still doing the same thing rather than solving this problem. There are many issues that I would like to raise in regards to this Vote Item and there are many issues that we need to address in this area of protecting our nation.
The first issue I see, the ICI engineers who are supposed to do this kind of work. The point I want to raise is, “it is who you know.” We are all aware of the disaster that happened to the southern part of Rarotonga who was struck with high seas and the washing away of seashore properties and some of the homes around this area they do not have anything to protect their homes.

I believe this can be classified as a second of the many issues that our people are facing because they do not have the money to pay these engineers to help them protect their properties. I also believe that this is the responsibility of our Government to assist our people in these areas of need.
Thirdly, timing. It seems we have to wait for this feasibility study to be made available before any work can go ahead. Mr Speaker, I can say this that there are no plans made to this area for protection. We see around the Avarua area the use of big rocks or boulders to protect our seashore; in my estimate, that is the only sensible or very cost effective means for protecting our coastlines.
