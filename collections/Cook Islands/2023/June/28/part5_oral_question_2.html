HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and your staff and Kia Orana to all Members of this House and all our people listening to our debate this afternoon.
Thank you Mr Speaker because the Prime Minister added comments to the query posed by the other side of the Floor and because the media, in the front page of the newspaper this morning, there was more information added, I think there was sufficient information made available to the queries from across the Floor.
May I also add a few comments to the query as well. Mr Speaker, to the question on the $400,000 budget proposed allocation to the National Environment Service regarding the coastal management feasibility assessment for Rarotonga and Aitutaki, may I Mr Speaker, express the need to identify the risks and concerns relating to exposure to coastal hazards and how well our current coastal protection is working. From there Mr Speaker, we will develop guidelines that will help NES evaluate costal management proposals for future environment impact assessments or EIA.
This will include minimal standards for different types of coastal protection works like design considerations, construction standards, construction materials and best practice procedures. It also includes guidance for EIAs.
Mr Speaker, this feasibility assessment will give a clear strategic direction to the investments we do in the future. Mr Speaker, it is important to note that a feasibility assessment involves numerous interconnected complex components and processes. So, Mr Speaker, this is an area that needs leadership given the threats of climate change and on-going developments into the future; that will continue to put pressure on our natural environment and coastlines.
