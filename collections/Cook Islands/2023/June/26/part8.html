MR. V. TUPA: Thank you Mr Speaker. Firstly, I would like to commend the Minister for the National Environment Services because he is instrumental in increasing the appropriation for Environment. It is not only about increasing the appropriation for Environment but also looking for some Environment officers in the Pa Enua. I believe a lot of appropriation has been allocated for the personnel’s in the Pa Enua, which adds up to $90,000.
Mr Speaker, looking at the Budget Book, an increase of $50,000 has been allocated to cater for the National Environmental Policy. In addition, related to that Mr Speaker is looking at the disposal of our waste. Government had allocated $360,000 for 2023 and 2024 for ways of disposing old vehicles and white ware refuses.
Mr Speaker, I wish to tell the House that back in 2010, I, Vaitoti Tupa, was the Head of Environment and that was the first time a request was put forward to Government, if an allocation can be appropriated for the disposal of these old vehicles and white ware trashes. I am happy that it is now over 10 years’ and Government had allocated some money to dispose of these trashes.
Mr Speaker, the Minister and Government have appropriated about $400,000 to find ways and means to protect our foreshore on Rarotonga and also Aitutaki. If this Vote Item is passed this project will happen this year. This is why I am happy to give my support for this appropriation.
I have a suggestion that I want to give to the Minister of Conservation and all Members of this House. I am not running down the business who is bringing this into our country, I am not denying them of what they are doing but if it will be possible for Government to do anything about big companies importing big trucks, cars and other vehicles; but then there are also small companies importing vehicles as well.
Mr Speaker, companies like Pickering Motors, Motor Centre, Raro Cars, they are importing vehicles and small companies are also importing vehicles into our country. I am asking Mr Speaker, if this can be controlled.
The problem here Mr Speaker is, when these cars are old and broken down, who is responsible for disposing of these cars? Will they be responsible or will it be Government? That is why I am happy that Government has allocated $360,000 for disposing of these wrecks and also whitewares.

HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, I am happy with this Vote Item especially the $360,000 that has been allocated for the removal of waste. During the past couple of years, when you ring the Cook Islands General Transport, they will not collect your whiteware but they will tell you to keep it and maybe to wait for this to happen. We know that if we leave the whiteware out in the rain, it becomes a breeding ground for mosquitos. That is why I am happy that there is an allocation to make sure that these things will be removed and taken to the appropriate places.
However, as there has been no mention in the Budget Speech of the Prime Minister, I am then not sure if this $360,000 for removal will be extended to the Pa Enua because waste is not only on Rarotonga but there is plenty in the Pa Enua.
So, Minister, if there is no allocation for removal of waste for the Pa Enua, will it be possible to also look at the Pa Enua and especially the island of Rakahanga? I humbly ask that Rakahanga be included in the definition of Pa Enua.
I am a bit unhappy because I am not sure where the Report from the Environment Services is or when their Report is due. If we look at Page 27 of the PAC Report, it says ‘report is in progress’ so I am not sure what Report. I would like to ask the Minister to please explain what year is being referred to as in progress. Those are my thoughts on this Vote Item and I am happy for the allocation that has been set aside for the disposal of waste. Thank you.
