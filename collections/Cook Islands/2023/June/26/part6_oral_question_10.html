Oral Question 10

HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you Mr Speaker. Just a few weeks ago, I was in New Zealand, I met up with Tauraki Rongo. He explained to me their situation in New Zealand and one of their biggest problem is our mother tongue or our language. They are looking at how they can find ways to improve the way our people speaks our language, especially in New Zealand.
I was happy because our leaders and Cook Islands people in New Zealand are trying to find ways as to how they can support our own people in speaking or using our own language. One solution is to involve our young people in the Maire Nui festival.
In our newspaper this morning, the New Zealand Government have allocated about $13 million for the promotion of pacific island languages to be taught in the various pacific communities. The question that has been asked by the Member of Matavera is in line with what Tauraki Rongo in New Zealand asked me.
