Oral Question 12

MR. T. VARU: Thank Mr Speaker. Firstly, greetings to the staff of this House. To the Prime Minister and Members on the Government side, to us the Opposition and our Leaders, Kia Orana.
Mr Speaker, my question is directed to the Member for RAPPA. But before I do so, if I can briefly explain on the foundation of my question.
Mr Speaker, last week Thursday, we saw that a tree fell onto the road in the Member Albert’s electorate and this also reflected what has happened in Puaikura. We can see that the trees are now pruned and trimmed. The fortunate thing about this fallen tree is that no one was hurt. Also, beginning of this year, a tree fell onto the road in front of the Cook Islands Christian Church.
Mr Speaker, I want to remind us Members in the House that just in case you and your family wants to go for a drive around the island and a tree falls on you or your vehicle, as Members of Parliament, how would you feel? The question to the Minister is, I believe that ICI is responsible for the clearance of roadways? All it is Mr Speaker is the safety of our people driving on the roads. So, Mr Speaker, if the Minister can explain to the House whether this is the ICI’s responsibility to trim the trees or is it the responsibility of the Member of Parliament from each constituencies? Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Honourable Member. I can see the Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. A. NICHOLAS: SRF – the SRF for this Financial Year has increased and I am sure that this is adequate to trim the trees on the sides of the road. But Puaikura is fortunate because the trimming came at no cost. On a serious note, Mr Speaker, just for clarification, firstly the tree that the Member was referring to fell in the Minister Mokoroa’s constituency and not mine. Some

problematic trees are the ones on the road at the Nikao Christian Church where a branch broke off and fell onto a car.
I commend Puaikura and the three Members of Parliament for taking the initiative to take care of this issue in their district from Tuoro to Toreaiva. But to be absolutely clear to the Honourable Member from Ruaau, under the current ICI Act, it is ICS’s responsibility to ensure the safety and clearance of our roads around the island, and for the safe keeping of our roads as well. That includes tree branches that impedes the right of way of the road.
Briefly, that answers the Honourable Members question. But may I also add, we do have a small emergency fund within ICI that helps support this responsibility. I must stress the point that this fund is minimal and it is only drawn upon once an issue is brought to ICI’s at short notice. But to formulate a program or a works program to clear the roadway of these impending branches or trees, that is going to require a much larger budget or some form of fund in the future Mr Speaker.
Since the issue in Nikao a couple of months ago and after contracting T&M to trim these trees in Nikao, the HOM of ICI and I have taken a closer look at this issue. I hope that answers the Members’ question.
