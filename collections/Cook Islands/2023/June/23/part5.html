Firstly, to the question regarding the application of tax to those collecting the New Zealand pension who resides in the Outer Islands. We are all subject to the same tax laws in our country but because this side of the House approved the change to the tax structure for the Outer Islands, just to remind our people that it was not Parliament that changed it, it was this side of the House because the Opposition opposed to it.

Therefore, from 1stJuly, those Pensioners’ income will be assessed under the new tax regime, which means if their income is below $60,000 per annum, they will not be taxed. That is good news for our people living in the Outer Islands who are collecting this pension. But not just this pension, if your income is less than $60,000, you will not be taxed. This is an example of a good economic policy from a very good Government which leads me to the answer to the Member for Ivirua asking about what the current departure tax is. Currently, the departure tax in every ticket is $77.68 and that applies to passengers over 12 years old.
Again, a bit of history for us, in 2011, this Government held an economic summit and amongst a number of economic initiatives, one of them those who can remember was to incorporate the departure tax into the airline ticket.
Many of us will remember in those days that there was a booth at the airport and if you were departing, you would have to pay your departure tax and get a stamp or a ticket before you depart. Despite the efforts of previous Governments in trying to incorporate the departure tax into the ticket, there was great reluctance from the likes of Air New Zealand in particular to do this. I remember very distinctly some of their comments were, “if you incorporate this departure tax into the ticket, your tourism industry will collapse.” This will led to again this Government compelling the airlines to do that by changing the departure tax law and forcing the airlines to incorporate this tax into their tickets and rather than our tourism collapsing, the opposite occurred of course and over the last ten years, we have experienced the significant tourism boom.
We did not just incorporate the departure tax into the ticket, we also incorporated an annual amount of increase or indexing of the departure tax each year and I do not recall the amount but it is a small amount of probably two percent or less. Our team will dig up on that but each year, that departure tax increases by a little bit to keep up with the pace of inflation.
So, while today, the departure tax is calculated at $77.68. From 1st July, it will be a slight increase to $79.23. Thank you Mr Speaker.
