Oral Question 8

HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Thank you Mr Speaker. This question is a result of the explanation by the Prime Minister regarding the tax for the Outer Islands.
Perhaps the explanation is for only that side of the House supporting that Bill because I have had many calls from Rakahanga; this is the other Pa Enua you do not know about or you on that side do not know about because they could not understand this Bill. To them, this Bill really did nothing for them. I can understand the Members of Parliament with large populations that support the Bill but islands like us, Mitiaro and maybe Penrhyn; I am not sure what the benefit is.
The reason why I say that Prime Minister and you can perhaps explain it in response is that there are 25 Public Servants in Rakahanga; 21 of them are on the Minimum Wage. And it is those 21 that desperately need the assistance, and it is those 21 who cannot afford a tin of corned beef. The four, maybe four, are the ones that possibly can afford the corned beef who will benefit slightly from the Pa Enua income tax.
So, that is the explanation as to why I was not supportive of the Bill because I could not see how it would benefit the people of Rakahanga. I can however understand the other islands like Aitutaki, Mangaia who have more people that receives above the Minimum Wage supporting the Bill. The question to you Prime Minister is explain to me how is it that my view of the matter is wrong.
MR SPEAKER: Honourable Prime Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you to the Leader of the Opposition for this opportunity to speak more about our tax policy.
What this policy is about Mr Speaker is more than just the immediate benefit to some of our people. This is about creating and enabling the environment that will assist our communities in the Pa Enua. This is about changing the minds, the views and attitudes of people who may be choosing to reside in the Pa Enua. This is about having a vision about what life would be like if you chose to go and work in the Pa Enua. So, while we are creating an enabling environment to lift economic opportunities in the Pa Enua, we are also looking at the immediate concerns of incomes of how people especially the minimum incomes which is why we looked at things like the Minimum Wage and though we have a very lean budget, we have managed to find some ways to accommodate the recommendations to increase our Minimum Wage by a little bit.

You will recall I mentioned in 2011, we held an Economic Summit. During that time, a number of initiatives were proposed and I mentioned the simple things like the incorporation of the Departure Tax into the tickets, but there were host of other economic reforms that Government instituted. Those reforms were to create an enabling environment so that our Private Sector could drive the economy because back in 2011, the Government did not have any money. There was no bucket of resources and there was no stabilisation fund. But we know that the solution of lifting economic prosperity was not just giving immediate money in the short term but it is to build the environment so that our Private Sector the engine of the economy can really start growing.
So, in the period from 2011 to 2018, our economy went from $350 million a year to over $500 million a year. Almost a doubling of our economy. That lead to the OECD declaring our country in 2020 to be a high income or Developed Nation status.
