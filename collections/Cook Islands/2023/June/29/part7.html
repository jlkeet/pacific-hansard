HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Thank you Mr Speaker. Good afternoon to all of us in the House. Those listening in on the radio and those watching on livestream, Kia Orana.
Before I share my views on the Vote Item for Police, I would briefly like to mention on the previous Vote Item that has just been passed. This is in appreciation to the Ministry of Internal Affairs for their assistance given to an elderly mama in Rakahanga. I would like to commend the Internal Affairs Officer on Rakahanga for assisting this mama to fill in the application to build her a small shelter and also that it is self-contained. I think it is not appropriate that I should not complain but to give credit where it is due. So, thank you on behalf of the mama in Rakahanga.
Coming back to the Vote Item for Police. This is one Ministry I believe is underpaid. When we look at the budget for this year, there is no increase for personnel. Maybe because of the situation we are in where there is not enough money. In terms of Policing, we need the Police and we need a good Police force.
What I like about the budget for Police is the $45,000 for training our police officers in the Pa Enua. Even though the crime rate in the Pa Enua is low, we however have a new Police Officer appointed. I am sure that he wants to go through a police-training program. So, I am giving my support to this Vote Item and I want the Minister to encourage the Commissioner to file in their Performance Reports and Financial Statements. I say this particularly in respect of the Police because we know that last year or the previous year, there were some activities in the Police maybe because their Financial Statements were not up to date. Maybe that is a good reason why Financial Statements should be updated so that if anything goes wrong with the finance of the Ministry, it can be picked up very early. The reason why I am saying this Minister is that in the PAC Recommendations, any Ministry that is not performing will be referred to the Speaker and you will get a smack on the hand. Thank you.

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you very much Mr Speaker. I think for the comments on this particular Vote Item, I certainly agree that there are parts of the Police force that remain underpaid and certainly something that we look forward to rectify especially as we look to recruit new police officers to look at Police as a career. Of course, the well documented issues with Police particularly in their Finance Department has highlighted some proper monitoring and financial reporting to ensure these events do not occur again. This is the importance of finance management Mr Speaker because it appears this year; the Police department have underspent their Personnel Allocation by a significant amount. The underspending leads to poor financial control but this is something we are working with the Police department and Ministry of Finance to try and overcome this because the role of Police is not just their financial management.
Police have a very significant role in maintaining law and order in our country. The one thing we all need to understand is the Independence of Her Majesty’s Constabulary. The Police department is not part of the Public Service they are a separate establishment so that their function of maintaining law and order is carried out with full independence so their hand cannot be smacked by any Members of these Committees. They report to the Minster of Police and it is the responsibility of the Police Minister to apply the appropriate measures required. Thank you Mr Speaker.
