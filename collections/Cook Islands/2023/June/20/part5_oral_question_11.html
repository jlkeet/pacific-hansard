We know at the time when Queen Elizabeth II passed away, Government announced that a holiday be given to everyone. It was later discovered that the Government had no rights to declare a public holiday for that day which caused a major problem. I can see with this request that we will face another problem if the Government attempts to make this a public holiday because we will be breaking the laws of this country.
A solution to this is, because before the Bicentennial for Rarotonga, there is also Nga-Pu-Toru. We cannot declare a public holiday for the 200 Year’s celebration for each of the Outer Islands. Not under the Public Holiday’s Act.
This is our proposal, on our Outer Islands especially the Nga-Pu-Toru islands which are holding their celebrations almost one day after the other and because on these islands, nearly everybody will be involved in the celebration. It is much easier for the public service on each of these islands to be involved in the work of the island as a workday, whether they are part of the ceremony or whether they are part of the participants of the celebration. There is justification for this because the Government is part of the celebrations on each of the islands. There is not much private sector on these islands but even if there is private sector, we know that the private sector will allow and will be part of the celebrations as well with their staff.
Especially on Atiu because their day of celebration is during the week and on Mitiaro and Mauke there day of celebration is during the weekend.
Therefore, for Rarotonga it is really difficult to declare a Public Holiday because it would mean affecting our private sector hotels and businesses where the private sector would be required to pay double time to their staff. Therefore, the suggestion here is if you are involved in this 200 Year celebration and you feel strongly about it you should have your Annual Leave sorted out to allow you to participate if you are a public servant. If you are in the private sector that is a matter between you and your employer whether you will have Time Off even if it is a Public Holiday whether you have Time Off or whether you will attend.
So, the short answer my friend, the Member of Parliament for Murienua is, no there will be no declaration of a Public Holiday but we encourage our people to utilise their Annual Leave to be able to participate in the Rarotonga celebration. Thank you.
