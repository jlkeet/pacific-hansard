Oral Question 15

HON. T. PUUKE BROWNE: Thank you Minister. I think, first, I wanted to congratulate the Ministries because as we saw yesterday, there were Papers received from the Ministries and this morning, I saw four papers from the Ministry of Agriculture. This is a very good response to the PAC Report. I am very happy about the Ministers invitation to meet with the Secretary of Agriculture and the workers but I wanted to say, if only the Ministries files their Reports as is required under the law, perhaps no questions will be asked as to what work are they doing in the Ministry.
So, when we look at yesterday to today and in reference to page 27 of the PAC Report, we will see the reporting is on the rise and less non submissions. I wanted to clarify for the benefit of the Minister on my question because I was in New Zealand last week and I know in our last Parliament Sitting about a month ago that the price of lime was $40 a kilo. Then last week, because it is winter, I think it was about $52 a kilo. I think, if you are a farmer and you know there are avenues of exporting lime to New Zealand, then you should look after your lime trees to get a good yield to export to New Zealand. This what I am seeing. But it might be Minister that the reply to my question is in our Annual Report, I will read it and take it to PAC to get some answers.
