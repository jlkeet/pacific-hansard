Oral Question 13

HON. V. R. TOKI-BROWN: Kia Orana to you Honourable Speaker, and your staff. Greetings to all the Members of this Honourable House. The Government Members and Oppositions Members and to all our people listening and watching our Parliament Sitting, Kia Orana.
I do not really understand where the Leader of the Opposition is directing her question because I am not aware of any difficulties or barriers that the Ministry of Agriculture is going through. The only issue as reported in our newspaper is in regards to the policy set by the government for importing food and goods from overseas. We are required to negotiate with this overseas market for the reduction of the cost or prices of these goods and the paperwork required to clear the importation.
With regards to the exporting of our produce overseas I see that the Ministry of Agriculture has been successful in clearing some of these obstacles and are exporting some of these goods. For example, our taro is now being exported to overseas market and the problem we face now is that the supply is not meeting the demand. Our Ministry of Agriculture is making negotiation with overseas agencies for the exportation of our pawpaw fruits to New Zealand. I truly believe that our Ministry of Agriculture is supporting our growers to provide seedlings and other agriculture needs.
Maybe I will ask the Leader of the Opposition if we can have a time together to meet with the Head of the Ministry of Agriculture where she will show you what the Ministry of Agriculture are actively doing.
