Oral Question 28

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you to the Leader. In terms of New Zealand exports declining, I can only put that to the fact that as New Zealand is primarily an agricultural based exporting country and exports primarily around meat, either beef or lamb and also dairy products are significant export commodities for New Zealand.
I am not sure whether the decline in exports has been due to the recent flooding that occurred which damage quite a large area of production and affected many of the farms in New Zealand, but I do know that recently, exporters have been encouraged by the exchange rate of the New Zealand dollar to the US dollar which means, they are now getting a better return for their exports.
So I cannot say for sure as suggested by the Leader of the Opposition whether there is a glut of exports that New Zealand would look to off-load, I have really no information on that. But I do know that the volumes that we would import represent only a very tiny fraction of what New Zealand’s exports are and whether as a Government or even as a Ministry would be able to negotiate on behalf of our businesses who are much better placed to negotiate with suppliers in New Zealand for pricing for their goods.
A question was raised to me a number of years ago regarding a similar situation but with fuel; could we get a better price from suppliers if we were able to negotiate a large bulk volume and the answer to that was no, simply because of the economies of scale.
To get the benefit of a lower fuel price with fuel being brought in by a big MRT tanker would mean that we would have to buy ten years’ worth of supply of fuel. So, that became impossible due to the volume, the storage and so forth. I think a better solution is through a question raised by the Leader of the Opposition earlier about within the process of procurement are there ways that we can reduce costs and thereby get a better price for our products when it lands here and in that regard our private sector, Mr Speaker are best place to also find ways on how we can get these cheaper prices.
The reduction in meat import prices was due to a matter raised by the owner of Prime Foods which allowed the Ministry of Agriculture then to work with New Zealand authorities to change the way that the administration of paper work is done which resulted in a significant reduction in the price of meat that we bring in from New Zealand and Australia.
One commodity that New Zealand does produce and exports globally quite well of which we are also a recipient of, is wine. Perhaps the Leader of the Opposition and myself could lead a Trade Delegation to negotiate a bulk purchase of wine, maybe not ten years’ worth but something that will drop the value down to make it more affordable. Thank you Mr Speaker.
