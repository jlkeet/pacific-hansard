Oral Question 11

HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: This is a question to the Minister of Agriculture. That is because yesterday we heard a very healthy debate on the Agriculture Vote Item. And I say healthy debate because at the end of the day, the time ran out and I know there were a few of us that missed out on making a comment on the Vote.
It was also a healthy debate because we heard a few solutions being suggested and the spirit appeared to me to be some suggestions being made to the Ministry of Agriculture to improve the spending in the Vote Item. And of course, agriculture is particularly important during this hard time. Not only for the household but also for our domestic needs to supply the hotels and the businesses.
And some of the solutions we heard from the various Members of Parliament that spoke were lack of work force, lack of assistance for seedlings, and lack of support for the Outer Islands, particularly Mangaia. And the suggestion from the Member of Parliament for Akaao was that they are starting schooling, the young people to learn how to plant.

All those really local solutions. The question that I have, is that we have heard in this parliament questions being posed as to what exactly it is that the agriculture is doing to clear the protocols for our produce to be exported to New Zealand.
Let me just give you a very short context about a month ago we read in the Cook Islands news that the Prime Minister was responsible for fast forwarding the protocols in relation to meat. And this was in response to request from the owner of Prime Foods, Mr Forsythe, who have been trying for some years to assist with reducing the cost of meat to our people.
And my question Minister is this, if the Ministry of Agriculture or the staff at Agriculture is having difficulty in organising the pathway for our export, would a viable option be to talk to your neighbour on your left? Or, what exactly it is that the Ministry is doing to assist with finding markets for our produce?
