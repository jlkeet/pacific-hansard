Oral Question 19

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker and the Member from AU. Yes, this Old Age Pension is under the New Zealand scheme. It is up to you the person collecting the pension to decide whether the pension will be taxed in New Zealand or here in the Cook Islands. I can see the majority of our people collecting this. Pensioner prefers to be taxed here because the tax imposed is less than the New Zealand tax.
So, your question is whether they qualify under the new law we passed on the tax free threshold of $60,000 per annum. I applaud you Honourable Member because this is a very good question. This question you asked never came across my mind. I will refer this thought to our lawyers at RMD to find out whether those collecting the New Pension qualifies under this tax-free threshold for the Outer Islands. Thank you very much, we will be awaiting the advice from the RMD team seeing that this new law commences on 1st July this year. I am sure if they qualify under this tax free threshold, a lot of our people will relocate to the Outer Islands to take advantage of this law. It is sad because I do not qualify under this law. Thank you Mr Speaker.
