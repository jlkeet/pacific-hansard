Oral Question 1

MR V. TUPA: Mr Speaker, I would like to convey some words of condolences to those who have passed away as per your announcement. Firstly, to the family of Mr Manuela Kitai, as he had served in this House in previous years as a Member of Parliament, I believe we started together. Therefore, to his family, please accept my condolences for the passing of Papa Kitai.
Secondly, to Papa Tangaina Patia, as alluded by you Mr Speaker, they usually congregate at the Avatiu Harbour and right now, most of them have passed away. Therefore, my words of condolences to the families of Papa Tangaina Patia. I also believe that the Member of Parliament for Tupapa and I played rugby with him for Tupapa.
Lastly, to our Mama Mavis, she had worked with my mum, Mama Ngai in Takuvaine and her partner Tuku Tunoa, I believe we were at Tereora together in 1971. He is a well-known rugby player for Takuvaine and he was a very good Mathematician; not too sure about Spelling but definitely a good Mathematician.
My question, Mr Speaker goes out to the Prime Minister. During the COVID, an illustration Mr Speaker, the government gave some grants to help some of the businesses in the country; I believe the amount was about $7 million. Mr Speaker, according to an article published in the Cook Islands News on 16 August 2023; there were funds that the businesses did not use and the government have asked for reimbursement. Apparently, according to an agreement between the government and businesses, these agreements were not completed. Therefore, I wanted to thank the government for helping these businesses during COVID. As for the unused funds, if this can be returned, maybe the government can use it to help with some of their work.
Honourable Prime Minister, my question is what is the update of the funds granted to these businesses in terms of reimbursement?
