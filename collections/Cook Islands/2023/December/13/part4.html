OBITUARIES
I have noticed some of our community members who have passed on and I would like to convey our condolences to all those affected.
Firstly, to Mama Mavis Aitupari, an Aunt for one of our Members of Parliament, we would like to express our deepest condolences to the family of this Mama and to her nephew here in Parliament. I know Mama Mavis Aitupari; she is a hardworking woman of Takuvaine, who is involved in many active groups. This news of her passing is very shocking, but I wanted to acknowledge her husband, perhaps some of you know him. I want to share a story about him.
This Papa is a former student of Mitiaro Primary School, which many teachers disliked him. In those days, when it comes to English Spelling in the mornings, one of the teachers will always find a way for this Papa to make a mistake. On this particular morning, when their English Spelling started, the teacher called this Papa to stand up and to spell the word CROCODILE. This Papa started to panic and then he started spelling the word crocodile, C-O-C-O-N-U-T. Everybody was laughing at him including the teacher knowing that he was wrong. The teacher went back to his office and when he realised the word that this Papa had spelt, he had spelt COCONUT and not CROCODILE.
This is a lesson for us all not to judge people by their character. Therefore, I would like to acknowledge him for caring his wife regardless of his attitude and character.

Secondly, our condolences to the family of Tangaina Patia from Tutakimoa, who is also from the island of Mauke, who also holds a Mataiapo Title. Therefore, our sincerest condolences to his wife and to the entire family in New Zealand and on Mauke as well. Papa Tangaina Patia is an entertainer for the people of Tupapa especially in sports such as rugby, for being the fastest player in Team Number 1; I am faster though.
Our third Obituary Notice is to one of our former Member of Parliament, Mr Manuela Kitai. We all know him, for he has been a Member of Parliament for quite some time, representing the Constituency of Vaipae-Vaipeka-Tautu. Therefore, on behalf of this Honourable House, we would like to convey our deepest condolences to this Member who have passed away and to those mentioned above.
