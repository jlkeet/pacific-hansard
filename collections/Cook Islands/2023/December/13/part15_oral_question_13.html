people from Tongareva as they wanted to travel back to take part in the opening of the newly built CICC Mission House in Omoka that was gutted by fire. The second group was the Matauia Family from Palmerston Island and the third were our children from the island of Nassau with a couple of families from Pukapuka tagging along.
So, we all know that the CICC Mission house is incomplete so the Tongareva group withdrew. The Matauia Family from Palmerston also withdrew because they are not ready to travel to Palmerston. And that leaves the Nassau families and a few from Pukapuka. We know the vessel is already on its way and arrives in Nassau on Saturday morning. They are very happy because this vessel has just been completed last year. I was told that this boat can travel fast and I have sat in one of the seats, it feels like travelling on a plane.
I am thankful to the Honourable Leader of the Opposition for the question because this gave me an opportunity to thank the government on behalf of the people of Pukapuka. As I have said, the Government of Japan had just handed over this vessel to the Samoan Government last year. I am happy my people from Pukapuka and Nassau are the first passengers to travel on this boat apart from the Samoan people. This is my short explanation. The planning has been going on for three years and is not a sudden occurrence.
And the charter by government is in excess of just over half a million dollars. But I want our people to understand that our leader who is responsible for our finances, and the Minister and I thought the people from China or India are the best economists and I want our people to know that the boat used by the people from Nassau/Pukapuka is not subsidised by government. These passengers paid for their own fares and I will tell you the cost. It is $2,000 per person for a return trip. For us to travel on this boat, we would say it is very expensive. However, the Minister of Finance and the Financial Secretary has done a good job according to the passengers onboard the ship.
The freight for their cargoes was paid at a subsidised rate. The only drawback is to apologise to our people in Nga-Pu-Toru because the barge will be going to these islands. Sorry, we do not have any rice, sugar and flour in store because everything were purchased by families of Nassau/Pukapuka. We should all be happy because these people came and bought the food from here, paid for the freight and their fares to help our people.
