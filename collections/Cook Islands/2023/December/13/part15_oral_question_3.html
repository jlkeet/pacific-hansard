Oral Question 3

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you to the Member for Matavera for his important question. During the COVID period, government granted loans to businesses to help them, there was about 151 businesses or loans that were issued out, and they totalled about $6.9 million in loans at a rate of 3 percent per annum. But if it was repaid within 12 months, it would be a 1 percent interest rate.

This is the update to everyone, as of the end of September. There are 116 loans remaining out of the 151 and the outstanding balance is $3.6 million. So, almost $3.3 million of loans has been repaid as well as small amount of interest.
So, some businesses are doing better than others and are taking longer to repay their loans. So, out of the remaining 116 loans there are 47 loans that are in arrears. And the total amount of arrears on those loans that are behind is approximately $300,000.
So, we can say then, that out of the $6.9 million that was lent out, the arrears represent less than 5 percent. And the name of this particular facility, Mr Speaker is the Business Continuity Credit Facility and it is managed by the Bank of the Cook Islands. I have to say, this is one of the better initiatives that we have put in place during COVID, Mr Speaker because it certainly, helped the number of our businesses to stay afloat when our borders were closed. And better than that, it enabled them to hit the ground running when we opened our borders for our tourism.
Thank you, Mr Speaker.
