I wish to greet our King’s Representative Sir Tom Marsters and Lady Tuaine Marsters and our retired former Queen’s Representative Sir Frederick Goodwin whom we met yesterday at our funeral service and it was wonderful to see his activeness. Greetings to our Ui Ariki of our country, Kaumaiti Nui, Ui Ariki of all islands and special greetings to the Ui ariki of Teau o Tonga, Makea, Karika, Vakatini and Numangatini and your tribes.
At this moment I would like to really Mr Speaker, acknowledge my Cabinet and our Caucus Members of the government side for the tremendous work they have done this year. This has been an exceptional year for our country and the Ministers that I have in our cabinet, our associate ministers, and our assistant ministers have all done their part to carry the load.
This was also the year that we were appointed the Chair of the Pacific Islands Forum so aside from doing our own national duties; it was my privilege and honour to be representing our Pacific as well. So, in saying that, if I look back in this year my first engagement was to re-despatch to Japan to meet with the Prime Minister of Japan to raise our concerns in the Pacific about the discharge of the nuclear waste water in Japan. And it was from there I was invited to the G7 meeting again hosted in Hiroshima to carry the Pacific Islands flag at this particular very prestigious meeting of the 7 biggest economies in the world.
From there, to the India Summit with the Pacific Island countries in the US summit in the Pacific Island countries in PNG then to the UN General Assembly where for the first time the Cook Islands sat behind their name and flag in the United Nations in addressing the assembly. Also from there the presidential summit again where I had the honour to co-chair our presidential summit with President Joe Biden at the White House.
In addition, of course from there, an invitation extended to the Cook Islands to attend a conference at the International Energy Agency the only Pacific island country to be invited and the only country that deals with minerals. This particular conference was to do with critical minerals supply and we were there with some of the biggest mining companies in the world and some of the biggest mining countries in the world.
Of course we wrapped up this year here with our Pacific Islands Forum hosting of our leaders and what a terrific outcome that was and I still have very good comments from all the people that attended our hosting of our Pacific Island Leaders forum and again, I take my hat off to our team that put their hands up to really help out in what was in some challenging circumstances.
And the year wrapped up of course with my attendance at the COP meeting in Dubai just last week where one of the outcomes from our leader’s meeting, the Pacific Resilience Fund Facility (PRFF). The PRFF was something that we put together nearly 6 years ago as Finance Ministers to help Pacific countries access climate financing much more quickly than we normally would through the Green Climate Fund. At our Pacific Forum Leaders meeting in November, Saudi Arabia flew here in their private jet and their Minister contributed $50 million towards this facility that we had
