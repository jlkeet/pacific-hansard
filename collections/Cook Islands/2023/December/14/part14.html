MR T. ELIKANA: Mr Speaker, before I move a Motion regarding Paper 125, entitled – Report by the Public Accounts Committee Annual Review 2021-2022 and Other Reviews.
If I may just say a few words on this Paper Mr Speaker, and that is to thank the hard work of the Committee Members of the Public Accounts Committee. Also, the advisor who is our Auditor, for the hard work that they put into reviewing the Reports which relates to the finalisation of the Report now before Parliament. I would like to edge all Members of Parliament to take time to read the Report. And more so, our Ministers responsible for Government entities and State-Owned entities and Crown agencies as there are very important matters that we need to take into consideration.
I will not be going into all of them but to just pick on one very important point and I hope the Head of Ministries are listening into the radio. The Committee found a disconnection between the business plan outputs presented to Parliament, which led to the appropriation of sums from the public purse to those Ministries and entities. Unfortunately, in their Reports, it was very difficult for the Committee to see in terms of how these funds were used, an area of concern that the Committee have found, to improve our accountability and our use of public funds within the public service, the Crown agencies and the State-Owned Enterprises.
I think I need to comment on behalf of the Committee. Parliament appropriate funds to Ministries, Agencies and Crown entities. Therefore, Government Agencies, State-Owned Enterprises and Crown Agencies must account the funds being used back to Parliament. The reason why I brought this up is that some of the Ministries, Crown Agencies and State-Owned Enterprises failed to provide their Reports on the performance plans and expenditure to the Committee.
That besides, Mr Speaker, in accordance with Standing Orders 156 (1), I move:
