Oral Question 8

HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and all your staff and all of us in this House and to our people listening in to our Sitting.
Mr Speaker, I rise to bring forth some information to answer the question posed to myself yesterday by the Honourable Member from Akaoa, the Honourable Robert Heather.
So, basically Mr Speaker in a nutshell, there is not a coercive policy that overarches over these three agencies or four agencies which is TAU, Vodafone, TTV and ICI. This is concerning the issue of road trenching for services. So, Mr Speaker each utility operator has its own policy and construction standards to comply with.
A service coordination committee was formed back in 2019, comprising of TAU, Vodafone, TTV and ICI. This committee meets every fortnight to discuss matters such as new developments, up- coming work, future plans and any issues or changes. I do acknowledge the comments of the Honourable Member yesterday, that there seems to be no coordination between these agencies.
And I understand this concern Mr Speaker, because at the end of the day all these excess costs only go to put pressure on the end-user or the customer. Such as trenching one trench as oppose

I hope that the information was sufficient to answer the Honourable Members question posed yesterday, thank you.
