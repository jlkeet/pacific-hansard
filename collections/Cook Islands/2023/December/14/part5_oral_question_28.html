Oral Question 28

MR T. ELIKANA: Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you to the Member for the question posed to this House. As I had mentioned yesterday in this House, there was a time span of about three years in the planning and preparation for this trip.
The question that was asked yesterday was, how did we access to this charter? That is why I took some time to come back with my reply because I had to go back in time to formulate a responsible reply in regards to the money to pay for the charter of this ship. The amount of money for the charter is what the company, Samoa Shipping Corporation; owner of the ship gave us $637,000. This is what the government paid so that we can get the ship.

In regards to what I mentioned yesterday. The Minister of Finance and the Secretary of Finance they have made it quite clear that a return of the spending must come back to government. As I told you that the fare on the ship is $1,000 per adult one way, therefore $2,000 return trip to Pukapuka back to Rarotonga. The criteria for adults specified is 12 years upwards. The children are classified from 2 years up to 12 years; the fare for children one way to Pukapuka back to Rarotonga is $250. My people did not complain they are happy with the spending of the government, they eagerly paid the fares for this trip.
