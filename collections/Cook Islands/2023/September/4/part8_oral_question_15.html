Oral Question 15

MR T. ELIKANA: Thank you Mr Speaker and also thank you to the Honourable Member for Matavera for the question raised. I think we all agree that each person is responsible for the safety of his own belongings and it is imperative on us individually to ensure the safety of our belongings and it does not matter where you go, be in the Cook Islands, Fiji, Australia, New Zealand, we all have to take that responsibility seriously.
I am sure if the Member had read the full article, the police issued a warning towards the end of that article advising visitors to secure their accommodations, even when they are sleeping. The same advice was given for our locals as well.
Of concern however, Mr Speaker, it is the way the matter was reported in the newspaper. Particularly, when it was inferred that it was a robbery and right through the newspaper it interchanges with burglaries. If I refer to the opening statement, “a Kiwi family who arrived on Rarotonga for holiday were robbed of their cash, passports and other items.” Robbery, Mr Speaker, implies some form of violence but when you read the whole story, there was no violence presented against the families or the visitors involved. I think, Mr Speaker, those who reports these issues need to do so in an accurate manner. If it is a robbery, call it a robbery; if it is a burglary, call it a burglary; if it is a theft, call it a theft.
Do not try and paint a picture just for the sake of selling the newspaper, you say it in a more serious tone. As my colleague from Matavera had stated, we rely on tourism and we need to look after our visitors and we need to show them the Cook Islands way of life and that also is a responsibility of those who write in public forums and news platforms like the newspaper, to ensure everything is accurate.
The demise that these visitors have experienced should not be used as a source for the sake of selling newspapers. Thank you Mr Speaker.
