This Bill makes amendments to sexual crimes within the Crimes Act, which are guided by the overall policy principles and it provides for greater and equal protection from sexual crimes to all individuals under the law; and ensuring that sexual criminal activity is prosecuted equally.
There are 4 key policy changes created by this Bill:
1.Non-gendered offences – our existing sexual offences are restricted by the genders of the perpetrators and victims. These amendments are made so that all sexual offences can be committed by anyone, toward anyone. This covers gaps left in our law, where for example, there is no offence to cover offending committed by a woman, on a young boy. Under this new Bill, all offending committed by anyone is treated the same, and no one is left unprotected.
2.New key definitions and concepts – the Bill uses four new key terms, which are guided by
New Zealand law, the first new term is:
a.Sexual connection –this is used to replace “sexual intercourse”, to address a broader category of sexual activity, and to include individuals of any gender.
The second new key term is:
b.Sexual violation – this is used instead of rape, and includes a broader category of
sexual activity;
The third new key term is:
c.Dependent family member;
The last new key term is:
d.Person with significant intellectual impairment.
