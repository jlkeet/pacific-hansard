HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker. I stand to advise this House that all 15 of our Government Members are in support of this Crimes (Sexual Offences) Amendment Bill 2023. In saying this, Mr Speaker, I acknowledge the individual views that the Members of our team has on parts of this Bill relating to de-criminalizing of sexual activity between members of the same sex.
Our team has debated this matter thoroughly in our Caucus and while each of us respect each Member's respective views on this subject, we are also mindful of our role as law makers in Parliament. That, as law makers, we cannot pass laws that knowingly discriminate against members of our community.
Our team has agreed that while we acknowledge the beliefs of our individual members, the Government team has chosen to vote as a block to support this Bill. The reason being Mr Speaker, is that there is much in this Bill that gives greater protection to victims of sexual crime. In fact, the bulk of this Bill Mr Speaker, is about improving the protections against sexual crimes.
This Bill has broaden its protections and definitions of sexual crimes. This Amendment brings into the 21st century laws that were passed more than 50 years ago. It removes legal language that is no longer appropriate in this day and age. It replaces the old stereo-type of crimes being committed by men on girls to recognize them now as crimes by adults whether they be men or women against children, whether they be boys or girls.
It now puts in place protection for boys that were not recognized in the old Act. Further, it removes marriage as a defense against rape. Domestic violence and domestic sexual abuse against women or men for that matter can no longer be accepted just because you are married. And speaking of rape, this Bill defines more accurately sexual violation in its broadest sense as a crime of which rape is just one part.
This Bill also allows for our Courts to prosecute anyone charged with sexual crimes against children regardless if that offence took place here or overseas. And finally, this Bill removes or decriminalizes sexual acts between consenting adults whether they are men or women.
Mr Speaker, this change in law comes from the idea that it is not the Government’s role to interfere in the private lives of individuals when activity is consensual and has not caused harm to anyone.
