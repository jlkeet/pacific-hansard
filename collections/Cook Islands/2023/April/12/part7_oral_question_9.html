Oral Question 9

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you very much to the Member from Matavera for the fitting question. The question is rightly directed to me because I am the Minister Responsible for both Customs and Police. I like both these departments to be like children to me and I am their father. The problem then is that both of them does not come to the parent to seek advice on how they will settle this new problem.
This problem at the International Airport came to my attention through a newspaper article. We had met with these two departments in regards to the importation of medicinal cannabis. Firstly, we have not amended our laws to allow for medicinal cannabis to be imported into the country. We have not changed the law yet. I would say this problem is a matter for each department to know their roles. It is like a rugby team, you have one group of forwards and one group of backline. Each one has a job to do but at the end of the day, they are all in the same team. So I am happy to say I have met with both Agencies and also the Solicitor General on this matter to make sure each department is clear on what the law is, currently and what their roles are as two Agencies of the Government.
I think it is a matter of us seeing more people coming through, bringing their prescribed medicinal cannabis into the country. This is a result of our Referendum that we held, Mr Speaker, where an overwhelming majority of people supported changes to our legislation to allow for medicinal cannabis to be used. In fact, I believe that 62 percent of the respondents supported this and this is more people than supported, or voted for the Government. But we are in the process of reviewing
