Some of us may recall before COVID-19, the Ministry of Finance had undertaken a review of our taxation, our country was on the verge of graduation, our income levels had risen considerably, so it was timely we reviewed our tax policy to see whether there is a need for adjustment. However, COVID-19 came into the country or came to the world in 2020 and that tax review was put aside and other measures were needed to change some of our tax to allow us to go through this period of reduced income and COVID-19 pressures that were on the country. One of the measures that we will recall in 2020 was an increase in the tax free threshold and also some of our Members will recall, there was a relaxation on the requirements to pay provisional tax.
One of the things we looked at Mr Speaker, was how do we reduce the burden on business in terms of the administration of VAT. So, currently, every month, there are approximately one thousand

VAT returns that are filed each month. Two thirds of these returns out of the two thousand are from the businesses with an annual turnover that is less than $300,000 a year.
So, what this Amendment Bill provides is, it allows those businesses with a turnover of $300,000 or less to be able to file quarterly returns instead of filing monthly returns. So, this has the potential Mr Speaker to reduce by 42 percent the number of VAT returns that are filed each month.
To put it another way, it has the potential to reduce by up to 5,000 the number of VAT returns filed each year. What this means for this smaller business Mr Speaker is that instead of doing their returns each month, they can do it every quarter. So this reduces the time that businesses are consumed in doing their VAT administration instead of 12 times a year, it is only four times a year. But it also reduces the amount of times spent by IRD in processing these VAT returns. This is a huge amount of processing that takes up the bulk of the RMD’s time on the VAT returns.
Now this measure Mr Speaker is a voluntary measure, so if businesses want to file monthly, they can still file monthly until such time as they want to file quarterly, then they can file each quarter. However, those businesses that do earn more than $300,000 a year, which is about one third of the registered VAT companies, they will still be required to file monthly returns. If your business does grow and exceeds the $300,000 turnover, then you will be required to file monthly returns.
It is hoped Mr Speaker by this amendment to the VAT Act in one small way, can help reduce the business burden and the administrative burden on both the businesses and also on the RMD.
I commend this Bill to the House Mr Speaker. Thank you.
