HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker. This is a very straight forward Bill that tidies up some administrative matters relating to the departure tax.
Our Members will recall when we first came in to Government in 2011, one of the first things we did was to incorporate the departure tax into the ticket of paying passengers.
Some of us remember the days when we had the departure tax booth at the airport and when you went to leave, you had to go there and pay $40 to get your departure tax receipt before you could get on the plane and previous Governments, Mr Speaker, could not find a way to get the airlines, to compel the airlines to incorporate the departure tax into their ticket like its done everywhere else in the world.
In fact, I recall very distinctly those years ago, the Senior Management of Air New Zealand were saying that if we put it in the price of the ticket, we are going to see our tourism industry decline. Well, it was those sentiments Mr Speaker that made us pass specific legislation to the Departure Tax Act to compel the airlines to include the departure tax in their tickets. And of course, as they say, our tourism industry has gone from strength to strength.
What this Bill does Mr Speaker is that it requires the payers of the departure tax, which is the airlines, it requires the airlines to file monthly departure tax returns. So currently, the airlines pay the departure tax that they collect off the passengers. However, there is no requirement for them to file a tax return.
This is similar to income tax returns that individuals are required to file, Mr Speaker. We all pay our taxes but at the end of the year, we are also required to file an income tax return. So, the requirement to file these returns will enable the RMD to reconcile the payments received to ensure that the departure tax collected is being currently accounted for by the Government.
The Bill also allows the Collector, the RMD, to determine the method in which the return and the payments are to be submitted. What this means is, it will allow a requirement for them to be able to submit electronically their payments and their returns which fits in with the way that we do business today, moving away from the filing of paper and making manual payments to the bank.
