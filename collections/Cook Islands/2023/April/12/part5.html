I have a message for everyone. On behalf of our Parliament, I would like to make a special announcement today.

I would like to express our heartfelt gratitude to all those who made the Ceremonial Opening of Parliament on Wednesday, 22nd March 2023, a grand success. It is with great pleasure that I take this opportunity to express my sincere thanks to you all for joining us on this auspicious occasion.
Firstly, to our Honourable Members of Parliament and your Spouses, for your unwavering commitment and dedication to serving the people of our Nation. Your invaluable service and leadership continue to inspire and motivate all of us.
I would also like to express my sincere gratitude to our esteemed guests who graced the occasion with their presence. Your participation and contribution have added much value and significance to the event.
To all the stakeholders, Civil Servants, Law Enforcement Agencies, former Speakers, Clerks and Members of Parliament, thank you sincerely for your continued support and commitment.
I would like to make special mention of the following organizations for their valuable service and support:
1.King’s Representative and your good staff; 2.Cook Islands Police Service; 3.Office of the Prime Minister; 4.Women Pacific, Jaewynn MacKay and Shaun Bamber; 5.Head of Ministry, Mr Anthony Turua and Staff of the Ministry of Culture; 6.PSC Commissioner, Carl Hunter and Heads of Ministries and their staff; 7.Head of Ministry for Education, Danielle Cochrane; 8.Principal, Mrs Engia Baxter and students of Avarua School; 9.Teachers and Schools on Rarotonga; 10.Uniform Organizations, Boys Brigade, Girl’s Brigade and Girl Guides; 11.Our Emcee, Emile Kairua; 12.Tina and Co for the lovely setup under the domes; 13.Apostolic Church for our beautiful catering; 14.Reverend Teava Nanai; 15.Takamoa Theological College; 16.Prison Services; 17.Ngatuaine Maui, Punanga Kaveao and all our Ushers for the day; 18.Isabelle Terekia and our three beautiful flower girls; 19.Our Maire Eis from the island of Mauke; 20.Papa Ngarima George; 21.George Ngapare; 22.CIMA and Pat Williams;
