Oral Question 6

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker. I am looking at Standing Order 240 (1) (j), “a question must not be asked seeking information readily available in official publications or ordinary works of reference.”
The question put by the Member for Murienua; he knew the answer because he answered his own question. He knew how much the machinery costs. I will wait for the rest of his comments. As we are the Ministers, therefore, we are the Fathers of these Ministries. His question is whether the Ministry of Corrective Services been audited with the work, he did.

The question was asked on the machine that was bought under the Corrective Services. When the request came through the Budget Appropriation Mr Speaker, it was a very straightforward request for machinery to help the prisoners in their work outside in the community.
We know the work that they have been doing in the past Mr Speaker is hard labour. So the Minister for Corrective and rightly so recommended and suggested to our Budget request for a machine that would be suitable to help our Corrective Services, also be useful in the wider community and as we can see this machine has been very helpful in helping our communities digging holes or graves amongst other works in our community.
Particularly for our low-income earners who cannot afford to hire expensive machinery for small jobs around their homes. So, for the cost of the price of fuel to run the machine, Mr Speaker that is all the community is required to cover in the operation of this machine.
Thank you Mr Speaker.
