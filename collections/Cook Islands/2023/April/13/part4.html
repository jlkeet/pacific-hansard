Firstly, let me answer the first question and that is to the different figures that you alluded to on the debt. I can assume that there are debt figures at different period of time and there may have been debt settled during those periods. But I can say that by the end of this Financial Year, by end of June, we estimate our debt levels to be at $216 million.
As to COVID debt, we do not expect to take out any more debts in regards to the COVID pandemic, in my own opinion, that issue has now been put to an end, we are now in the process of recovery from COVID.
Certainly, our debt levels have increased as a result of COVID. In fact, we have to change our rules in regards to debts and the Government have set a net debt to GDP ratio of 55 percent. Currently, our net debt to GDP sits at 43 percent. It was just brought to my attention in the last day or so that the impact of COVID on our economy over that period from 2020 to last year, and the contraction of our economy, we have been using the estimate of around 25 percent of our economy in contracted.
However, the International Monetary Fund, IMF, has just finished their assessment of our debt situation and our economic situation, and their result, ready at hand, indicate that our contraction to our economy was actually 41 percent. Far greater than we expected it to be, but I look at this in a positive of light because, according to them, we are the only country in the world with that high as level of economic contraction.
Any other country in the world that had an economic contradiction of 41 percent would have seen a total collapse of their economy. However, in the period leading up to COVID and under the very good skillful management of the Ministry of Finance, the Government have managed to accumulate $60 million in cash reserves, plus $57 million in an economic stabilization fund. If we did not have that, our debt position would be much greater than it is today.
As it is even with a net debt ratio of 43 percent which we are expecting to the end of this financial year, we are still in a very modest debt position especially compared to other countries in the region that have far higher debt profiles than our country.
As has been pointed out to me on a number of occasions, it is not the amount of debt that we need to be overly concerned with, it is the repayment amount of the debt that is the concern. The ability to be able to service your debt moving forward is the most critical factor.
To answer the question on the plan, most definitely this Government does have a plan and it is a continuation of the plan that we outlined at the last Budget but with much better forecasts. And over the next three years I expect to see our debt levels decrease as debt servicing takes care of debt but also as the economy increases.
