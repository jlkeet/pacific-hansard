HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker. If I may now speak to the Bill that is before the House, the Income Tax Amendment for the Pa Enua. This Amendment Bill Mr Speaker, Members will be aware, is part of the Government pledge during the last campaign to make workers on the Outer Islands receive income at a tax free threshold of $60,000.
It was an ambitious pledge Mr Speaker that we made. It was a pledge made out of what Government has been through the two years of COVID that ravaged our country to find ways how we can help all of our people.
It was a pledge we made Mr Speaker and this Government keeps its promises. It is clear to all, I think everybody, not just Members of the Outer Islands but it is very clear to everybody that there is an uneven playing field when it comes to Rarotonga and the Outer Islands.
This Bill goes some way towards levelling that playing field Mr Speaker. We think that things here on Rarotonga cost a lot of money but when you go to the Outer Islands, everything costs more than what it is here.
Fuel cost more, imported food cost more, materials cost more and the time lost in terms of convenience to be able to go down the road and pick up something at the shop cost more. If you need a part for a machine, over here you can go down the road and pick it up or you can order it and it will be on the plane the next day. In the Outer Islands, you can wait days or weeks or months for the same thing.
So, the Government came up with a plan to see how we can start the ball rolling; to do the first step. The first step was, let us lift the tax free threshold from $15,000 for everybody and up to $60,000 for those in the Outer Islands.
The immediate impact of this Bill Mr Speaker will be that over 1,500 workers in the Outer Islands will be better off. 1,500 workers Mr Speaker, depending how much they earn, will start to get a little bit more money in their pocket because the tax will not be deducted from their pay.
Our Ministry of Finance estimates that this will inject $1.2 million into our Outer Islands. This means, those people working in the Outer Islands, teachers, nurses, police and all Public Servants, they will automatically get a pay rise.
