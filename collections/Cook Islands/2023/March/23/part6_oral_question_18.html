Oral Question 18

HON. G. ANGENE: Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you to the Honourable Member for Matavera, Honourable Vaitoti Tupa for the question. The question is whether burial services is a private business of mine? When you clarify these types of questions, it relates equally to people selling chestnuts on the side of the road. Anyone can sell chestnuts on the side of the road and this is not business.
Mr Speaker, these types of questions will affect people in the heart.
I have been digging graves since my grandmother passed away in 1983. Costs of funeral services that are provided by Companies are a burden to families because of how costly they are. When I saw this, I wanted to do something about it and then offered to help our people instead.
I wanted to clarify to this Honourable House that the work I do to support these families is free of charge. I think from now, I will bring names of those who have passed away and will give them to the Deputy Speaker.
Therefore, to answer the question by the Honourable Member of Matavera whether this is a private business or not, let me answer this before the House. This is not a private business. It is just basically me lending a helping hand to our people.
