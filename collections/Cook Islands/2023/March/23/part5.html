OBITUARIES
Our first notice is to the people on the island of Atiu, in the passing of Paerangi Mataiapo Tutara and Moetaua Boaza; our deepest condolences to both families.
Here on Rarotonga, we have the late wife of Tinirau Tamarua, Mrs Ina Tinirau, she passed away in New Zealand and was brought back from New Zealand yesterday; our deepest condolences to you.
We also have Manea Turepu who have passed away yesterday morning. Again, our deepest condolences to the family. We pray that the Good Lord will comfort you all during this time.
Our deepest condolences also to the family of Ieremia Tuteru, fondly known as Mia, from the island of Manihiki. This gentleman was a co-worker of mine when we used to work for the Electric Power Supply Department, back in 1966. I joined in 1967.
Now, we will move to the island of Mauke. We would like to honour Teariki Teao, Teao Mataiapo from Ngati Paruarangi, who is also a Public Servant and a strong Cook Islands Party supporter; our condolences to his wife and his family on Mauke. We would also like to extend our condolences to the family of Rara Ioane, who have passed away last week. We pray that the Good Lord will comfort both families currently mourning for their loss.
Our last condolences for the island of Mauke is for Dyer Taringatamaka, who is Teakatauira Mataipo Tutara, from the Ngati Teakatauira of Mauke. He is also my brother-in-law.
Let us move to the island of Tongareva. To the family of a Former Member of Parliament and Minister of the Crown, the late Mr Tepure Tapaitau, his wife Mrs Miera Tapaitau, our condolences to you all. Especially, to their son, who is now a Member of Parliament for the island of Tongareva, our sincere condolences to you.

Also, our deepest sympathy for Tini Ford, a former Mayor for Tongareva, father-in-law of the current Member of Parliament for Tongareva. He had worked alongside with the current Member of Parliament for VAI-VAI-TAU, while being the Mayor for Tongareva. Please accept our deepest condolences to the family of Papa Ford.
Likewise, our condolences to the family of Matetoru Tautu, a former Government Representative for Tongareva, who have served his people on the island of Tongareva.
Furthermore, to the family of a Former Island Secretary for Tongareva, Andrew Vananga Vaeau. He was the first person appointed on this position when it was first established in the Outer Islands.
Again, our deepest condolences to the many families who have lost their loved ones; may God comfort you all.
Now, may I remind all Honourable Members, you have the Order of Business of the House in your hands, if you have any notices to relay for your constituencies such as obituaries, please hand them over before we sit so we may include it into our Sitting for that day.
Honourable Members we have come to Question Time.
