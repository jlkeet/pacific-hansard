Oral Question 21

HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you Mr Speaker. Firstly, greetings to all of us this afternoon. I stand to congratulate you Mr Speaker for being re-appointed as Speaker for this House. As this is the deliberation in this House, I therefore would like to welcome all the new Members into this Parliament.
I would also like to say Kia Orana to the Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister and Members on the Government side. Kia Orana also to the Leader of the Opposition and your Members from the Democratic Party as well as Members of the United Party.
One thing that I have picked up that the Clerk may have forgotten to mention is the history or some of the unique moments of Parliament, where one of the Members of the United Party is my brother- in-law. However, I do hope that he will correct his records.
I would also like to express my gratitude to the Leader of One Cook Islands Party.
I would like to thank the Member of Parliament for Matavera for his question. Mr Speaker, the 200 Years Bicentennial Celebration for Aitutaki was celebrated in 2021 and requests were received from Principals of Araura College and Schools around Aitutaki, including requests from Senior Students of Nga-Pu-Toru, to be able to attend and witness the Celebration in Aitutaki.
As this was a one-off celebration in Aitutaki, Cabinet met and discussed the proposed requests from the Schools of Aitutaki and Senior Classes of Nga-Pu-Toru, in which they approved for these students to attend the Bicentennial Celebration of Aitutaki.
In regards to the 200 Years Bicentennial Celebration for Nga-Pu-Toru and Rarotonga, the Cabinet have spent some time in the past few months and last year discussing about this specifically. This subject was raised in Cabinet and it was decided that when we get to those dates, Schools will be allowed to attend these events just as I had said about these special events being a one-off event.
