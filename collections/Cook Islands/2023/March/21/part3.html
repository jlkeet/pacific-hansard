OPENING PRAYER
MR CLERK (T. VAINERERE): Please be seated. Kia Orana everybody. Honourable Members and all our visitors and guests in the Public Gallery, greetings and welcome to the First Sitting of the 18th Parliament.
Before we tackle the Official Business of the Day, we have a few clarifications that I would like to share with the Members as well as the guests in the Public Gallery as well as those who are watching by livestream.
We have the new numbering model for our Parliament of the Cook Islands with this Parliament being known as the 18th Parliament of the Cook Islands. We make a point of clarifying this morning because of the many questions that we have received regarding this new numbering.
For explanation, the Legislative Assembly as we know it now, otherwise known as the Parliament was established by an Act of the New Zealand Parliament on 25th October 1957. So the Cook Islands Amendment Act of 1957, Section 32 to 50 describes the establishment and the composition of the new Legislative Assembly of the Cook Islands.
So the very first General Election by Universal Suffrage in the Cook Islands was held on October 13th, 1958 and that effectively sets up the Legislative Assembly as we know it now. So, from that first Parliament in 1958 to 2022, we have had 18 General Elections altogether. I hope that clarifies the matter for everybody.
Under our new Standing Orders which came into force on 1st July 2022, we have a number of changes to the way we do business in Parliament. I hope the Honourable Members will bear with us the staff, as we try to navigate this new Standing Orders together. We are learning and you will also learn together with us.
So there are a few things that I want to point out in terms of some unique features of the 18th Parliament.

Political entities are required to be recognized in Parliament under our Standing, Orders. So today, we have in the House the Cook Islands Party, the Democratic Party, the Cook Islands United Party, One Cook Islands and three Independents.
We have a number of distinctions amongst the Members. The first one, under our Westminster system, we have a Father of the House. In our Parliament, we have two of them but we can only have one father, so I have to choose which of the two will be the Father for this Parliament. So Ladies and Gentlemen, Honourable Members, our two Fathers by virtue of their longest service in the House happens to be the Honourable Prime Minister of the Cook Islands, Honourable Mark Brown for 13 years of service, followed by the Honourable George Angene, also with 13 years of service. So I am going to have to choose one of them to be the Father of the House and on this day, because of something called the alphabet, I choose the Honourable George Angene as the Father of the House of the 18th Parliament. Our first business for this afternoon is prescribed under Standing Order 06.
Pursuant to Standing Order 06 (a), I hereby read the notice summoning Parliament to meet. I therefore, read from the Kings Representative, the following:
“Appointment of the first meeting of the 18th Parliament of the Cook Islands.
To the Members elected to serve in the Parliament – greetings. Whereas the Parliament was dissolved and whereas I have thought fit to convene the first meeting. Therefore, pursuant to Article 29 of the Constitution of the Cook Islands, I, Tom Marsters, Kings Representative do hereby appoint Tuesday 21st March 2023 at 1.00 p.m. in the afternoon as the day on which and the hour at which the First Meeting of the 18th Parliament of the Cook Islands shall commence in the Parliament Chamber, Nikao.
You are now hereby summoned to appear on the day appointed for the dispatch of the business and to take into consideration the state and welfare of the Cook Islands and therein to do as may seem necessary.
