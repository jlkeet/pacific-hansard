ELECTION OF SPEAKER
Our Constitution Article 31.1 and the Constitution Amendment 2001 No. 24 provides for the election of the Speaker of the House.
We will now go to the election of the Speaker. Honourable Members, before I call for the nomination for the Speaker of the House, I would like to draw your attention to the Constitution Amendment No. 24, “The person who shall be elected Speaker of Parliament and no other, shall be the person nominated by the Prime Minister.”
We will now commence the election process for the Speaker. I now call on the nomination.
I recognise the Prime Minister, Honourable Mark Brown; you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Kia Orana to you Clerk and Kia Orana to our Honourable Members of this House. A special Kia Orana to the new Members of this House who have been elected by your people to represent them here. Kia Orana also, to all our people that are here in the Public Gallery.
Mr Clerk, I do move:
That Mr Tai Tura, do take the Chair of this Parliament as Speaker
MR CLERK: Thank you Honourable Prime Minister.
Honourable Members, we now have a nomination for the Office of Speaker.
Do we have a Seconder?

Honourable Members, the Honourable Tai Tura, Speaker Elect; all rise please.
MR SPEAKER ELECT (T. TURA): All be seated.
Greetings to all of you Honourable Members in the Name of our Lord this afternoon. Greetings also to all our people in the Public Gallery; I am excited to see everyone and it is my privilege to greet you all. I hope that this House will get filled whenever Parliament sits.
Honourable Members, I am required to present myself to His Excellency, Sir Tom Marsters, the King’s Representative to take and subscribe the Oath of Allegiance and to lay claim to all the privileges of this Parliament. Standing Order 08 (7).
Honourable Members, Parliament is now suspended until such time that we return from the King’s Representative.
Sitting suspended at 1.32 p.m.
Sitting resumed at 2.52 p.m.
MR SPEAKER (T. TURA): Parliament has now resumed. Honourable Members, I wish to report that I have taken and subscribed the Oath of Allegiance before the King’s Representative and that on behalf of Parliament, I have laid claim to all Privileges of Parliament and that His Excellency on behalf of the King had been pleased to confirm the same [Standing Order 09 (2) and 10 (a) and (b)].

We will now go on to the next business which is the swearing in of the Members. As the Clerk has previously read the names of Members of Parliament earlier today, the Clerk will call each name in alphabetical order. You will come up to the Table in front here when your name is being called. You will take the Oath and you will sign and then you will take your seat again. You will have in front of you the Oath in Maori and in English. You may select which one you prefer to read and you will have your hand on the Bible as you take your Oath.
I will now proceed to the Table where we will begin with the swearing in of our Members.
