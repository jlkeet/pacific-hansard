Today, Mr Speaker, I rise to provide a Ministerial Statement regarding the passing of her late Majesty Queen Elizabeth II. This is the first occasion for this Parliament to acknowledge the passing of our former Head of State. Her late Majesty, Queen Elizabeth II was our Queen and the Head of State of the Cook Islands. And we, her people of the Cook Islands, mourned her passing when she left this world in September last year.
I was fortunate enough to be able to represent our country and attend her funeral. And I shared with King Charles and the members of the Royal family that all of our people had deep admiration and respect for his Mother.
Her Majesty left behind an enormous legacy of dedicated service to her subjects, including ourselves and around the world. Her reign spanned 7 decades and saw her appoint 15 British Prime Ministers during her tenure as Queen. As world leaders came and went, she endured and served her people.
As the world changed around her, she endured and provided the strength and constancy for her people. Queen Elizabeth visited the Cook Islands in 1974 to open our international airport.
Next year, 2024, we will celebrate the 50 year anniversary of the opening of our airport and an invitation has gone out to the new King, King Charles, to personally attend as we will re-enact the momentous occasion when his Mother opened our airport 50 years ago.
And while her country may have lost a Queen, the world has lost a truly towering figure. She will always be remembered with the utmost respect and fondness.
Kia Orana and Kia Manuia.
I lay this Statement before this House.

HON. T. PUPUKE BROWN: Honourable Speaker, your staff, Honourable Ministers of the Government and supporters of the Cook Islands Party, our supporters from the Democratic Party and the United Party and One Cook Islands.
Firstly, I want to congratulate the organizers of the Ceremonial Opening of the Parliament this morning, well done. This is the first time I have attended a Ceremonial Opening and it was quite an experience.
While we were sitting in the Auditorium. I could see that a lot of our people were fanning themselves as it was hot. Maybe next time, if we have the cash, put some air condition in so we do not feel the heat.
I stand to support the Ministerial Statement that was given by the Honourable Prime Minister about the passing away of our late Queen. I do not want to tell you what I know but I want to say that we women know that there are programmes and documentaries on Queen Elizabeth and Princess Margaret and also about the Coronation of the Queen, Her marriage. All this is shown on television.
These documentaries are factual and it showed us the character of this Queen from the time her Father passed away until she assumed the role as Queen Elizabeth.
There were two things I learnt from her and maybe we in this House can look at this and be an example for us because yesterday, we took the Oath that we will do our work to the best of our ability.
Firstly, I learnt of her dedication to her role as Queen. This means that even though she was married, her people was her first priority and family came next. That is what I gathered from these documentaries.
I can also see that she was trained for this role from a very young age and when it was time to assume the role, because she became Queen as a result of the sudden passing of her Father. They did not expect her Father to pass away suddenly.
For me, I had the opportunity to actually meet with her and shake hands with her when Sir Pupuke was given his Knighthood. Even though it was a very short experience, it was an honour to have met her. In God’s leading, she lived a very long life. On that note, we from this side of the House would like to support the Ministerial Statement from the Prime Minister.
