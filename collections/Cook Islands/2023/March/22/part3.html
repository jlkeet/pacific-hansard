OPENING PRAYER
MR SPEAKER (T. TURA): Please be seated Honourable Members. Kia Orana Honourable Members this morning. I would like to take this opportunity to thank our Chaplain for his Words of encouragement this morning for our Order for today.
Kia Orana to all our people in our nation that are listening in; the Southern Group and the Northern Group, and not forgetting our people who are listening in from overseas, we greet you all in the Name of our Lord.
Kia Orana to all of us this morning. The Honourable Prime Minister, Honourable Mark Brown, Honourable Ministers, Honourable Members on both sides of the House; greetings on this beautiful morning.
Before we proceed, we will wait for the arrival of the Messenger from the King’s Chamber.
Honourable Members, if you do not have copies of our procedures for today, please let the Clerk know.
At the moment, our business for this morning is to wait for the Messenger to arrive and then we will proceed to the King’s Chamber. At about 1.30 p.m. this afternoon, Parliament will resume. Thank you Honourable Members.
I would also like to take this opportunity to congratulate, especially those of you who are newly elected to this Parliament, my heartfelt congratulations to you all, now that you are Members of this Honourable House. I look forward to some very interesting deliberations with you all in this House and I hope that everything will go well.
I would also like to welcome our former Deputy Prime Minister back to Parliament, the Honourable Teariki Heather; welcome back.
