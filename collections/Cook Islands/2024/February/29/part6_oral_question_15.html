Oral Question 15

MR R. HEATHER: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to all of us in this Honourable House from the Leader of the nation extending to the workers in this House. Mr Speaker, my question is directed to the Prime Minister the Honourable Mark Brown. If I may be bold to give a brief overview before I ask my question.
As of today in New Zealand, the New Zealand Tyre Stewardship fee will be introduced. All tyre imports into New Zealand will be taxed. Exporters out of New Zealand who regularly export to the Pacific Islands has been trying to lobby the New Zealand government for exemptions or rebates but to no avail. The problem here is we have just under 17,000 vehicles in the Cook Islands. And increase for motor cycle tyres of 21 percent to the higher end of 51 percent for a bus, truck and loader tyres. So, this will hugely impact not just government, our tourism industry, the rental operators and private businesses. So, there is two parts to my question. If government aware of this levy and secondly is there a way that government can lessen the impact or mitigating circumstances regarding the importation of tyres? Thank you, Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Prime Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you to the Honourable Member. The short answer is I am not aware of it but I am reading about it now on my computer and I believe the reason why the levy is being imposed by the government is to try and address the issue of the disposal of these tyres when they have reached the end of their life. If what the Honourable Member says is correct that there is no opportunity for rebate for tyres that are exported to the Cook Islands, it means those costs will be passed onto the buyers of those tyres here. I am looking here the assessment of the levy is based on the weight of the tyre. So, the bigger the tyre, the more expensive the levy.

Our Ministry of Finance has been in the process Mr Speaker of looking at applying some sort of levy on goods that are imported into the country as a means of building up a small pool of funds to enable these waste products to be returned back to where they came from. This includes things like cars, batteries, tyres, whiteware, computers and so forth. This has not been implemented yet because we are still working on what would be the economic implications on business and consumers. But this is I guess a law that is being passed by the New Zealand government. The first step for our government to do is to approach the New Zealand government through our formal channels to see if there is a possibility of introducing a type of rebate similar to the exemption of VAT of products that are imported into our country on exports so that we are no applying the same tax twice on the same products.
This is a matter that has been brought to my attention, I thank the Member for that and it is certainly something that now we will have to take into consideration. Thank you.
