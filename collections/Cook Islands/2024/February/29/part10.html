MR SPEAKER: Thank you Honourable Member and I see the Honourable Minister, Tingika Elikana and you have the Floor.
HON. T. ELIKANA: Mr Speaker, I stand not as Minister of the Crown Law Office but I thought it is important that I make some comments on this Vote Item. I stand in agreement with the Leader of the Opposition with the comments she made regarding this very important office. As she allured to as part of the PAC inquiries, we were advised by the Solicitor General of the difficulties that this office is facing.
Not only with its operations but also with the required human resources to deliver on their outputs. While we are discussing on a Supplementary Appropriation Bill I thought it is probably better to alert particularly our Ministers and Ministries in considering priorities for the next budget. To be really focused to what are the necessary priorities for your Ministries and to enable the diversion of necessary resources to this very important office so that they can be in a position to recruit necessary skills required for it to function properly. As we all know they are finding it difficult to recruit staff at the moment. Because of that we are all aware of the difficulties of meeting our legislative programmes because of the lack of resources.
So, my plea to the Ministers and Ministries as we look into our next budget to please look carefully so that we do not tie down resources unnecessarily so that can be freed up and so it can be diverted to this very important office, also like the Audit and Parliamentary Services. With those comments Mr Speaker, I support the Vote Item.
