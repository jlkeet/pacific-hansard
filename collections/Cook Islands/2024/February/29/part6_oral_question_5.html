Today, the graduates have received certificate in 15 distinct vocational programs, which range from diploma in teaching, sports and hospitality, electrical engineering, building construction, plumbing, business and management courses. Our heartfelt congratulations to all 140 graduates.
Mr Speaker, the pass rate of 18 percent that was mentioned by the Member, I have received more clarification from the Ministry, yesterday evening. This 18 percent is the aggregate total of New Zealand and all of its realm countries which includes us in the Cook Islands, Niue and Tokelau.
Our statistic for 2023 from the Ministry indicated that in level 3 we have a 76 percent and in level 2 we have a 75 percent and in level 1 we have a 62 percent pass. If you remember I mentioned yesterday that the Prime Minister of New Zealand set his pass rate at 80 percent. We would like to set our pass rate to achieve 80 percent as well. Mr Speaker, having addressed these misconceptions I would like to provide an overview of the strategies and considerations being undertaken by the Ministry of Education. Considering the long term strategies, the Ministry has embarked on developing a new Education Master Plan which is supported by ADB. This Master Plan aims to reset the education system placing a strong emphasis on the first seven years encompassing Early Child Education and the Early Primary years. Extensive research underscores the critical importance of this formative years in a child’s educational outcomes and brain development. This will lead to improved educational performance in the longer term as these students enter secondary education and NCEA.
