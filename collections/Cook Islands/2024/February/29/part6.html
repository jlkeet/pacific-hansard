Before I open the Floor to Honourable Members, there is one outstanding question from our last meeting on 19 February. The question is from the Honourable Leader of the Opposition to Minister Tingika Elikana relating to foreign workers in our country.
I can see that there are two questions and the first question is, what are we doing to protect the main employer of foreign workers?
The second question is, what authority does the Immigration Officer have to make an employer and employee amend their contractual relationship?
I can see the Honourable Minister Tingika Elikana and I believe that you can execute both questions within one answer.
HON. T. ELIKANA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. In response to the first question, the advice is that foreign employees come into this country either under sponsorship or direct sponsorship by their employer. As a result of that their primary obligation is to their sponsor or their primary employer.
And the relationship between the employer and the foreign employee is govern by agreements or contract of employment between those two parties. I think in respect of that part it is very clear that the employee is obliged to the sponsor or primary employer.
And since that relationship is govern by arrangements or contract of employment then it is up to both parties to exercise their rights under those agreements or contract of employment.
But I take from the Leader of the Opposition’s question raised in this House is the concern as raised by the letter writer to the Cook Islands newspaper is where the foreign employees are employed by a secondary employer. And what protections are afforded to the sponsor or primary employer.
The short answer to that is, it is up to the sponsor or the primary employer to see whether he or she will allow the foreign worker to work for another person or a secondary employer. And where that should occur, I implore on the sponsor or primary employer to relook at the agreements or contract of employment.
