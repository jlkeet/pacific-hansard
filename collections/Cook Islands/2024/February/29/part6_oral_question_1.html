Oral Question 1

HON. T. ELIKANA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. In response to the first question, the advice is that foreign employees come into this country either under sponsorship or direct sponsorship by their employer. As a result of that their primary obligation is to their sponsor or their primary employer.
And the relationship between the employer and the foreign employee is govern by agreements or contract of employment between those two parties. I think in respect of that part it is very clear that the employee is obliged to the sponsor or primary employer.
And since that relationship is govern by arrangements or contract of employment then it is up to both parties to exercise their rights under those agreements or contract of employment.
But I take from the Leader of the Opposition’s question raised in this House is the concern as raised by the letter writer to the Cook Islands newspaper is where the foreign employees are employed by a secondary employer. And what protections are afforded to the sponsor or primary employer.
The short answer to that is, it is up to the sponsor or the primary employer to see whether he or she will allow the foreign worker to work for another person or a secondary employer. And where that should occur, I implore on the sponsor or primary employer to relook at the agreements or contract of employment.

So, that new relationship is govern by some form of agreement or a new or variations of the original contract of employment.
The policy introduced by the Immigration Department because of the shortage of employee of labour is to encourage employee to take up secondary employment but subject to the consent of their sponsor or primary employer. Because it is important for the sponsor or the primary employer to protect themselves should anything happen during the secondary employment relationship. Where there are injuries sustain as a result of the secondary employment relationship then the new agreement or varied contract of employment should address those issues.
In relations to the second question, whether it is the duty of the Immigration officers to advise employers and employees what terms to include in their contract of employment. The short answer to that is, it is not the place for Immigration officers to advise employment relationship.
And I invite the Leader of the Opposition to contact the Principal Immigration Officer as I take this as a very serious matter. That is the advice given from any Ministry should be in accordance with the legal framework. As it involves staff of the Immigration Department, I invite the Leader of the Opposition to talk to the Principal Immigration Officer. So, that we don’t reveal the identity of the officer involve which she has dealt with, I think that should be a private matter between her and the staff.
I hope that answers the question.
