Oral Question 19

HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and your good staff this afternoon. To the Leader of the Opposition and your Members on your side, Kia Orana and good afternoon. To the Prime Minister, Cabinet Ministers and colleagues, Kia Orana and more so to our Ariki’s from Mitiaro, thank you for your presence.
Mr Speaker, the question from the Member from Matavera is crucial and a critical one for us as a nation. If many of us has seen the new Prime Minister of New Zealand talk about the failure rate in New Zealand, although they were sitting at 55 percent, the Prime Minister has tasked and instructed that he would like the NCEA result of New Zealand raised to 80 percent. And over and above that, he had made a directive that all mobile phones in schools be removed.
But when we come to ourselves, we know that our curriculum is aligned to New Zealand’s standards and criteria. We have moved from New Zealand School Certificate or University Entrance to NCEA. I am also critical about this process because it seems that we are educating our children so that they can be qualified, graduated and good enough for New Zealand government or New Zealand and Australia to employ.
If we go to Fiji, Samoa and Tonga, they have their own qualification criteria set by themselves. This is probably one of the main reason why our people are migrating because we are being highly educated to be accepted by both New Zealand and Australia or all over the world.
Yesterday in Cabinet, we were also discussing this issue about the 18 percent pass. It is a concerning result for us in Cabinet and if we look at what is happening inside our Education system now; we can see there is a lot of sports played within our schools. But we are very supportive of sports. I made a passing remark to the Prime Minister that maybe it is time that we start encouraging sports codes to take their practice and training back in the village. This will revive our sports in the clubs, and this will enable the codes to go and visit clubs and train our children in the clubs. This will also allow quality time and more time for our students to learn in classrooms. In addition, our answers will be just to remain with our traditional sports, such as athletics, rugby and netball.
Mr Speaker, the issue concerning the pass rate to my understanding is an aggregated overall amount resulting from the whole of the countries that is working under the NCEA system. It is notable that not only New Zealand is suffering the failure rate, but it is a global result. Now, considering the long term strategies, you would have noted that we have brought onboard Mr Owen Lewis. We are well aware the history of Owen’s dad, Papa David Lewis who have passed away.
