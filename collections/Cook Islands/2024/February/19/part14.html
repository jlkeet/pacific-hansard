Mr Speaker, I now move on to discussing the Seabed Minerals Amendment Bill. As mentioned by our previous speakers this is a short Bill. There are no major policy changes. Rather these amendments are intended to improve our overall Seabed Minerals regime. Since the end of our exploration licensing process and two years into exploration, the Seabed Minerals Authority has been reviewing our laws and processes to ensure they are fit for purpose. They have identified the areas for improvement which we are now addressing in this Bill and I will speak briefly to that.
Firstly, our standards and guidelines. These support the implementation of our Act and Regulations and they were previously set out in our Exploration Regulations. These standards and guidelines has now been elevated to the Seabed Minerals Act and the process for developing them has been further refined and now also includes a requirement for Cabinet approval.
Secondly, the use of the terms mining and mineral harvesting. Mining is a broad term which ranges from extracting through to digging and cutting. The clear feedback from our stakeholders and

communities is that they prefer to use a specific term when we are referring to collecting nodules from the floor. So, we have introduced the term harvesting to reflect the actual activity that takes place.
Fourthly, in terms of due diligence and cost recovery. The Authority undertakes due diligence checks whenever companies propose to make management or governance changes. These due diligence checks are costly and currently the Authority does not charge fees for this service. This Amendment will make clearer that the Authority can prescribe cost recovery fees. Next in terms of information management. We have made some minor amendments to sections 18 and 18A to make the intentions of these sections clearer. Other changes in this Bill include amending the Annual Reporting period to match the date from which the Licences are granted as opposed to following a calendar year. We have also provided a process for a company that chooses to change its name.
