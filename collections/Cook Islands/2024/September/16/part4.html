I call the Honourable Tukaka Ama to present the, ‘Keep our Water Free’ Petition.
MR T. AMA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Pursuant to Standing Order 236 (3), I hereby present to Parliament the ‘Keep our Water Free’ Petition and with your indulgence Mr Speaker, to make a brief statement.
MR SPEAKER: You may continue Honourable Member.
MR T. AMA: Mr Speaker, Honourable Members of this House, and our guests in the Gallery, Kia Orana in the Name of the Lord. For my opening address to the House this afternoon is to bring the Word of God before us in saying that everything on earth belongs to the Lord. To further support the Scripture, there is a song known by our people as “when I was hungry you fed me, and when I was thirsty you gave me water to drink.” I do not want to sing as it might take us a while.
What is the most important thing in life? The answer is water from God. As per the To Tatou Vai Act established in 2021, the Water Authority is required to be financially independent and use a user pay funding model. In my opinion, this group should initiate a dialogue with the Water Authority to reach an agreement. There are alternate ways to obtain water and financial support, and we must work together to ensure that taxes are imposed fairly on all individuals based on their income. This will ensure that everyone has access to water and we can work towards the betterment of our people.
A total of 3,407 people have signed the 'Keep our Water Free Petition', which I have the honour of presenting today on behalf of my constituents. This petition highlights their concerns over water and deserves our attention and action. As elected representatives, it is our responsibility to listen to our people and take their concerns seriously.
To conclude, I strongly advocate for the continuation of water collection, treatment, and distribution without imposing any ongoing charges or tariffs on users. I firmly believe that our Maori culture, practices, and way of life will suffer if we start paying for water. I pray that God blesses our nation and all of us.
Thank you very much.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. Pursuant to Standing Order 236(4), I now order the Petition to be laid on the Table without debate.
I call the Clerk to read the Petition.
CLERK (T. VAINERERE):In the matter of ‘Keep our Water Free Petition’, the To Tatou Vai Act 2021 sets that the Water Authority be financially independent, and mandates a user-pays funding model. The Petition respectfully requests that the collection, treatment and reticulation of water continue without tariffs and ongoing charges to users.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Clerk.

Of course, Members of the House, I know some of them have an interest in netball, some of them has been playing netball when they were trying to play rugby. That’s beside the point, they will also be interested in attending this very important tournament.
In fact, I think Mr Speaker and I stand to be corrected the last time particular tournament was hosted in our country might have been back in 2009. So, 14 years ago since that was last hosted here in our country.
