Oral Question 3

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Thank you to the Honourable Member from Matavera for the question in relation to the suggestion on the high number of voters in this constituent. Maybe it is about time for us to split this area so that they can have two members. But if you look Mr Speaker, should we increase the number of Members of Parliament, then rightly so we should be looking at a cut in some areas. I am looking at constituencies with a low number of voters then we should cancel that constituency out. So, if we cut down the Mangaia and Rakahanga seats then there is less representative from their party in the House.

This is a wonderful idea to me Mr Speaker, maybe if we can then combine Matavera and Tupapa then we can justify in having two members. This is not a new concept Mr Speaker. Maybe it is about time we carefully look at the number of voters in the constituencies then maybe it is about time to make adjustments to suit our situation today.
In 1965 when the constituencies were established in the Cook Islands, there were a lot of people in our country. The allocations were appropriated based on the number of voters such as in Aitutaki at the time. However, nowadays we can see a lot of our population from the Pa Enua have relocated onto Rarotonga. The number of people in the Pa Enua has decreased. So, I think it is the appropriate time to look at these seats whether to reduce them and increase others where there are a lot of voters. Not only Tupapa with 1049 voters but Nikao/Panama had 1220 voters.
I know this is a problem area with some Members when we talk about Social Responsibility Fund which we usually refer to as the SRF. The SRF amount for the constituencies is $20,000 per annum. Regardless whether there are over 1000 people or only 80 people in your constituency, the entitlement is still $20,000. Maybe it is about time we need to look at an Electoral Reform on these matters. So, thank you to the Member from Matavera for this question although you did not mention anything on your constituency.
