Oral Question 5

HON. T. PUPUKE-BROWNE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and your staff today. Greetings to the Prime Minister and the Members on that side of the House. Also, greetings to us on this side. To all those who are listening to the radio and those watching livestream greeting to you all.
Before I ask my question Mr Speaker, I would like to thank the Prime Minister for his Motion yesterday for an adjournment so that we who were affiliated with netball in the past were able to go to the TSA for the Opening Ceremony of the Under 21 World Netball Qualifying Tournament. Because to qualify and Fiji had already qualified, so the other four teams Tonga, Samoa, PNG and the Cook Islands, we need to get the best three out of the four. Should the Cook Islands get into the top three then we get to play in the World Youth Tournament in Gibraltar next year. I am very proud because even though we are a small country but we are always putting up our hands to host events such as this.
It is also beneficial for us that these countries are visiting to be part of this qualifier. Because our tourism business is blooming again, then apart from one team staying in a motel the rest are staying in hostels and the CIFA complex in Matavera.
I would also like to thank the Member of Parliament for Manihiki because she represented government at the Opening Ceremony. It is appropriate because she had been involved in netball activities in the past. The only downfall Mr Prime Minister is that maybe the other Members did

The Speaker is signaling to me to ask my question and before I do so, I have a request to ask us to come and give your support. Our girls won their game against PNG by about 20 points difference. We will be playing against Tonga today and your support is needed. There are three players from Takuvaine, two from Titikaveka/Ngatangiia so this gives you an incentive to come and support.
This is the question I wanted to ask the Prime Minister. However, I truly believe that the question should be directed to the Minister of Sports but I know he is not here.
The Australian Government provides funding to the Oceania Netball. For some reason however, that funding only goes to Samoa, Tonga and Papua New Guinea. Apparently, the claim is that we get funding from New Zealand; our netball gets funding from New Zealand. Of course, that is not correct.
So, this is my question, whether there will be funding available through our government to communicate to the Australian High Commission as to why we are not included in that funding, and yet our netball does not receive funding from New Zealand.
