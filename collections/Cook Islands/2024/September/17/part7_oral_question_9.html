Oral Question 9

MR T. HEATHER: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I am pretty sure that I will take all of the remaining time for Question Time because the time were used by the many questions before. My question Mr Speaker is to the Prime Minister or the Minister Responsible for Health.
My inquiry pertains to the individuals who return to the Cook Islands after being referred overseas for medical treatment or those who have been discharged from hospitals and require long-term care. It has been brought to our attention that many of our elderly and sick people returning from New Zealand are not being admitted to hospitals upon their return. Instead, they are sent directly to their homes, where their families are expected to provide care for them, even though they may not have received proper training on how to do so.
There is a 97-year-old grandmother in our constituency who comes from the family of Papa Arapati Henry, and they are highly respected in the community. She has a son who was referred to New Zealand and returned with health issues, and both individuals require medical attention. We are grateful to their family for taking up the responsibility to provide care for this elderly mother and son, despite their own health conditions.
It is evident that in the past, our hospitals have taken care of our sick and elderly people. However, over the past decade, we have observed a decline in the healthcare services provided by our health systems. Therefore, I would like to request, Honourable Prime Minister, that you pay particular attention to the needs of our community members who are struggling with such health conditions. While you may not have personally experienced these issues, there are some households in our community that are currently facing them.
As we think about the 97-year-old sick grandmother who has connections to Papa Arapati Henry, I must pose the question - where is the Cook Islands Party in this matter and what support are they providing to ensure her proper care?

And for most of us for our history of our country that care is provided by the family and it continues to this day because we do not have a facility that looks after our aging people for geriatric care.
