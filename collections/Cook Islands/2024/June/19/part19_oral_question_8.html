Oral Question 8

HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you once again, Mr Speaker and may I cross the Floor to the Leader of the Opposition, concur with you that your remarks are exactly what my thoughts are. My only issue, Mr Speaker is the timing of those views and those remarks because these are views and opinions that I feel would have been best served during the PAC process because I am pretty sure the Chair of the PAC Committee will concur with me that as a PAC Member you have the right and the ability and the power to summon certain individuals from within this Ministry to face the PAC Committee.
It is at that point, Mr Speaker that we can fix this sort of nonsense. Because, Mr Speaker once PAC passes it and endorses the Budget and hands the report to Parliament, it is what they say, a missed opportunity because when it gets to Parliament and we start debating it, it cannot be changed, Mr Speaker. So, like I said, Mr Speaker being a PAC Member is very important. But when one decides to remove oneself from a PAC Committee, from one’s duty as a PAC Committee, what can I say, I am pretty sure we can all make up our minds.
In commenting again, Mr Speaker I do concur with the Leader of the Opposition’s views and why I do because we owe it to the tax payer because we need to report truthfully as required under the law of the land. On that note, thank you for those comments from the Opposition from across the Floor and may I close with some advice, please get back on the PAC Committee. Thank you.
