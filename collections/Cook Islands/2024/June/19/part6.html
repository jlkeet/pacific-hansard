ORAL QUESTIONS
I believe there are two questions from yesterday that needs to be answered. The first question was from the Honourable Leader of the Opposition, Tina Browne to the Honourable Minister Vaine Mokoroa regarding the complaints that have been lodged with the Ombudsman regarding the status of the Police.
I see the Honourable Minister Vaine Mokoroa. The second one will go the Honourable Deputy Prime Minister.
HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and all your staff this morning. Salutations have already been conveyed to us this morning.
I stand to provide a response to the question raised by the Leader of the Opposition yesterday. The question pertained to the complaints that were submitted to the Office of the Ombudsman. I have since received a response from the Ombudswoman.
According to the report, the Office of the Ombudsman received eight complaints from the Police Office between 2017 to 2024. Out of the eight complaints, only two have been resolved, while the remaining six are awaiting a report from the Police Office detailing the actions taken. The delay in the resolution of these complaints is attributed to the extended time taken by the Police to furnish the results of investigations to the Office of the Ombudsman.
The lack of response from the Police to requests for information from the Ombudsman is a significant concern. This issue, unfortunately, is not limited to the Police and is also prevalent in other Ministries within the Government. Although we cannot provide specific details regarding the nature of the complaints, we are willing to address any complaints that have been submitted to us through the Ombudsman's office. I hope my responses have been satisfactory. Thank you.

The officials of the agency in question have the authority to release information to you upon request, and this applies to any agency or HOM.

With that said, I would like to direct my question to the Minister for CIIC. As we are aware, some contract workers, particularly carpenters, are departing due to the lack of available work. HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you to the Honourable Member from Murienua for your question. You are correct that some companies are experiencing a lack of work and, as a result, have laid off employees. Regarding the question of why CIIC has undertaken these projects, it is due to financial considerations, Mr Speaker. During project planning, costings were taken into account. However, after discussing the allocated funding with contractors, it became apparent that the funding was insufficient to complete the project.
To summarise, Mr Speaker, I would like to share a recent example. We had a budget of $60,000 for tarsealing work on the Ports Authority parking area, and we sent out an RFQ. Unfortunately, the lowest quote we received was $200,000. As a result, we are unable to pay the contractor as we only had $60,000 allocated for the project. In short, Mr Speaker, it comes down to costs and what we can or cannot afford. Thank you.
