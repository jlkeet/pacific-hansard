HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I am just looking at our time, mindful that I know when the Minister will get up to speak, he will have one strong message to give and I don’t want it to be interrupted by the ending of our meeting so I will spend the next few minutes talking about the Corrective Services.
The one thing I can say in this Appropriation, Mr Speaker that it’s not reflected in the Budget is the way that our Corrective Service has been run and we’ve heard from the Minister, when he took on this role as the Minister, the roll in the Prison Service of inmates was over sixty and today that number is down below thirty.
In addition to that, Mr Speaker during his time as the Minister, we have not had one inmate escape from prison. What it doesn’t show in the Budget is what the Minister does. He teaches these inmates, he works with them, he’s with them in the morning for their Prayer, he’s with them in the evening for their evening Prayer and he rehabilitates them.
As he has told us before many times, he knows the ins and the outs of the Prison system. His own personal experience allows him to talk to our inmates and to provide them a guiding path way to a better way so they don’t come back into prison. I recognise the attire that he is wearing the uniform of the Commissioner of Prison. All he requires now is a hat. I wanted to praise and thank the Minister for the way that he is running the prison and I support the allocation for the Corrective Service.
