HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Kia Orana Mr Speaker and Kia Orana to everyone present. I would like to express my opinion on the allocation of funds for this Ministry, which I believe has been neglected for many years. As members of this Parliament, we often discuss the importance of diversifying our economy, and while we recognise that tourism is a valuable asset, we must also consider the need to support agriculture in the event of another crisis such as the COVID-19 pandemic, which highlighted the vulnerability of our economy due to overreliance on tourism.
I acknowledge that the Budget needs to be distributed fairly across all Ministries. However, upon reviewing Book 1 specifically pertaining to Agriculture, I am disheartened by the meager allocation given to this Ministry. Considering that we regard agriculture as a critical sector and a fallback option, it is disconcerting to note that only three-quarters of a page is dedicated to it.
I would like to direct my message to the Secretary of Agriculture, who is present here today. I understand that it may be challenging, but with the current allocation, I urge you to explore ways to increase local food production before we focus on exporting. My concern stems from the significant contrast between export and import figures stated in the Budget book, where the

HON. V. R. TOKI-BROWN: Thank you to all Honourable Members for your thoughts and for providing supportive comments on the Vote Item. A special welcome and appreciation to Secretary Marama Anguna and her staff in Rarotonga and throughout the Outer Islands.
On behalf of the Ministry of Agriculture, I would like to thank the government for increasing the Vote Item, although we understand that it may not be sufficient, as mentioned previously. However, we are optimistic that more funding is available, as well as donor assistance from overseas. I commend Secretary of Agriculture and her team for their hard work over the past few years.
Kia Orana to all the growers on Rarotonga and the Outer Islands, especially those who do not rely on agriculture for assistance, but rather manage their crops through their own investments. I would also like to acknowledge the efforts of Papa Kiriau Turepu and Willie Kauvai for their assistance in importing fertilisers and chemicals for the growers. Despite facing some challenging times, we have managed to overcome these issues.
We have experienced some difficulties with bringing in fertilisers, however, we managed to address these issues. One of the intentions in this is to encourage our people or business owners to enable them to bring their agricultural products such as fertilisers and soil, especially products that are needed by our people for agricultural purposes. This is the intention of our government, to support our private sectors. However, it has been noticed that our people are still facing difficulties in importing these products for the need of our people. It was also noted that the containers of fertilisers that are ordered, usually they are ordered to last for three months. Last few months, the containers of fertilisers that arrived did not last long, it only lasted for about two months. Another order has been placed for our use and for your use. I can confirm that to bring these fertilisers into our country, our people will not face any more difficulties or issues.
