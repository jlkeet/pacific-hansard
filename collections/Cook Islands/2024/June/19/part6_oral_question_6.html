Oral Question 6

HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you, Mr Speaker. First, Kia Orana to you and your staff. Kia Orana to our Members of Parliament who are in attendance today. Kia Orana also to our people who are listening, and I hope the blessings of the Lord are with you all. Mr Speaker, I am here to address the question posed by the Member from Ruaau yesterday.
Yesterday, I experienced hearing difficulties and did not fully comprehend the question. So, if I understood the Member's question correctly, they were wondering whether each island received the same amount of money donated by the Airport Authority. In response to the Member's inquiry, the answer is yes. Each island that marked the arrival of the gospel to Aitutaki, the islands of Nga- Pu-Toru, and most recently, Mangaia, received a donation of $10,000 from the Airport Authority. These donations were made on behalf of the Crown and all taxpayers of our nation. Additionally, if I may add, Mr Speaker, I believe the Honourable Member intends to donate all of his SRF for the next fiscal year to the next island celebrating 200 years. The Member can correct me if I am wrong, but we will wait for his confirmation.
In conclusion, Mr Speaker, this information is accessible to anyone, especially to Members of Parliament. As a Member of Parliament, you have the right to request any information from an agency of the Crown, unless it is confidential. However, there may be difficulties obtaining such information due to its confidentiality. Nonetheless, as a Member of Parliament, all you have to do is send an email to the relevant agency requesting the desired information. Alternatively, you could arrange an appointment by driving there on your bike and personally make your inquiry. The officials of the agency in question have the authority to release information to you upon request, and this applies to any agency or HOM.

Mr Speaker, you may be curious as to where I am headed with this. What I wish to convey is that there is no need for Members of Parliament to come to Parliament to request information. Although, I do understand that it is more advantageous to seek information in Parliament as it presents the opportunity to broadcast your proactiveness and your fulfillment of the duties set forth as a Member of Parliament. In conclusion, I have provided my perspective and hope that I have answered the question at hand.
