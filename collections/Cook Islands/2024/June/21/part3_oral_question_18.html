HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I rise quickly as I know many Members have an interest in speaking. I support the allocation for Rakahanga, although it is relatively small. However, I am delighted to see that it includes the Pa Enua government building as a project under the Cook Islands Investment Corporation. This building in Rakahanga is in dire need of replacement and has been neglected for some time. Therefore, I am delighted to hear that the new building should be completed sometime in the near future.
Furthermore, I am curious if the recently mentioned cyclone shelter is intended for Rakahanga as it appears in the major project list. Since Rakahanga is the only island remaining, we are the last country, aside from Nassau, I presume it is for our use. Thus, I am pleased to see that two projects are scheduled for Rakahanga's progress.
Lastly, during the agriculture debates, I neglected to submit a petition for Rakahanga. However, the Leader of the United Party had advised the acquisition of a digger to enhance taro farming in our region. We take pride in our taro as it is the finest quality, and we do export to Manihiki, where taro is not grown. Unfortunately, our labor resources are limited, and we need this digger to improve our swamp systems. I humbly request consideration for this request, as it may help us expand our exports in the future. I am uncertain of the cost, but the primary goal is to enhance our taro crop.

Mr Speaker, I rise to express my support for the $13.4 million that have been allocated to the Outer Islands. While I do understand that complaints are emerging from the Outer Islands, we must bear in mind that we are accountable to Parliament for this expenditure. Since the inception of the PAC, and the formation of Islands Governments, we have yet to receive any Annual Reports regarding Outer Islands’ expenditure. This lack of transparency means that we, in Parliament and the Ministry of Finance, are unaware of how these funds are being utilised. Despite this, we are determined to find ways to rectify this situation and improve upon it.
Mr Speaker, I would like to point out that the government is providing additional funding to the Outer Islands beyond the $13 million, including investments in Health, Education, and Welfare through Internal Affairs, among other areas. On our island of Aitutaki, we have already allocated $9 million towards various initiatives. It is important that as Members of Parliament from the Outer Islands, we communicate with our constituents about the amount of funding we have received and how it will be utilised.
Additionally, I believe there are other avenues for us in the Outer Islands to enhance our financial situation. The government has plans to improve the airport on Manihiki, and we have also heard the Prime Minister mention other upcoming projects for the Outer Islands. They are actively seeking funding to make all Outer Island airports more modern, and it will improve living conditions. This is one way to ensure that tourists visit the other Outer Islands, including the Northern group, and not just Rarotonga and Aitutaki.
However, we know that tourism is currently limited to Rarotonga and Aitutaki. Therefore, we need to spread tourism to the other Outer Islands as well. Mr Speaker, I will conclude as I have run out of time.
