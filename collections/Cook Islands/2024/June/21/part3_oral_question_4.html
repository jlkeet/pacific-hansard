MR V. TUPA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. As previously mentioned by the Leader of the Opposition, I have been designated as our spokesperson for this Vote Item, and the Minister of Tourism will sum up after my statement.
Mr Speaker, I lend my full support to this Vote Item and wish to provide some comments regarding the budget allocated to market and promote the Cook Islands. This year's budget allocation is set at $100,000. However, I am delighted to note that the estimated amount for the following year, 2025/26, will be increased to $500,000. This increase in budget will facilitate travel opportunities for the department's team, thus promoting the Cook Islands on the global stage.
Previously, the Honourable Member for Ngatangiia, Tukaka, would accompany teams travelling abroad to promote the Cook Islands. This method is a crucial tool in allowing those living outside of our country to become familiar with the Cook Islands' splendors.
Moving on, Mr Speaker, this year, more than $376,000 has been allocated towards enhancing flight services into our country, which is a significant amount. For the year 2025/26, this amount is anticipated to increase to $1.5 million and will continue to increase if our tourism industry remains productive. This allocation provides an opportunity for us to negotiate with other airlines to secure their service to our country.

MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Prime Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you. Yes, my gratitude towards our tourism Board and industry for their outstanding efforts in the past four years, despite the difficult times. Their contribution has helped revive our economy. To continue this growth, we have allocated almost $5.5 million towards marketing, with a focus on expanding our reach in key markets such as New Zealand, Australia, North America, and Europe.
In contrast to the Fijian Government's expenditure of almost $30 million on marketing, our Budget may seem smaller. However, when considered proportionately, we invest significantly more per capita than Fiji.
As I always try to clarify when comparing costs, examining per capita costs such as runway construction reveals that the expense remains constant regardless of the size of the population. Consequently, we end up paying a higher cost per capita. This holds true for marketing, as well. Regardless of whether we're promoting a million or a hundred thousand visitors to the Cook Islands, the cost of a 30-second commercial on TV or a billboard in New Zealand remains the same. Therefore, we must prioritise our presence in these markets.
In our marketing efforts in New Zealand and Australia, we face competition from destinations such as Hawaii, Bali, and Fiji, all of which have much larger marketing budgets than ours.
I would like to take my hat off to the Board of Tourism, CEO Karla, and the entire team who, despite being a small group, have been doing an excellent job. Moving forward, I see the marketing budget increasing by another million dollars to enable us to compete with other popular destinations. While their primary role is to boost tourist numbers, it is our responsibility as a government to explore ways to diversify our product and ensure that the benefits of tourism are shared with our Pa Enua islands.
During my recent trip to Hawaii with our PACfest team and to see this island with 9 million visitors a year coming into it, the opportunity provides to businesses over there are hugely available, hugely expanded. But we don’t need to be like Hawaii. We need to be like the Cook Islands. In a conversation with the Member of Parliament for VAI-VAI-TAU from Aitutaki, it was highlighted that the tourism sector offers opportunities not only to big businesses but also to households in Aitutaki. Households in Aitutaki are utilising their boats to provide private charters
