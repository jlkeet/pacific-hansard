MR SPEAKER: Honourable Prime Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you very much to the Leader of the Opposition and I well certainly convey those messages to the current Head of the Anticorruption Committee. This was a committee that was set up with the makeup of senior government officials and the Anticorruption Committee forms part of our checks and balances of the government systems to firstly, prevent corrupt practices from being taken up and secondly, to be able to catch any corrupt practices that may be undertaken. I am very happy that our Anticorruption Committee ranks quite highly in the region. And for the information of the Members of the House the Chair of the Anticorruption Committee does rotate amongst its membership and the current Chair now is the Public Service Commissioner Carl Hunter.
The only thing I would like to point out to Members that maybe of interest to them is in the Budget Book 83 where there is a broader description of the Administered Payments that Ministry of Finance is responsible for. We can see it is quite a significant amount just under the Administered Payment amount of over $23 million that Ministry of Finance is responsible for. But if we take a closer look, we can see that bulk of that Administered Payment close to $18 million for this year is for the Airline Route Development or the Airline Subsidy.
But the Table on page 85 spells out exactly where each of this Administered Payment by the Ministry of Finance goes to. The only other point that is interest for Members also is currently, the Ministry of Finance is involved in the work on the development of our sovereign wealth fund. And secondly, have finalised and will be presenting to Cabinet next week, the revised procurement policy. And that will help, at the way I am looking at it, help streamline and reduce delays, particularly in the procurement of large capital project items.
So, there will be a measure of training provided across our government agencies so that government agencies are very clear on what the procurement policy is and how they need to go about in either procuring or tendering for services or goods. And probably the third one I would just like to advise our Members is, the further work that we are now doing in the review of our RMD, our tax and customs division within the Ministry of Finance. There needs to be an improvement in the services provided to individuals and companies so that we do not create situations where companies will find themselves into hardship in order to try and meet their RMD obligations. And my policy and strategy with the team is to lift and improve their service and support, rather than where the focus is right now is on enforcement and compliance. If you can help an issue and find a solution, it is far better than trying to solve the problem once it is already occurred. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
