MR R. HEATHER: Thank you Mr Speaker and Kia Orana to all Members of this House. The hymn that we sang during our Opening Prayer and the thoughts shared by the Chaplain feel quite fitting for this allocation.
According to Book 2 on crime prevention, I notice there is no appropriation for Community Policing. It could be due to financial constraints or recruitment issues. However, it is reassuring to know that the Prime Minister has recognised this as an urgent service.
The Minister for Corrective Services said yesterday that he’d like to take over the Police Department. We know he has good intentions but I do not agree. The reason, we have three former Police members who are Members of Parliament in this House. Between the three of them, they have 30 plus years of experience in our Police Force. They know where we are failing today and I am sure they can assist our Police Force in improving the standards that we see today.
Vaka Puaikura Community Police was established in 2008 and unfortunately ceased operations in 2016. Crime dropped significantly during that period because of the relationship between the community, our Vaka Police and our Volunteer Wardens.
The three Members of Parliament for Takitumu have supported this initiative and they see the need to revive community policing. It is grassroots policing, connecting people with police and vice versa and it works and the results speak for themselves.
Three out of the 16 volunteer Police Wardens went onto careers within the Justice sector and five were warranted as Honorary Constables.
We’ve had eight major and 11 minor vehicle accidents so far this year in Puaikura and thankfully, no fatalities. Six vehicles were clocked at over 110 kilo meters per hour by the traffic counter meter. Bike theft and burglaries are on the rise and affecting families and businesses.
So, Mr Speaker, to our government, we implore you to take action now, recruit to bolster our ranks and pay them well to provide this essential service for a safe and secure Cook Islands.
As stated by our Chaplain, you have the power to change our lives for the better. To our men and women in blue, we applaud you for your sacrifices, we salute and thank you for your duty and service and may the force be with you.

MR T. AMA: Kia Orana Mr Speaker, Members of this Honourable House, and a special Kia Orana to the Ngatangiia constituency, which I represent. I wish to thank the Member from Puaikura for bringing up the topic of community policing in our respective districts.
I will keep this brief. I support this Appropriation for one important reason. After discussing with the Minister of Police once again, we both agreed that it is important to reintroduce community policing in the Takitumu district.
As our constituency forms the largest tourism sector, it is imperative that we prioritise community policing not just for the people of Ngatangiia but for every resident and tourist in the area. Hence, I extend my gratitude to the Minister for allowing us to establish Community Policing in the Ngatangiia district. I have strategies in mind to assist our people, and some retired police officers, as mentioned earlier by the previous Member, reside in our area and can lend their support for community policing. This will benefit the entire Takitumu district.
Mr Speaker, on behalf of myself and the Members of Matavera and Titikaveka, thank you to the Minister of Cultural Development for their support towards the Takitumu Cultural team for the upcoming Maire Maeva Nui. Though I have not attended any of the practices, Vaitoti and I have been deeply moved to witness the children's splendid performances last night. In conclusion, I support the appropriation for police. Thank you.
