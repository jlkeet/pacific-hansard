HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, before proceeding, I would like to extend our best wishes and a speedy recovery to our Clerk, currently on sick leave. Our thoughts are with him during this time. Additionally, I would like to thank the new Minister Responsible for the Parliament of the Cook Islands and the Minister of ICI for their prompt actions to rewire the electrical systems in Parliament. This ensured the uninterrupted proceedings of this Sitting.
My remarks pertain to the revisions in the allocation for Parliament, specifically the $450,000 for MP Travels and Allowances. It also includes my own observations. I noted that in the 2023 Budget, the initial amount was $250,000, which was then supplemented by two top-ups totaling approximately $466,000. My concern is if we plan to increase the amount, why not specify a figure that reflects the actual expenditure from last year? This would provide transparency on the anticipated expenses for MP Travels and Allowances. Moreover, I am troubled that the only increase in the Parliament budget pertains to MP Travels and Allowances. As a member of the PAC that visited Wellington, my experience informed me that Parliament lacks sufficient resources to support PAC effectively. For instance, we do not have a research personnel to assist PAC with their findings, and we lack a designated person like the New Zealand Finance and Expenditure Committee to draft reports for PAC's consideration. Every Sitting of Parliament refers numerous reports to PAC for further elaboration. My concern is unless we properly resource PAC, the quality of the reports from PAC may not be effective. It is not about the composition of PAC; instead, it is the support system that we currently lack, which I urge the Minister Responsible for Parliament to address thoroughly.
My second point is a request for the Minister, Prime Minister, or Cabinet to review the workshops that we attend. My belief is that if the workshop serves its purpose and benefits our country, then we should send people to attend. However, if it does not, we should abstain from doing so, disregarding that they may cover the airfare while we pay for the allowances.
My third point, Mr Speaker, concerns the requirement of Ministries to report their expenditure to the PAC. I believe the same should apply to MPs attending workshops. We should be accountable for the knowledge we gain from such events and whether they provide value for money. In essence, we should set an exemplary role model by reporting our expenses, just like we expect Ministries to report their expenditure. With these remarks, Mr Speaker, I fully support this allocation.

Let me reassure the House that we have already begun to assess the effectiveness and shortcomings of our procedures and processes within Parliament. We will examine the cost-benefit analysis of bringing our outer islands constituents to Rarotonga, taking into account the timing of meetings to optimise their output. The goal is to minimise the cost of transportation while prioritising the results that can be achieved while they are on Rarotonga.
As the Leader of the Opposition has referred to New Zealand practices, I believe it is necessary to fully utilise their visit on Rarotonga by engaging them in committee work during the morning and parliamentary work in the afternoon. To optimise our limited resources, we should consider bringing them to Rarotonga for one or two weeks so that they can complete both parliamentary and committee work without requiring additional intervals for their visit. I have already discussed this with the Clerk and Deputy Clerk and expect that we will develop a program for the House and Parliamentary Services next week.
In conclusion, I would like to express my appreciation and best wishes to the staff, Speaker, and everyone who has assisted Parliament's work, past and present. Thank you.
