MR V. TUPA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I would like to inform the House that only two Members from our side wish to voice their views on this Vote Item. Despite the absence of the Minister for ICI, I hope the Minister of Education will offer support to the current allocation.
Our party gives its full support to this Appropriation and other proposed increases. Additionally, we highly appreciate the increased fund allocation for road maintenance, waste management, and drainage. Also, we acknowledge that the Member for Ruaau will address the issue of the rubbish dump in Ruaau.
Moreover, I notice that the employees' salaries have been increased, and I would like to thank the Minister for ICI and the Secretary for finding new ways to improve the appropriations for this Ministry.
Furthermore, I would like to congratulate the Secretary Liz Koteka, who has simplified ICI's financial statement and made it more transparent, unlike previous years, where it was disclaimed. Hence, I give my full support for this Appropriation.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Member Robert Heather; you have the Floor.
MR R. HEATHER: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I support this Vote Item for ICI but would like to provide some suggestions that could enhance the road division in this Ministry. It is stated that roads should serve as vital emergency routes, with a particular focus on deteriorating roads that fail to meet the required standards. Although the Minister might find it repetitive, I implore him to be patient and consider my recommendations.
Recent road statistics from the Arorangi main road, captured between November 2023 and January 2024 via traffic counter meters, reveal that an average of 14,500 vehicles ride the road monthly, with a total of 43,105 vehicles passing through the main road. This road serves emergency vehicles, public transportation, transport businesses, cyclists, and residents.

Our country's two most prominent hotels have operated for 40 to 50 years, respectively, contributing significantly to our economy. We owe it to them to maintain better road conditions for our visitors and tourism to thrive.
In Puaikura, we have over 2,000 residents who are employed and pay their taxes, with companies and businesses also generating revenue and paying their fair share of taxes. We pay for vehicle registration annually, and our vehicles require a Warrant of Fitness every six months. Yet, our cars are continually exposed to potholes, requiring regular maintenance and repairs. Perhaps it is time for our roads to undergo a Warrant of Fitness as well, to ensure they consistently meet the required standards.
Moreover, one of the problems affecting our youth could be due to their vehicles getting damaged on the bumpy roads with numerous potholes. My mother had multiple hospital visits, and the ambulance ride on the terrible roads was not a pleasant experience, although fortunately, she did not fall.
ICI mentions priority areas, but I need to request that they scrutinize our region's road conditions. For ten years, we have been waiting, hoping, and persevering on these bumpy roads. Thus, I request the Government to prioritise our region's road development, and acknowledge this as an urgent and much-needed requirement for not just Puaikura, but for all the people of the Cook Islands. Thank you.
