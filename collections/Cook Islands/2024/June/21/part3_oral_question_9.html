MR. V. TUPA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I stand to support this Vote Item and the proposed increases. I am particularly pleased with the allocation for patient referrals to New Zealand, which has been raised by $1 million. This is important as we have observed a significant number of our patients being referred to medical facilities in New Zealand. I would also like to acknowledge the Minister and Secretary for the Ministry of Health for their efforts in creating a training program that focuses on training more nurses and dentists. It is a commendable initiative, and I fully support it.
As there are only two more speakers from our side before the Minister for Health's turn, I would like to express my support for this allocation. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you. I see the Honourable Leader; you have the Floor.
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, I am going to be brief. My comment is really with regard to the referrals, the increase in referral. While I support that increase given the health condition of our people in the Cook Islands, it gives me a bit of concern because we look like our health condition is getting worse.
We know and we talk about it many times amongst ourselves that we need to deal with obesity because it leads to diabetic, high blood pressure, etc., etc. and I think many of us have considered many options but it’s not working. For example, when we were in Mangaia last Saturday, even the Orometua Ngateitei was referring to the health condition and how it is that we should lower our food intake.
And the reality for us is that when we go to feasts, we see this happening all the time. We might follow the example by the caterers of the Apostolic Church. Instead of the people coming to the table and scooping their food, we have servers. I know it’s a very sensitive subject to talk about but I believe we as Parliamentarians need to take a step higher to try and combat this problem because it’s being reflected in the money that we spend on referrals. And we should encourage people to take responsibility for their own health.

HON. V. R. TOKI-BROWN: Kia Orana Mr Speaker, staff and Member of this Honourable House, and our people listening to our debate. Thank you to the two Honourable Members who gave their support to the Vote Item for Health
Thank you to the Ministry of Health's Secretary and his staff for their commitment to serve our people. I am also thankful to CIIC, the Minister, and the workers who are replacing the roof of the health building in Tupapa.
I agree with the Member for Rakahanga's concern about the unhealthy eating habits prevalent among our people. Despite the Ministry of Health's efforts to promote healthy eating habits through Church outreach programs, some people reject these efforts by saying that they buy their food and that we should not interfere with their traditional ways of life on our islands.
Some individuals are yet to comprehend the importance of our message to the people. Our current focus is to prioritise promoting good health among young children and even the unborn.
It gives us immense pleasure to inform that the Green Climate Fund has extended its generous support to us. We, as the first country in the Pacific to receive funding from the Green Climate Fund, can now mobilise nearly $24 million toward improving health and associated facilities.
The initial workshop was conducted yesterday to deliberate the implementation of devised plans. While the Ministry of Health took charge of developing the plan, the Ministry of Finance, Agriculture, and Internal Affairs were also actively involved in the planning phase. In collaboration with the Ministry of Health, these Ministries will work towards implementing the program over the next five years.
The objective of this initiative is to enhance the quality of health facilities, clinics, train our healthcare professionals, including nurses, doctors, and teachers, to identify and address newly emerging diseases. Additionally, there will be programs aimed at educating people to shift their focus towards adopting a healthier lifestyle and nutrition. Moreover, we will introduce health programs in schools that cater to our children's needs.

The primary focus of this program is to promote healthy living by adopting a more sustainable lifestyle. The program is set to commence this year and will run for five years, benefiting from the extended funding.
Now, turning our attention to the nurses undergoing training, there are several nurses in New Zealand who recently completed their primary training. Thank you to the New Zealand government and the University here in Rarotonga for their contribution towards this program. The training occurs mainly in Rarotonga, ensuring that the nurses need not leave their families and children behind.
This is a three-year-long program, and the nurses travel to New Zealand in the final few months of their training to complete their studies. I strongly urge our nurses and doctors to avail this training opportunity, as only a few Cook Islanders undertake this training.
It is evident that a substantial amount has been allocated to the referral program. The Prime Minister mentioned that our people are sent to private clinics in New Zealand due to the inadequate allocation of funds by the New Zealand Health Service. It is noteworthy that the Member for Amuri is presently undergoing knee surgery in New Zealand.
Therefore, I support this allocation for the Ministry of Health. Thank you.
