HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, I rise to discuss the operations of the Justice Department, primarily due to timing. Firstly, I would like to pay tribute to the Justice Department staff who have provided exemplary service. It is commendable that many have abandoned outdated practices and have initiated positive changes. Consequently, lawyers are seeing good service out of them.

It is noteworthy that we have a robust system in place. The Court sittings are satisfactory, and the schedule is prepared a year in advance. Land sittings, Civil and Criminal sittings are held three times each regularly, and if necessary, sittings are also scheduled for the Court of Appeal.
Furthermore, there has been an increase in Justices of the Peace sittings, and my colleague Janet Maki has been handling a significant portion of the backlog of Land Court applications as a Justice of the Peace. Frequent sittings of the Land Court by Justices of the Peace have resulted in the clearance of the list of outstanding cases for judges. This aspect of the Justice Department is operating effectively.
What we do have a problem with, Minister is, with two areas in particular. There is a Division in the Ministry that does recording of orders on to register of titles of lands. In simple terms what this means is once you have succeeded to your mother or father that then is entered onto the register of that land to show that you are a landowner. So that when you go to meetings called by the Court they only call out the names of people whose names are on the register. If your name is not on the register, even though you have succeeded, you have a problem.
So, in my experience and bear in mind this is my playing ground, there is a real delay, a long delay between the making of the order and the entry onto the register of titles. I have only given one example of many orders. I am aware that the person who was leading or in charge of that aspect has left, she’s retired and I am not aware whether there was ever succession plan in place but this Mr Minister is an area that a new Secretary of Justice will have to focus on.
The second area relates to the payment of rental monies and that division I think I am aware that there are only two staff members looking after that area and it is best if I give you an example of what I’m talking about.
About four years ago, the Court fixed the rental for the Pacific Resort in Aitutaki. One or two months later the lessee, the Pacific Resort paid the outstanding rental which I think exceeded a hundred and something thousand to the Court for distribution. As far as I am aware the first rental might have been paid out last month, four years later.
This is a better example, the other examples are probably ten or fifteen years and it’s not only difficult for our locals to receive their rental, the process is even more difficult if you are not resident in Rarotonga. So, Mr Minister if you can assist with those two areas, I am sure our public will be happy.

HON. V. MOKOROA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Good afternoon to you, Members and staff of this House. I take on board the comments highlighted by the Leader of the Opposition but before that at the outset, let me take this opportunity, Mr Speaker to thank one of our most effective, humble and qualified Secretary of Justice, Mr Tamatoa Jonassen. He has been serving and leading this Ministry in the last five years. To me he was there mainly to tidy up all unfinished work, unattended and improve the work that former Secretaries all from Atiu like Terry Hagan, Mark Short, Papa Nono and Tingika Elikana didn’t sort out when they were there.
I would like to welcome our new HOM, Mr Peter Graham. I think he is Mangaian. He has been one of our local lawyers working in the Police, regionally and lately from the Ministry of Marine resources.
The complimentary notes from the Leader of the Opposition have been targeted at praising our Justices of the Peace. On that note I would like to thank the New Zealand High Commissioner and the Government of New Zealand for their support in assisting our Ministry in running trainings for all of our current JPs and as it is we are always looking at recruiting and appointing very good JPs to assist this Ministry.
Mr Speaker, we have some inhouse teething issues that will require some delicate approach and resolving and I am confident that with the new HOM we can address these issues as raised by the Leader of the Opposition.
