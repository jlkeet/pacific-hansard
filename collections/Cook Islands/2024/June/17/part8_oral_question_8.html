Oral Question 8

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you very much Mr Speaker. In regards to this question about hosting the Cultural Arts Festival for 2032, yes, our application has been submitted to the Committee for the Pacific of Arts. At the Ministerial meeting that was held just prior to the Pacific Festival (Pac- Fes) in Hawaii, the Minister and the Head of Ministry put forward our application to host Pacific Festival for 2032, and I believe that it is one of two applications that will be considered. It will be favourable to the Cook Islands to host this event because it will be 40 years exactly since the last

time we hosted in 1992. We are hopeful that the Committee will make a decision on that this year, so that gives us close to eight years to prepare.
This proposal of involving the Member for Titikaveka and Ngatangiia into our group, no! When that time comes, their warrants would have expired. Their names will be added into the group, but it will not be them, it will be their children. We look forward to that. Mr Speaker, I might just add that on the back is also an application we have put for the Pacific Council to host the Mini Games for 2029. These are important regional events because it helps us to also upgrade and refurbish our own infrastructure to be prepared to host these events. So, while we will be upgrading our schools, our infrastructure, our venues for the Pacific Mini Games, if we are successful with the host for 2029, it will lead in nicely to the hosting of the Pacific Arts and Festival in 2032.
I would just like to add Mr Speaker that my own words of a very proud leader of our country, our team that represented us in Hawaii, did our country very proud. Not only their cultural performances were outstanding, their costumes were just absolutely stunning to see, but the conduct of our team as well were something to behold with our young people. As I said in my opening statement, this was the flower of a generation that we took to Hawaii and they really bloomed over there.
I would also like to acknowledge the youthful leaders that took this team, responsible for the choreography of the dancing, the drumming led by Mark Short and Jane Rupena. What we saw Mr Speaker was original compositions, original drumming, not that I’m an expert, and absolutely stunning uniforms led by their costume designer, Mareta Angene. It is right that we recognise these new composers, up and coming leaders of our culture into this new century. We are no longer in the 1990s, 1980s, we are in the 2020s and so what they showed us is their version of what our culture is today. It showed through the enthusiasm, the energy, the passion of our young dancers and performers in the way that they performed. It was a truly, a really remarkable performances that they had throughout the week in Hawaii. We have a unique approach, at times we do not make requests, but our team managed to get there. We had the dinner hosted by the Governor of the State, the programme is set, Secret Service at the front door vetting everybody that comes in, and then when our team arrived, they could not control them, they got on stage and then drumming started. So, the Governor said, well it is so good that he is coming to Rarotonga.
I also wanted to acknowledge the 60 odd group of supporters and trades people that also accompanied the team who paid their own way to go to Hawaii. These were the people who trade in the pearls, the market vendors, the TAVS designers, and a whole range of our own business people here, who accompanied our team and used the PACfest an opportunity to showcase their products as well. I was really pleased to see our master carver Papa Mike Tavioni and his Mrs, who were staying in the same hotel as us, enjoying their time in Hawaii. I had a couple of occasions

to have a morning coffee with Papa Mike and he was telling me that it has been a while since he has been back to Hawaii, but he was able to visit some of his old friends, some of the places like the Bishop Institute to just share ideas with young cultural carvers in these areas.
I would also like to acknowledge during the event Mr Speaker, very important occasion we had which was the signing of our sister-city relationship between Rarotonga and Honolulu. Last year, when I attended the White House, the US President and Pacific Island Leaders’ Summit, one of the outcomes was the US wanting to establish Sister City relationships with the American cities and towns and islands in the Pacific. So, we were very pleased Mr Speaker to have a sister-city signing and with the city of Honolulu. I cannot think a better city for us to sign up with as a part of the United States, because we have a very closed traditional links to Hawaii. Hawaii of course is the northern most of the Polynesian triangle, and one of the islands in the great voyages of discovery by our Polynesian navigators, and the Hawaiian language Mr Speaker when they were speaking it, it was very similar to our language as well. The Sister City relationship is going to open again and re-engage our relationship with the likes of Kamehameha Schools, with the likes of University of Hawaii, and also strengthen the existing relationships that we have had with their BYU, Bringing Young University.
I know the International Sister City organisation is very keep to support exchanges of people, particularly young people between Hawaii and Rarotonga. And I know that some of our vendors who attended the PACFest will now look at this particular destination as a market for some of their products that they are selling.
So, there are plenty of opportunities that has now opened up with this Sister City relationship that we have developed and now with the one-year anniversary of the Hawaiian Airlines flight flying direct to Hawaii, a six hour flight, it makes our connection so much easier.
So, I look forward, Mr Speaker to these new relationships that we will develop as a result of our Sister City relationship to benefit our people, our community where we can seize on the opportunities provided by some of our counterparts in Hawaii.
So, fingers crossed, we wait for the outcome of the Pacific Arts Council meeting that we may be the successful host in 2032 for the Pacific Arts Festival. Thank you.
