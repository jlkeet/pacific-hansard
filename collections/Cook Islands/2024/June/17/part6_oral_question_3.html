Oral Question 3

MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I think we have come to the conclusion of this discussion and must now proceed to Question Time. I see the Honourable Minister.
HON. T. ELIKANA: I will be very brief on the Report from PAC if I can seek your indulgence, if I could make some comments on that.
MR SPEAKER: You must be brief!
HON. T. ELIKANA: The Prime Minister has indicated that the primary role of the Committee is to ensure that the Estimates comply with Article 70 of the Constitution and that the Budget presented to the House is responsible financially. This responsibility falls on the PAC Committee when the Estimates are referred to them.
The Member from Matavera referenced other reports, which, as you are aware, have also been referred to the PAC Committee. The PAC Committee will present their deliberations and findings on these reports in due course.
Regarding my so-called resignation or stepping down as head of PAC, I presented the positions on behalf of the government, proposing that the Honorable Tai Herman take on the role of Chair and the Honorable Stephen Matapo be a Member of PAC to replace me on the Committee.
In my correspondence with you, I acknowledged that the decision regarding these appointments lies with you. Interestingly, Mr Speaker, the Leader of the Opposition suggested that the Deputy Speaker take over as Chair. However, the Standing Orders do not provide for this option. It is the Committee's responsibility to elect the Chair and the Deputy Chair, and they should exercise their democratic right. In our current PAC Committee, we have two Members from the Cook Islands Party, two Members from the Independent candidates, one from the United Party, and two from the Democratic Party. It is essential to respect the Committee's decision when they elect the Chair and Deputy Chair.
It is worth noting that the Standing Orders don't distinguish between government or Opposition; it is up to the Committee to select their Chair and Deputy Chair. Despite this, I support the Committee's Report. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. We will now move to our next business for today, Presentation of Papers.
