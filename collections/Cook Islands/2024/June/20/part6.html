HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I rise to address the fact that the question addressed to the Member from Titikaveka may not be relevant to their jurisdiction. The project in question falls under the management of CIIC, and therefore, is not the responsibility of the Member from Titikaveka.
In response to that question, I would like to briefly state that over 10 years ago, an agreement was made between the government and landowners for the development of a new road behind the Sheraton Hotel. This road would have encroached on the land of these landowners.
However, in the present discussions regarding this development, there has been no mention of redirecting the road, and it is unlikely that any changes will occur in the foreseeable future. It is good news for our community that we will continue to use the current roadway.
It is worth noting that the developer of this project, Chris Vaile, has already built a restaurant beside the main road, and it seems unlikely he would have done so if a new road was going to be constructed. Ultimately, it is up to you to decide if this arrangement is satisfactory.

Recently, the CEO for CIIC was summoned by the PAC committee, and the matter of the Aitutaki harbour was discussed during the session. There is considerable public concern about this project, and so we have asked the CEO for CIIC to submit a comprehensive report to PAC regarding its status. Therefore, Honourable Member, we are currently awaiting their report. Once we receive the report, it will be presented in Parliament. From our observations, the project is progressing satisfactorily, but only time will reveal when it will be completed. Thank you.
