Oral Question 11

MRS T. MATAPO: Kia Orana to everyone in the Name of our Lord. Before I pose my question, I would like to thank the entire Cook Islands community, including the government, Prime Minister, Cabinet Ministers, the Opposition, Parliament staff, including you, Mr Speaker, the Churches, and everyone else in the country and overseas. On behalf of our High Chief Numangatini, thank you for all your efforts in relation to the Bicentennial celebration for Mangaia.
I would also like to thank the Honourable Ministers and Members of Parliament for their visit to Mangaia. All those who visited deserve our thanks and praise. By coming and experiencing everything, you have observed the Mangaian way of doing things. Once again, thank you for everything. Praise the Lord.
To the Minister responsible for ICI or CIIC, I have a question regarding the state of the roads in Mangaia. During the recent visit of the Honourable Ministers and Members of Parliament to our island, the Honourable Minister had announced his intention to work on the roads of Mangaia. If you were fortunate enough to travel by bus on our roads in Mangaia, then you are lucky indeed. However, if you travelled by bike, then your hair colour would have changed due to the poor road conditions.
My question concerns the budget allocation towards the roads in Mangaia. Looking through the budget, I could not find any specific allocation for our roads, unlike the funding provided for the roads in Rarotonga. Therefore, Honourable Minister, I am seeking information about when the roads in Mangaia will be considered and when the work on these roads will commence.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Deputy Prime Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I apologise for my physical discomfort as my knees are sore from repeatedly standing up and sitting down. Mr Speaker, I wish to address a situation that arose one month prior to the bicentennial celebration on Mangaia. However, I must clarify that the request did not originate directly from the Member of Parliament, but rather from the Mayor and EO of Mangaia. They requested support to improve the road in Mangaia for the celebration period. However, I informed them that if they had waited just two days before the celebration, we could have come and repaired the road.
Mr Speaker, as time was limited, only two individuals, Amara and Teina, were flown to Mangaia. The CIIC had already provided support to the island to repair the crusher, which was a request made by the EO of Mangaia.
At that particular time, the EO was instructed by CIIC to produce gravel as soon as the crusher was repaired to fix the roads on Mangaia. However, when Amara and Teina travelled to the island,

there was an insufficient amount of crushed gravel. Consequently, they were only able to repair the roads in the Oneroa area.
Mangaia was fortunate to have the assistance of these two individuals who were able to travel to the island and help fix the roads in Oneroa.
Members may recall that a few years ago, it was promised in this House that once the work in Mauke was completed, Mitiaro would be given priority to have their roads upgraded. Following Mitiaro, the initiative would continue to upgrade roads in Mangaia. However, due to the bicentennial celebrations, some work was undertaken to improve the road in Mangaia.
Today, I would like to assure the Honourable Member for Tamarua that we will continue to follow the programme that has already been established. There have been complaints from the Member for Mitiaro about Mitiaro being left out while we provided road upgrades in Mangaia. In the meantime, while waiting for the completion of the Mitiaro road project, I urge you to ask the people in Mangaia to crush more gravel in preparation for the roadworks.
In conclusion, Mr Speaker, we have completed extensive preparation work for the 200-year celebrations. However, more aggregate needs to be crushed in preparation for the full road sealing in Mangaia, which will commence after the Mitiaro road project. Thank you.
