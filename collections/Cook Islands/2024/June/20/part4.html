MRS T. MATAPO: Thank you, Mr Speaker and Kia Orana to all Members of this House on this new day.
I rise to mention the passing of Papa Tavita Katoa, who left us yesterday while performing the breakfast blessing in Tamarua. He was a member of the Pua Inano delegation visiting from New Zealand to the Cook Islands. Following his passing, his body got brought back to Rarotonga from Mangaia and is currently in the hospital morgue. Today, his remains will be returned to New Zealand. In light of this loss, I would like to express our sincere condolences to his family, residing both in the Cook Islands and New Zealand, as well as any other regions where his family may be located. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you very much, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Member Sonny Williams from Titikaveka; you have the Floor.

MR S. WILLIAMS: Kia Orana Mr Speaker and Honourable Members of this House, as well as all those in the Public Gallery, Kia Orana to you all today. Kia Orana also to those who may be listening or watching from afar, including my electorate of Titikaveka.
First of all, I would like to express my deepest sympathies to the family who recently suffered a loss. Mama Ngatokorua Nana Russell, the 86-year-old mother of my dear friend Tua Russell, passed away last night.
Tua and his family would like to thank the Minister of Health and the dedicated nurses who provided round-the-clock care to Mama, as she had been bedridden for an extended period of time. Specifically, the family would like to thank Tuvaine Numanga, as well as Maria (a Fijian nurse) and Wata (a Pukapuka nurse) from the Ministry of Health for their compassionate service.
Additionally, Tua and his family would like to acknowledge the efforts of Rarotonga Home Health Care Services, a privately owned business. Maui Daniels and Apii Mateariki, the owners of this organisation, provided exceptional care and support to Mama Ngatokorua Russell during her illness. The family would like to thank these individuals for their exceptional efforts for helping their loved one.
Mr Speaker, I would like to take a moment to commemorate the passing of several individuals from the Titikaveka community over the last few weeks.
Firstly, we mourn the loss of David Moeroa, who held the title of Puati Mataiapo. We also mourn the loss of Papa Moe Tutira, a stalwart of the Titikaveka Ekalesia. Additionally, Papa Toreariki Teao, elder brother to John Teao, has also passed away. Finally, we offer our condolences for the loss of Siai Taylor, the beloved granddaughter of Aunty Pito Maeva.
To the families affected by these losses, please accept our deepest sympathies during this difficult time. We also extend a special condolence to the family of Mama Ngatokorua Nana Russell, who passed away recently.
