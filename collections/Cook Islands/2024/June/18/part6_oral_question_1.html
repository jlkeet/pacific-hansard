Oral Question 1

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to all this afternoon. In response to the question raised by the Honourable Member for Murienua, the status of our Police, like many of our Agencies and Ministries, we can be attributed it to finance and financial management. We recognised that retention of staff has been a challenge, not just in the Police, but in some of our key Ministries as well, as we try to be competitive with our salaries that we can offer to some of our people. The attraction being provided by other organisations and from overseas is a real challenge. So, in the medium term, the ability to be able to increase salaries for our workers needs to be hand in hand with the growth of our economy. We need to grow our economies so we can afford to pay people more.
In terms of the Police department with the retirement of our Police Commissioner, we have an opportunity now to reset, if you would like the strategy for the Police department. I would be seeking more meetings with our current Deputy Police Commissioner, who is the Acting Commissioner at the moment, to look at how we can address the immediate concerns.
The first step is to engage with the Ministry of Finance for their shared financial services that they provide to a number of Agencies, to take the Police on as one of their clients now to manage the finances of the Police department. This will allow the Police to concentrate their efforts on policing matters rather than looking at ways to find money or to save money. Using the Ministry of Finance shared services also allows for better budget management so that the appropriations that had been given to the Police goes to where it is intended for. We have heard the comments about the levels of underspent within the Ministry in their Personnel of up to $200,000 - $400,000. Much of this is not money that has been underspent. This is money that is being accrued under accumulated annual leave. In particular, annual leave accrued by members of our Police Force in the Outer Islands, where have not used up their leave.
So, this accrues in the balance sheet as unspent funds. But with better budget management of the Police Force, we believe that we can improve that situation to ensure money that is appropriated for certain things in the Police Force are actually spent in these areas. And it is through this much better improved financial management that we are looking at, that we can improve the remuneration of Police Officers up to a more appropriate level. And in the forward years, I will be looking to sit with the Police Commissioner to review the Policing strategy moving forward to

One of the new initiatives in the region that has been announced recently is, Australia who have set up a new Regional Policing Centre for training police officers in the Pacific and they are looking to establish the centre in Melbourne which will be catering specifically Pacific Police Officers in order to train them up and upskill in their facility that they will cover the cost of.

One of the new initiatives that we are looking to implement in this financial year, Mr Speaker is the re-establishment of our Community Policing. We are looking to re-establish Community Policing in Puaikura and in Takitumu to have more of a Police presence in these communities.
So, with the collaboration of the Ministry of Finance and likely the Ministry of Foreign Affairs as well, working with the Police Department we should get better results and better staff retention of our Police Officers. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
