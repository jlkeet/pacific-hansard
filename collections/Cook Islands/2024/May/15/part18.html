Now that is quite a tight qualification.

The purpose of this Bill is to rectify an oversight that has become apparent in the qualifications for appointing Directors to any Board. As you may recall, Mr Speaker, the objective of this Bill is to expand the pool of potential candidates eligible to be Directors of any Corporation. The current legislation only permits the appointment of individuals in two categories, as appropriately mentioned by the leader of the Opposition – those who work in Statutory Corporations and those who work in State-owned Enterprises.
The Bill presented to the House broadens the scope to include people working in organisations that are comparable with a Statutory Corporation. This amendment opens the possibility of appointing individuals who have previously served as Heads of Ministries or have ample experience in such positions, which is much needed, particularly in our situation where there exists a limited pool of qualified individuals to serve as Directors.
Therefore, Mr Speaker, I support the Bill presented to the House. Thank you.
