The question raised in this House regards the support of the different dialects and languages spoken on individual islands in our education system. I am pleased with this question, as the newly elected Education Secretary, Owen Lewis, has assured me of his efforts to teach our language in schools, beginning at the preschool level up to Year 6. It is notable that other countries, such as Fiji, Samoa, China, Japan, and Korea, have legislation requiring the teaching of their respective languages in schools. However, this is not currently mandated by law in our country. Therefore, I believe it is essential that we include this mandate in our Education Act.
I had the privilege of attending a meeting for the Council of the University of the South Pacific in Vanuatu two weeks ago, where it was approved and agreed upon to endorse a Certificate of Maori Studies, focused on teaching and recognising our language and culture. This is why I am content with the Member from Matavera's question. There is strong community support for the teaching of our language, as it not only encourages the preservation of our customs but also combats the predicted disappearance of our indigenous languages by the end of this century.
