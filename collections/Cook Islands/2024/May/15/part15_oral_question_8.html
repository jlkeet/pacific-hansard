HON. A. NICHOLAS: I thank the Honourable Member for their question. If permissible, I would like to request time until tomorrow so that I may confer with the Secretary of Environment Services and obtain further clarification on this research. I pledge to return with an answer as soon as possible, for discussion in the House.
MR DEPUTY SPEAKER: I see the Leader of the Opposition, Honourable Tina Browne; you have the Floor.
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Kia Orana to all present in the House today, including the Honourable Prime Minister, Cabinet Ministers, and Members of Government. Kia Orana to all on this side of the House, as well as the Independent Members. Kia Orana to the Deputy Speaker, Clerk, and staff of Parliament, and to the families and attendees in the Public Gallery. We extend a warm welcome to the Solicitor General, who is seated in the back.
I would like to begin by extending my congratulations to the newly elected Member of Parliament for Penrhyn, Mrs Sarakura Tapaitau, on her swearing-in and Maiden Speech. As a native of the Northern Group, I am elated to see three women MPs from our region, with only one male representative from Pukapuka. Based on this, we will certainly outvote him in our future meetings.
I also wish to express my appreciation to the Deputy Speaker for assuming this challenge. It is noteworthy that the Deputy Speaker only takes the Chair in the Speaker's absence. During a previous visit to the New Zealand Parliament, we observed that they rotate the individuals who preside over meetings. I would like to take this opportunity to express my appreciation to the Speaker for allowing you, Deputy Speaker, to obtain the necessary training and experience to effectively run our business.
