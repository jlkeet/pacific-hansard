Oral Question 17

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you for the question. The airline flights that we are working on to come into our country, we certainly did put a recommendation, a proposal to Fiji Airways to consider a flight from Nadi to Rarotonga and we went a step further and helped to facilitate a direct flight, a charter flight from Nadi to Rarotonga to bring the participants and the leaders for the Pacific Islands Forum and that proved to be a successful flight.
But the airline business is a tough business and airlines will look to where they can be assured that their flights will provide the best return. We were certainly attracted to Fiji Airways because they

have now a well-established network into North America, the United States and Canada and in the last year they posted a $1 billion profit and they also took possession of two brand new aircrafts just last year and I have just seen the recent announcement from the CEO that they are looking to expand their market into America.
Understandably so, because America is a huge market of millions of potential tourists whereas we are a small market of a few thousand passengers that will travel and one of the only ways that we can make ourselves attractive to airlines is to what we are doing now, look at subsidising air flights into our country.
But this of course requires, Mr Speaker careful analysis, cost analysis to be part of the wider programme of marketing our destination and to work out the cost benefit of this additional subsidy. Right now this is not something that we can entertain and so at this stage there is no indication that we will have a direct flight with Fiji Airways coming through to Rarotonga. However, the future remains promising and if the airline sees that there is an opportunity to make a decent return, a fair return then I have no doubt we will be an attractive destination.
But make no mistake we have a tourism destination and we operate a tourism product that is up there at the top. Fiji brings in a million tourist a year into Fiji compared to their population of just under a million –900 thousand, that’s almost one for one.
Just prior to COVID we were bringing in just under 180,000 visitors compared to our resident population of around 18,000 that’s ten to one. So, Fiji had a tourism product like we would, they would be bringing in ten million tourist a year not one million. But we remain open, Mr Speaker to any opportunity from any airline that wishes to come into our country particularly looking at expanding our market through North America and Europe. We have run out of time, thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Honourable Prime Minister and thank you all Members who raised questions and those who provided answers.
