Mr Speaker, on Aitutaki, a volunteer is dedicated to assisting our elderly citizens who have repatriated to the island. He assists them in securing their New Zealand pension and superannuation, which is not his designated responsibility, as this service is performed by the Executive Officer when he is on the island. Our volunteer is making a valuable contribution by serving the elderly in my constituency.
Previously, the Customs office at the MFEM office provided this type of assistance, specifically in relation to travel declaration forms. However, this service is now unavailable on Aitutaki, causing a significant problem for our elderly citizens, as they now have to fly to Rarotonga and schedule an appointment with MFEM to address this issue. The volunteer, who assists the elderly in my constituency, faces this challenge.
The elderly individuals have to pay a fee of $25 to the government and bear the cost of flying to Rarotonga, completing the necessary formalities and returning to their homeland, Aitutaki. Previously, support had existed for these individuals on Aitutaki, but changes have made it burdensome for our people. If these elderly citizens receive their travel declaration documents, they will be unable to visit the New Zealand High Commission office to begin processing their pension application.
The second problem is the lengthy waiting period for approval from MFEM, which takes approximately three to six months, and in some cases, over a year. I do not intend to blame or criticise anyone, but I want to underscore the challenges that our people are facing.
