Oral Question 13

HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to all Honourable Members in the House today. My question is directed to the Prime Minister, as you mentioned community policing in your previous response. A few weeks ago, the Cook Islands newspaper reported on various troubling behaviours exhibited by our youth in the community, including breaking into private homes and damaging people's property. I recall discussions in previous parliamentary sittings about encouraging policing efforts in our communities. Could you provide an update on how the government plans to address these issues through community policing initiatives?
Perhaps the Honourable Prime Minister could provide specific details on the government's plan regarding community policing. Additionally, I would like to ask whether the government is considering revisiting our legislation concerning juvenile delinquency. The reason for my inquiry is that we all know that minors cannot be legally prosecuted or convicted. Given the increase in juvenile offenses, it may be time for us to consider legislation addressing this issue within this House.
