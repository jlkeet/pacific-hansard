Oral Question 6

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker, and thanks to the Member for Amuri-Ureia for bringing this issue in Araura to our attention. From what I understand, our people returning from New Zealand who are receiving the New Zealand pension are exempt from paying tax under this law. Specifically, those receiving the New Zealand pension fall under the Outer Islands tax guidelines, which allow for the first $60,000 to be tax-free. I'm not clear on the specific tax issues that the Inland Revenue is pursuing concerning our elderly people.
However, I have had individuals approach me regarding their personal tax matters. It is often more beneficial for these individuals to discuss their specific tax issues with us privately. Their concerns may not necessarily relate to the tax-free threshold; they might involve other sources of income for which they owe taxes or cases where their income exceeds the $60,000 limit, making them liable for tax. These are matters that would be better addressed outside of this House.
