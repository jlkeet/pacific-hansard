HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker, and thank you to the Member for Matavera for your request. I would like to refer to the 1975 Act that established the recognised Churches here in the Cook Islands. It has been 50 years since that legislation was enacted, and currently, I believe only four Churches are recognised under that Act. However, as we know, many more Churches have been established in our country since then, representing a variety of congregations and denominations that actively preach the Gospel within our nation.
Next year as we celebrate the 60th year of the establishment of our country as a modern country we know that we have changed and the changes have been significant, Mr Speaker not just from the size of our country which have moved from 250 square kilometres of islands to now two million square kilometres including oceans.
Furthermore, the population of our country 60 years ago was about 30,000 people, but today it has grown to well over 100,000. Our society has become more diverse, with many Cook Islanders now having intermarried with individuals from other cultures, so we have Cook Islanders who are also part of other cultures.
This diversity invites not only a variety of cultures but also an array of religions.
