Oral Question 4

MR T. ISAMAELA: Kia Orana to you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to the government side, including the Prime Minister, Cabinet Ministers, and your colleagues. And to the Opposition, and myself standing between both the government and the Opposition.
Mr Speaker, my question is directed to the Honourable Prime Minister. Last year, on 22 June 2023, we were discussing proposed changes to our tax laws that would impact the people in the Pa Enua. During that discussion, I raised a question in this House regarding whether individuals receiving the New Zealand pension would be affected by these changes, and I also inquired about the potential for this to pose future issues.
I believe, Mr Speaker, when I asked that question, I received confirmation from the Prime Minister that as of 1 July 2023, our people in the Outer Islands would not be required to pay tax due to

My question to you, Honourable Prime Minister, is this: Why are our people in the Outer Islands, particularly in Aitutaki, being pursued by the Inland Revenue staff?
