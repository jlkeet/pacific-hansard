For clarification, Mr Speaker, it is important to note that CIIC is a government agency established by law, which means it derives its authority from the Act that governs it. This Act outlines how the agency manages its operations and allocates the dividends received from various SOEs. The expenditure of these dividends is somewhat different from the expenditures allocated through the annual government budget that Parliament appropriates to the agency.
The annual appropriation for CIIC is approved by Parliament in accordance with their annual business plan and the requests submitted to the Finance Committee during the budget process. We are all aware that the budget allocated to CIIC was approved by this House. However, it is important to clarify that the dividends collected by CIIC are managed under the authority of the Board, based on the work directed by the CIIC CEO and the agency. I want to ensure that our people understands that the dividends received by CIIC lies with the Board, as stipulated by the Act.
We want our people and this House to understand that the dividends collected by CIIC are being used to help improve our schools, buildings, hospitals, and other government properties for the benefit of our people. These funds are directed towards public renovations and improvements in government assets. For instance, a portion of the dividends has been allocated to support the power supply on Aitutaki. Additionally, some of these funds are being used to improve the island's water system, as well as to improve the harbour of Aitutaki.
Mr Speaker, the key point I wish to convey to all of us, as well as to our people listening in and the Chairperson and Committee Members for the PAC, is that there is a difference between the appropriations allocated by Parliament for CIIC and the dividends collected by the agency. This distinction is crucial in understanding how these funds are managed and expended.
Mr Speaker, the process for financial expenditure is governed by the CIIC Act. Furthermore, I want to make it clear that I do not support any form of travel expenditure.
