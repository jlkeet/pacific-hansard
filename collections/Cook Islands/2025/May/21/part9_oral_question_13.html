Oral Question 13

MR. T. ISAMAELA: Thank you, Mr Speaker, and thank you to your staff. To our government, the Prime Minister, and the Members of government today, Kia Orana. Kia Orana to the Opposition and those in the Public Gallery.
My question is directed to the Minister responsible for CIIC. Minister, I know you are a busy man within our country. I can recall that when you were appointed as a Minister about two years ago, you visited our island and stated that you are an Aitutaki boy of Aitutakian heritage. It has been two years now, and this is not to place any blame on you, as I understand that you are very busy.
Mr Speaker, I am speaking on the electricity for Araura. The problem with our electricity on Aitutaki is still not been solved. This is not the first time I’ve raised about this issue. I have talked about this in our previous Sittings but the people are coming back complaining about damages to their televisions, fridges and other electrical appliances caused by the power surges.
Last week, I spoke with the Chairman of the Aitutaki Board and the CEO of Te Mana Uira o Araura Enua. They expressed concerns, stating that things are not looking good because there is only one genset operating on Aitutaki today. According to them, if this genset fails, it will result in a blackout across Aitutaki. This 'horse' is running 24/7, and there is no other 'horse' in the world that can do this; fortunately, this one is from Russia. If this genset were to blow up, it would become a problem for my MP friends from Vai-Vai-Tau and Are-Nikau. As members of government, it is their responsibility to push this issue forward, but I’m uncertain about the current situation.
The problem is next year’s elections and I don’t know if they will get in. Mr Speaker, I will leave this and ask my question.
The upcoming elections are a concern, and I am unsure whether they will secure their seats.
Mr Speaker, I will leave that aside and ask my question. Honourable Minister, my question is straightforward – as an Aitutaki resident, can you confirm today that you will secure a genset for us? This confirmation is critical for the people of Aitutaki listening in, as well as for the Council and the three of us here, to ensure that the two representatives do not lose their seats in government. If you can confirm that you will provide a genset as soon as possible, that would be great. Please do not mention potential funding without a clear plan; if there is funding available, it should be directed towards securing a genset. If the funding ends up in your hands without proper commitment, it may simply be diverted elsewhere. Thank you very much.
