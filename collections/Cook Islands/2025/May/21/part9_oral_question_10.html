This is my question for the Minister of Foreign Affairs. We are currently experiencing a shortage of workers in our country. To clarify my question, when advertisements are placed in our newspaper, there are often no applicants for the advertised positions. When we post advertisements for individuals to work in homes, we see no applications coming in. As a result, we are left with no choice but to seek workers from overseas. However, this process is not straightforward, as it typically takes nearly three months to obtain police clearances and medical checks from their home countries.
Regarding our immigration process, as I understand it, you must submit the application and medical documentation within seven days. After that, you have 20 days to submit your application to Immigration as the sponsor for the workers. Following this, you apply for the Work Permit, which also takes an additional 20 days.
My question, Minister, is whether we can review these timelines. Typically, when an application is submitted, it is checked, and we are informed to expect a reply within 20 days. I raise this concern because I have personally been affected by these delays, and it is important to establish a more efficient process. The response I received was that they would look into the situation. However, I speak not only for myself but also for others, as we discussed our frustrations while waiting for our appointments. We all agreed that the timeline takes too long. I look forward to the Minister's response.
