CONSIDERATION OF REPORTS OF PARLIAMENTARY COMMITTEE
Paper No. 188
Report on a proposal to recognise the Cook Islands as a Christian Nation
I call the Chairperson for the Special Select Committee on Religious Organisations, the Honourable Tingika Elikana.
HON. T. ELIKANA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Before I move a Motion regarding this Report, I would like to thank the members of this Committee. First, to the Leader of the Opposition, Tina Pupuke Browne, who serves as the Deputy Chairperson. I also appreciate the support from the Honourable Minister representing the RAPPA constituency, Honourable Albert Nicholas; the Member from Arutanga/Reureu/Nikaupara, Tereapii Maki-Kavana; the Member from Matavera, Vaitoti Tupa; the Member from Ruaau, Tim Varu; and lastly, the Member from Amuri/Ureia, Toanui Isamaela.
I would also like to thank the staff from Parliamentary Services for your support in assisting the Select Committee and for your patience in accommodating our requests.
Mr Speaker, I would like to note that our main Churches are well represented on this Committee. I believe their appointments were made due to their connections with the Churches. There are four members from the CICC, including myself, Deputy Chairperson Tina Pupuke Browne, Vaitoti Tupa, and Toanui Isamaela. Minister Albert Nicholas represents the Catholic Church, Tereapii Maki-Kavana represents the Seventh-day Adventist Church, and Tim Varu represents the LDS Church.
I want our people to be aware of the Committee Members and the Churches they represent. I believe each member has dedicated their utmost to support this committee.
We now have the Report before us, and while I do not want to go through the merits at this moment, I believe it is important to table it before we engage in further discussions. I would like to say that we have reached a conclusion, and this Report reflects the outcomes we have gathered from the public consultation.
Firstly, we believe that the recommendation presented to us for consideration is inappropriate, as the Preamble of our Constitution clarifies our identity as a Christian Nation. I would like to share this with everyone, “In the Name of God Almighty, His love is endless and there is no end and we the people of the Cook Islands declare that we do follow Christian belief and to apply the Cook Island custom and as outlined by the law to remind us that the Sabbath remain a Holy day and that

day in the week according to each person’s belief as the day of the Lord”. For the benefit of our people listening in, I feel it is important to read the relevant passage from our Constitution in the Preamble.
It states, “In the Holy Name of God the Almighty, the ever loving and the everlasting, we the people of the Cook Islands recognizing the heritage of Christian principles, Cook Islands custom and the rule of law, remember to keep Holy the Sabbath day being that day of the week which according to a person’s belief and conscience is the Sabbath of the Lord”. This is why we believe it clearly demonstrates to the world that the Cook Islands is a Christian Nation.
Secondly, if we adhere to the recommendations proposed, we would be in violation of Article 64 of the Constitution, which outlines the rights of our people. While we have reached a decision, it is also noted in the recommendations that the Secretary of Justice should be given the opportunity to review the legislation concerning Churches that are seeking to establish themselves in the Cook Islands.
Because we have identified and organised a group known as the RAC (Religious Advisory Council), they have no legal standing under the laws of our country.
Consequently, we have recommended to the Secretary of Justice the establishment of laws for the formation of Churches. This will empower the relevant body to engage with the Secretary of Justice and the public during their consultations and inquiries. Mr Speaker, I would like to share some of the recommendations that have been presented to the Committee.
In accordance with Standing Order 156 (1), I move:
That Parliament take note of the Report for the Special Select Committee on Religious Organisations on a proposal to recognise the Cook Islands as a Christian Nation

HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, I rise to support the Motion put to the House by the Chairperson of the Select Committee. I would like to provide a brief explanation to support the report of the Select Committee.
On page 14 of the report, there are recommendations submitted by the Committee regarding the Religious Organisations Restrictions Act 1975. These recommendations were made during the Committee's consultation with the Cook Islands Christian Church, even though the Church did not specifically mention this Act. Nonetheless, we believe it is important to propose that the Minister considers the principles of that Act as well.
In fact, we have concluded that the Minister should repeal this legislation, as it does not align with our Constitution. We recommend that the Minister propose a new legislation to outline the religious standing in our nation. Additionally, we suggest that legislation be created to address the RAC. As mentioned by the Chairman of the Committee, the Minister must consult with the RAC before making any decisions.
However, we have discovered that the RAC council is not registered. This issue is not isolated; other legislation, including the Immigration Act, also fails to mention the RAC. Therefore, it is important that the Minister reviews and carefully examines the relevant legislation. Thank you.
