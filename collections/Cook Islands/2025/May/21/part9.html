MR T. HEATHER: Thank you, Honourable Speaker, and greetings to all our Honourable Members present in the House today. I have a brief question for the Minister of Agriculture, though I see she is not here. My question pertains to our fertilisers – what is being done with the stock of fertilisers currently held by the Ministry? In the Cook Islands, we often face delays in the arrival of fertiliser shipments, and we frequently find ourselves running low on supplies.
The fertilisers provided by the Ministry of Agriculture are more affordable than those from the CITC Store. Therefore, I encourage the Minister of Agriculture to look into this matter, as we need to support our planters who are ready to cultivate their gardens and require fertilisers.
MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Deputy Prime Minister acting on behalf of the Minister of Agriculture.
HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you, Honourable Speaker, and thanks to the staff of Parliament. Kia Orana to our friends who have joined us in the Public Gallery today. I offer my heartfelt words of comfort to the Member who raised the question, as they are family to me. My thoughts and support go out to the family during this time of bereavement and the loss of a loved one.
Now, I would like to answer the question raised by this Honourable Member. I will not go into a lengthy explanation, as I want to ensure my answer is accurate. However, I would like to share what I know with this House.
Mr Speaker, I believe the government has allocated over $200,000 to the Ministry of Agriculture to support the purchase of fertilisers. The proposal entails that the Ministry of Agriculture will use these funds to acquire fertilisers, which they will then sell at a reduced price to the planters. The revenue generated from these sales will be reinvested into purchasing additional fertilisers.
Mr Speaker, I believe the fertilisers have run out, and I am uncertain about the purchase of another container. I understand that the Minister and the Secretary of Agriculture are currently overseas attending an important conference. I will follow up on this matter and I will return to provide the Honourable Member who raised the question, as well as all our planters, with a clear answer. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member.
MR T. HEATHER: Mr Speaker, I would like to thank the Honourable Minister for his concise and straightforward answer to my question. I recognise that this is a matter of interest to all our planters, and I appreciate that the Minister has provided the information they are seeking from the government.
