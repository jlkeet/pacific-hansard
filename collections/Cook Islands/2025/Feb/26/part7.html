CONSIDERATION OF BILL IN DETAIL – DAY 2
The Honourable Prime Minister had the Floor. I now call the Prime Minister.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. In terms of the Crown Law appropriation, we can see in this Supplementary Budget totals $587,000. $368,000 Mr Speaker is to go towards the critical staff retention and recruitment needs. In addition, which is not in our Budget here, Mr Speaker, but which had already been approved in the previous budget is an additional $420,000 through the Economic Recovery Roadmap funding. That brings it to an additional $1 million over what was previously.
I note the concerns raised by the Honourable Leader of the Opposition. In August last year, our Crown Law was in a pretty dire strait with only five lawyers engaged. And the government has been working very hard to prioritise the needs of Crown Law to the extent that today Crown Law has 11 lawyers with two additional Senior Counsels that are currently going through interviews as we speak. By the end of this calendar year, the Crown Law Office is expected to have up to 16 lawyers, including which I would have to be very happy about five brand new Cook Islands law graduates.
