Oral Question 6

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker, and thanks to the Member for their question. Regarding this exercise, I must report that I have not received any briefings from any government, be it Australia, New Zealand, or China, concerning the developments that have occurred in the international waters off the coast of Australia. However, I want to note that our Secretary of Foreign Affairs is currently in Wellington, while our Minister of Foreign Affairs is attending a high-level fisheries forum in the Solomon Islands with other Pacific region ministers. I will ask our Foreign Minister to make inquiries to see if we can receive updates or briefings on these specific events.
One point worth mentioning, Mr Speaker, is that in recent weeks, the New Zealand Navy participated in a joint naval exercise with Australia and the United States in the South China Sea. This might explain China’s decision to conduct naval exercises in the international waters off Australia’s coast. Additionally, I am aware that in the last two weeks, the Australian government and the Chinese government signed a Security Treaty between their countries. These matters, Mr Speaker, are not within our purview; they are treaties and agreements between two or more nations.
However, in due course, we may receive further information regarding the naval exercises that these countries conduct in international waters off each other's coasts. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
