MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Robert Heather; you have the Floor.
MR R. HEATHER: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I rise to express my support for the comments made by the Member for Rangiatea, as I am a member of the PPC committee. You may have reviewed our Report, and I would like to highlight some of the issues addressed within it.
Mr Speaker, the island of Rarotonga has a resident population of 9,000. From January to December 2024, we welcomed 170,315 visitors to our shores. This means that residents account for only 5.5 percent of the population at any given time, while the visitors make up a substantial 94.5 percent.
The question we must address is, who is utilising the majority of our water resources here in Rarotonga? This is our home; we pay taxes and strive to make a living on average incomes. The increasing pressure from tariffs serves as a significant push factor, compelling many people in our country to migrate abroad in search of better opportunities, thereby leading to a rise in the number of foreign workers filling the gaps left behind.
The impact on our agriculture sector is significant. Our growers and farmers utilise vacant land to cultivate fruits, vegetables, and root crops to meet consumer supply and demand. They keep these arable lands functional and well-maintained, all while paying taxes to the government from the revenue they earn through their hard work, which unfortunately often leaves them struggling to make ends meet.
High inflation in the costs of goods such as seedlings, fertilisers, feed, and fuel are major factors that we must consider. If we fail to support our growers and farmers, the only option left will be the importation of goods, which will gradually undermine the sustainable future of our hardworking agricultural community.
We believe that tariffs should be directed towards commercial use and aimed at addressing excess and wastage, rather than impacting our domestic and residential users. Therefore, I support the Report from our BPPC committee, advocating that TTV should remain a public service. Thank you.
