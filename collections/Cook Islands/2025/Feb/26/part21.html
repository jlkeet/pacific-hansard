MR V. TUPA: I stand in support of this appropriation for Parliamentary Services, despite the fact that there will be no increase in their funds until the end of June. I would like to request that the Minister responsible for Finance consider areas that need support for Parliamentary Services in the next financial year. I have noticed that some staff are facing challenges, and additional support is necessary, including the potential creation of new positions within Parliament.
This is particularly important to strengthen areas such as the Select Committees. For instance, within the Public Accounts Committee, we are encountering difficulties with report writing and other tasks, as there is currently only one staff member handling this workload. If we could secure an additional staff member or even a contract worker, I believe that the Public Accounts Committee, along with other Select Committees, would greatly benefit from this. Thank you.
In addition to this, I want to acknowledge the valuable work of Margaret Numanga to the Public Accounts Committee, chaired by Teokotai Herman, as well as to the other committees. Lastly, Mr Speaker, I would like to commend all the staff working here. They put in significant effort behind the scenes on various tasks that often go unnoticed. Once this Sitting concludes, they have additional responsibilities and continue to work diligently until everything is completed.
Therefore, I would like to give my support and call for the government to increase funding for Parliamentary Services in the upcoming financial year. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Leader of the Opposition, Honourable Tina Browne; you have the Floor.
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I stand to share my thoughts on this Vote Item. For many years in the past, we have been saying that the Parliament should be independent. Next year will be the eighth year that we have been talking about this but I cannot see that steps are being taken for Parliament to be independent.
As I mentioned on the Audit Office, this is one office that needs strengthening and for them to have sufficient staff to do the work of Parliament. We need to look for a lawyer for Parliament only. The government should have its own lawyer and Parliament have its own lawyer.
I notice that there is no suggestion for a legislation to make Parliament independent. I know that the Bill for the Audit Office is in its draft form. If these suggestions are not included in the Supplementary Budget, then maybe it can be included in the Budget in June.
I am very happy to hear the Prime Minister say that there will be an increase number of lawyers for the Crown Law Office. This means that in the next few months, there should be more drafting legislation that will reach this House. And this is one way that we can resource our Parliament so that we will receive good reports from our Select Committee. I know that right now each Member is writing their own reports. We do not have degrees for writing reports but because there is no one else to write the report, each Chairperson write their own reports. So, I am requesting that we look at Parliament so that it is well resourced so it can have its own lawyer to advise our Speaker. So, when the Speaker makes ruling, it is based on the law. Thank you very much.
