PARLIAMENTARY SERVICES – MP TRAVEL AND ALLOWANCES (LOCAL AND OVERSEAS)
I propose the Question:
That the sum of $913,516 for MP Travel and Allowances (Local and Overseas) stands part of the Schedule
I see the Honourable Vaitoti Tupa; you have the Floor.
MR V. TUPA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. I rise to express my support for this Vote Item, which aims to facilitate travel for MPs both domestically and internationally. I have heard some concerns from Members of Parliament on both sides of the House regarding travel arrangements outside the country. It is clear that when a Member of Parliament travels to places like New Zealand or Australia, they typically fly in Economy Class. However, Ministers are afforded Business Class travel.
While I fully support this Vote Item, I would like to make a request to the government regarding travel accommodations. I empathise with the Member for Are-Nikau, who had to travel a long journey in Economy Class. I just wanted to put this request through, understanding the existing criteria for MP travel. Thank you.

Therefore, I am requesting that in this Appropriation, the government consider upgrading ordinary Members of the House to Premium Economy when they travel. I want to thank the Minister of Education, Vaine Mokoroa, who, during our trip to New Zealand, was in Business Class while I was in Economy. He upgraded me to Premium Economy out of compassion, and I truly appreciate his generosity during that trip.
As such, I stand before you to support this request and to seek the government’s support for upgrading MPs to Premium Economy Class for their travels. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Leader of the Opposition, the Honourable Tina Browne; you have the Floor.
HON. T. PUPUKE-BROWNE: I stand to talk on the Report from Parliament in response to the questions posed by the Public Accounts Committee (PAC). One of the primary inquiries made by the PAC pertains to Members of Parliament's travel arrangements, and Parliamentary Services provided details on both internal and external travels. It is anticipated that the PAC may issue recommendations based on the information provided regarding MP travel.
Upon reviewing the Report, I noticed an annexure detailing travel expenses from 2021 to 2023. It appears that a significant portion of the funds allocated was spent on MPs from the Northern Group, primarily for chartered flights to return them to their constituencies. The figures outlined in the Report indicate that chartering a flight would cost between $26,000 and $35,000. We look forward to the next report from the PAC, as they may have suggestions on how we can reduce these travel expenses. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Teokotai Herman; you have the Floor.
MR T. HERMAN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. It is indeed the case that we have requested a travel report from Parliamentary Services, especially focusing on the travel of our MPs to the Northern Group islands. In the case of Pukapuka, there are no scheduled flights available, which necessitates Parliament arranging for Members to travel there, often through chartered flights.
One of the key responsibilities of the Public Accounts Committee (PAC) is to thoroughly examine the expenses incurred in this area and to identify ways to minimise spending. Unfortunately, we must ensure that Members of Parliament are able to return to their constituencies, including Penrhyn, Rakahanga, Pukapuka, and Manihiki.
Mr Speaker, I would like to mention that the PAC will soon return with recommendations aimed at reducing travel costs, such as exploring flight-sharing arrangements with other ministries. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Members.
I put the Question. The Question is:
