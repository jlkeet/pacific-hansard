Oral Question 3

HON. G. ANGENE: Thank you, Mr Speaker, for the opportunity to respond to the question from the Member for Matavera. I want to assure you, Honourable Member, that your question does not unsettle me. Regarding the inquiry about who funded the construction of this water station, I believe the answer lies in the Word of God. The Bible states, “Seek, and you will find; knock, and the door will be opened; present your request, and it will be granted.”
Mr Speaker, I have been preparing for some time to construct a water station near the Ministry of Cultural Development. Therefore, to answer your question about who funded this development, it was the Tupapa Sports Association and the Tupapa Housie team, along with my personal contribution of $50, which I believe is sufficient.
The second point is about who will cover the costs associated with the use of water, as we know that water rates will apply. I urge those who are concerned about the payment for water to listen carefully. Our Prime Minister has addressed this issue, affirming that there will be no charges for water usage. Furthermore, I want to clarify that this water station in Tupapa is not only for the people of Tupapa but for everyone.
The water station next to my home is accessible not only to the people of Tupapa but to everyone, including visitors to our island. Additionally, I am planning to construct an ice-making facility beside this water station, so when we celebrate our 60th anniversary, people will have access to ice and cold water to enjoy while at the Auditorium. That is my response to the question posed by the Member for Matavera. And please, do not ask me where the funding for the ice-making machine came from; I will tell you that it is from the Bible.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Minister and I see the Honourable Robert Heather; you have the Floor.
