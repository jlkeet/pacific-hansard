However, Mr Speaker, I have been able to compile a small amount of information for the Honourable Member, and I assure them that as soon as I receive the complete report I have requested, I will make that information available to them.
In any event, Mr Speaker, there are currently over 780 foreign vessels registered with Marine Cook Islands (MCI). I must admit, I was surprised by that number; I hadn’t expected it to be so high. This information is readily accessible on the Marine Cook Islands website. According to the limited information I received prior to coming to Parliament this morning, Mr Speaker, there are no illegal operators or foreign-registered ships on our registry at this time.
The reason for this, Mr Speaker, is that these vessels have either been deregistered or have voluntarily removed themselves from the registry. I have been assured that Marine Cook Islands is an open registration entity accessible to mariners from around the world. To register, individuals must apply and complete an application, which then undergoes a due diligence process. This application is subject to a vetting procedure that requires specific information, and I was informed, Mr Speaker, that even this process can be quite lengthy.
There are several screening mechanisms in place within the process, Mr Speaker, and these must be satisfied before any decision is made.
