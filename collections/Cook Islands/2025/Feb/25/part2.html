SITTING DAY 37: TUESDAY, 25 FEBRUARY 2025
MR SPEAKER took the Chair at 1 p.m.
OPENING PRAYER by Rev. Teava Nanai
MR SPEAKER (T. TURA): Please be seated. I call the House in order. Honourable Members, pursuant to Standing Orders 53 and 54, I have received and approved applications for the remote participation in today’s sitting of the Honourable Tingika Elikana (zooming in from the Solomon Islands) and the Honourable Vainetutai Rose Toki-Brown zooming in from Fiji.
Their participation is governed by Standing Orders 33 and 34.
I would also like to advise all Honourable Members that I have also received a hard copy of this.
I see the Honourable Leader of the Opposition; you have the Floor.
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, and all of us in this House, Kia Orana. I have difficulty with the ruling that you just have made. The reason for that is Article 29 of the Constitution places the obligation on the King’s Representative to summon Parliament at such places and at such time as he shall appoint. The Summons that we have received to call this Sitting did not make any reference to a virtual participation. In the opinion by the Crown Law that was provided to the Standing Orders Review Committee in 2021, that opinion expressed a view as to if a Virtual Sittings are to happen, this should be reflected in the wording of the Summons. Of course, it made sense the reason for that is so that everyone, all Members of Parliament that are not able to participate physically can make arrangement to participate virtually. It was only last Friday when I enquired whether there was going to be any virtual arrangements or appearance by virtual, and I was advised “No!”
The reason why I enquired was because I have a Member on this side of the Chamber that was going to be in Hawaii for a wedding of her god-daughter. She therefore cancelled her trip, only to find yesterday morning of a suggestion that we can have virtual participation.
I would like you Mr Speaker to have the benefit of the opinion that was provided to the Standing Orders Review Committee, I am sure that the Crown Law will be able to provide a copy for you and to pull a fast one like this is unfair on every other Member of Parliament, even if it was going to be allowed during this Session.

MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Prime Minister, Honourable Mark Brown; you have the Floor.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Mr Speaker, the opinion piece that the Leader of the Opposition refers to, provided the basis for the Standing Orders that we now operate under. The Standing Orders, quite clearly, allow for the participation, the remote participation…
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Point of Order!
MR SPEAKER: What is your Point of Order?
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: In-accurate statement! The basis of the opinion was to consider whether proxy votes were going to be allowed and the discussion went on to proxy votes and virtual participation. And it was because of that opinion that resulted in the current Constitutional Amendment to allow proxy votes.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member for your Point of Order. I will ask the Prime Minister to continue.
HON. M. BROWN: As I was saying, Mr Speaker, the new Standing Orders approved by this House permit remote participation, subject to the approval of the Speaker. I would like to point out, Mr Speaker, that the two Ministers on this side of the House were not absent due to attending weddings or social events; rather, they were engaged in official duties, representing our country at international meetings.
With the leave of the Speaker sought and approved, today's request is for those Members to be allowed to participate remotely, as outlined in the Standing Orders. The two Ministers acknowledge, Mr Speaker, the significance of the Motion being proposed. Given this, every effort should be made to ensure that their voices and representation in this House are preserved, rather than excluded.
Therefore, Mr Speaker, this side of the House requests approval for the remote participation that has been communicated from the Chair, specifically for the Honourable Minister Tingika Elikana and the Honourable Minister Rose Toki-Brown. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Subject to the Standing Orders we have referenced, I approve the absence of the two Ministers for government business and their ability to communicate virtually back to the Cook Islands. I stand by this approval and will now ask the Honourable Members to continue with our meeting.
I call upon the Honourable Teariki Heather to present the Motion. There will be no further debate on this matter; we will proceed directly to a closure. I have no other communications or announcements to make. We will move straight to Oral Questions for the next 30 minutes.
