I now open the Floor to Honourable Members. I acknowledge the Honourable Leader of the Opposition; however, I would like to offer the Floor to the Deputy Prime Minister, as the Honourable Minister needs to respond to Honourable Tetangi Matapo. Thank you, Honourable Member, and I see the Honourable Deputy Prime Minister; you have the Floor.
HON. A. NICHOLAS: Thank you, Honourable Speaker. First and foremost, Kia Orana to you, the Clerk, the Deputy Clerk, and all the staff of this House, including every Member of this House and our people listening to our parliamentary proceedings.
Honourable Speaker, yesterday the Member for Tamarua posed a question to me, and I am not quite sure why she directed it my way when she is already aware of the answer. I believe the new Executive Officer of Mangaia, Mr Mia Teaurima, has already provided the necessary information, outlining the situation regarding the plans for the airport in Mangaia.
Perhaps Parliament is indeed the best place for asking questions so that everyone is informed about the situation. However, Mr Speaker, it is not about being heard; it is about being proactive in addressing the issues within our respective constituencies. Sometimes, Mr Speaker, you would say “no”, by actually saying nothing. In this instance, I had a discussion with Mia this morning, and he informed me that he is collaborating with Lash, the mechanic working for the Pa Enua. Currently, Lash is in the process of sourcing a roller machine for the island of Mangaia to replace the one that was unfortunately burnt by the people of Mangaia.
MRS T. MATAPO: Point of Order!
MR SPEAKER: Honourable Member, what is your Point of Order?
MRS T. MATAPO: I want to correct the Deputy Prime Minister for saying that the people of Mangaia burnt this roller machine down.
MR SPEAKER: Honourable Deputy Prime Minister, can you refrain your speech earlier.
HON. A. NICHOLAS: Yes, my statement can be corrected, but that is the information I received. In any case, Mr Speaker, Lash has been contracted by the island government of Mangaia to find a replacement roller machine to support their airport runway maintenance program. The Executive Officer of Mangaia has confirmed to me that they made every effort to repair the old machine that was burnt. He also mentioned that the cost of repairing the old roller machine would be nearly equivalent to purchasing a new one.
The EO is collaborating closely with the MPPS, MFEM, and ICI to secure funding for the work needed in Mangaia. He also confirmed that the issues with the airport runway typically arise during heavy rain, but when the weather is clear, there are no problems with the runway. The EO, in partnership with ICI, is dedicated to fully addressing the concerns regarding the Mangaia airport runway. The aim is to ensure that the airstrip can effectively handle rain during adverse weather conditions.
