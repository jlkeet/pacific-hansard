Oral Question 2

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker and thank you to the Leader of the United Party for this question. Now, I would like to take you back to the year 2020. During that period of COVID and a quarantine was imposed on all businesses in New Zealand. In those two years of the lock down period in New Zealand, Mr Speaker our office was not used. The workers, our Consul General, the staff all operated remotely from home.
In fact, when the lease came due for renewal, Mr Speaker we paid significant amounts of money in rental for a building that nobody was inside. So, when the lease came up for renewal the Government decided to wait until we see how the situation is going to pan out and since that time, Mr Speaker as our economy has been looking to recover, we have been very careful in where we put our money into these areas that need additional financing such as renewal of leases especially when our staff in Auckland have been so effective in being able to work remotely.
I understand the Ministry of Foreign Affairs is looking at a review of our International Offices and our High Commissions and the Minister of Foreign Affairs would be better prepared to answer this question in full. From what I understand the outcome of the review will determine what type of office we will need in Auckland and we will also look at the role of our High Commission in Wellington and our High Commission in Fiji and with our growing international engagements, whether it is worthwhile for us to also look at considering a High Commission in Australia. But these, Mr Speaker will be the outcomes of the review that is currently being undertaken by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
