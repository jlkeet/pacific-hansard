CROWN LAW OFFICE
I propose the Question:
That the sum of $1,838,628 for Crown Law stands part of the Schedule
I see the Honourable Leader of the Opposition, Honourable Tina Browne; you have the Floor.
HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. We all know that part of the reasons why we have only 12 days of the year Sitting in Parliament because there is no business. The Business Committee decides on a yearly calendar and so far, the average has been about 42 to 45 days of the year. And at the last day of the last Sitting of each year, that calendar is brought to Parliament to be confirmed for the following year. We also all know that even sometimes when the calendar says we will Sit for 10 days, by the end of the second day or sometimes the first day, we do not have any business in the House.
The explanation given and I think we all know and aware is that our Crown Law is so under resourced. When the Solicitor General was asked as to how much money would he think himself should have to better resource his office. He gave a figure of $1.5 million, he said that he needed to employ six more lawyers and we can understand that. And I am not criticising Crown Law Office because Crown Law Office has multi roles. And one of those roles include drafting of legislation for Parliament and that is part of their role they are unable to perform which then affects us. So, when we come to our Parliamentary Sittings if we have no business, we have no choice but to have Parliament adjourned.
So, the amount of increase of $587,837, is that going to address part of the problem that we have with Crown Law? Because unless we address the problem of Crown Law being under resourced, we are not going to be able to create business for this Parliament. Many of us are also on Select Committees and we rely on Crown Law Office to be the legal advisor and because they are so under resourced it delays our reporting to Parliament. So, this is one office that I think that we really should look at very carefully if we are going to address the problems that we have had in the past about no business for Parliament.
MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Prime Minister.
HON. M. BROWN: Mr Speaker, I reserve my comments for when we resume Parliament tomorrow.
MR SPEAKER: I put the Question. The Question is:
That the sum of $1,838,628 for Crown Law stands part of the Schedule
