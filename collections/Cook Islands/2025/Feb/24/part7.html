Regarding the comments that our budget is unusual, Mr Speaker, as I mentioned in my Budget Speech, the additional $68 million may initially appear to be a significant and excessive amount. Let me again put that into perspective, Mr Speaker. Of the $68 million, $19 million is being carried forward from the previous year’s appropriation. A substantial portion of this amount is due to timing of infrastructure projects. This is not new money, as you would say; rather, it is unspent money that needs to be carried forward to this year and utilised this year.
Another significant amount, Mr Speaker, is the $40 million allocated for debt servicing. During the COVID years, particularly in 2020, the government tapped into its entire economic stabilisation fund of $78 million and also accessed over $150 million in New Zealand grant funding. However, we still needed to incur debt, Mr Speaker, through the Asian Development Bank, which was essential for keeping our businesses afloat. What we have done, Mr Speaker, is that as these debts have now become due, four years after their inception, we have successfully repaid a considerable amount, totaling approximately $40 million. This achievement has allowed us to reduce the anticipated debt servicing costs for the next four to five years to a more manageable level.
