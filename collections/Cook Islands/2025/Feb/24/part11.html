HON. T. PUPUKE BROWNE: Mr Speaker, in the Budget of the Cook Islands Investment Corporation, there was an amount of $120,000 stated to be for the Cyclone Centre in Rakahanga. And of course, the cyclone centre in Rakahanga has been in the program for many years and in fact, it is the only island that does not have a cyclone centre. I mistakenly understood that in the last budget that our Cyclone Centre was going to be moved forward and be constructed. Particularly given the situation with climate change and should there be a cyclone, a severe cyclone, the whole island will be wiped out.
So, I met with Investment Corporation to see what this $120,000 is for. The Budget Committee was under the impression that that was the amount of money to be paid for the land that centre was to be constructed on. I thought that was odd because there hadn’t been any discussions at all about paying for the land. But the Investment Corporation corrected me and the Budget Committee. They said that the $120,000 was to do more consultations and more scoping. They were planning a trip to go up north so they can go and identify where Rakahanga’s problem was. I said to them, you have been scoping for the last 10 years, you do not need to scope any more, I will scope it for you next time I am in Rakahanga and then I will report to you.
So, this money was not for the landowners. It was going to be for another scoping exercise to pay the cost of a scoping exercise. In the meantime, of course the land that has been made available has the site for the Cyclone Centre has been there for the last five or six years. Now we all know having heard the comments on the Manihiki airport that the delay is going to cost more money because of material prices. And the last time there was some discussions on the cost of the Rakahanga cyclone centre the figure that was quoted at that time, and that was about a year and a half ago was $3 million dollars. And we can imagine that probably in another year’s time that price will have increased. But I am hopeful Minister, that you will have it in your next budget for 2025- 26 and perhaps take the consultation fee out and insert the cost of the building. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Tina Browne.
