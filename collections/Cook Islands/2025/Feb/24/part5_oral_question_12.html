Oral Question 12

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, to the Member from Rangiatea for this question on the Sovereign Wealth Fund. I can report, Honourable Member, that this week, two of our microeconomists from the Ministry of Finance are in New Zealand engaging with the Reserve Bank of New Zealand regarding the establishment of our Sovereign Wealth Fund.
The Sovereign Wealth Fund is something we have been discussing for over 10 years, Mr Speaker. As part of our journey with Seabed Minerals, we need to consider how we would safeguard any income generated from these resources, especially after all our exploration, studies, and environmental assessments have indicated commercial viability. How do we ensure that the revenue we earn from Seabed Minerals is protected?

We examined how other countries manage their mineral wealth, including oil and gas resources, Mr Speaker. Countries like Australia, Nauru, and Norway served as key examples in this regard. It is clear, Mr Speaker, that in terms of long-term investment and safeguarding national income derived from mineral resources, the model provided by Norway stands out as one of the best in the world.
So, after a series of public consultations with no doubt, that the Member has been a part of, the team has now progressed their consultations and discussions now into looking at, how others in particular the expertise of the Reserve Bank of New Zealand would be able to assist us. In fact, it was just last year, Honourable Member that I met with the governor of the Reserve Bank when he was here in Rarotonga, that is the governor, Edwin Oar. And he expressed his willingness to help the Cook Islands in the establishment of our Sovereign Wealth Fund.
I look forward, Mr Speaker, to the updates from our team regarding the progress being made on the establishment of our Sovereign Wealth Fund and potential collaboration with New Zealand. Contrary to reports in the media suggesting a breakdown in our relationship with New Zealand, Mr Speaker, the reality is that it is business as usual. This extends beyond finance, Mr Speaker, encompassing other areas under the Office of the Prime Minister, including national security and specifically Cyber Security. My team at the Office of the Prime Minister is in ongoing discussions with the New Zealand government on this important matter. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
