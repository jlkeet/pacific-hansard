Oral Question 6

MR T. VARU: Kia Orana, Mr Speaker and all Members in this House. The two members of our United Party, my colleagues from Puaikura, have already shared their perspectives on the budget. However, I would like to take this opportunity to express my own thoughts regarding my constituency of Ruaau.
In our area of Ruaau, one of the essential needs for our electorate is water. I recall that last year, there was a public consultation focused on water for the island of Rarotonga. I attended the meeting held in Puaikura, where the Deputy Prime Minister explained the government's objectives for our water system. However, before he could share the government's explanation, several individuals had already voiced their opinions and thoughts. When it was the Minister's turn to speak, he provided some insights. I understand that public consultations were also held in Titikaveka and Te-Au-O-Tonga.
Mr Speaker, regarding our village of Arorangi and my constituency of Ruaau, we are significantly affected when water levels are low. I deeply sympathise with my constituents, especially since this problem often arises on Sundays. Unfortunately, that is often the day when they choose to contact me, and it coincides with the closure of the To Tatou Vai office.
We understand that when people lack access to water, they will seek alternative solutions. I have been cautioned that once water is being transported on a Sunday, people will start approaching you for assistance. Most of the time, when individuals come asking for help, you can't help but feel sorry for them. I want to reference the statement from this Supplementary Budget, which mentions a provision of $500,000 for "water allowances to support household water allocation."
A few weeks ago, I saw some employees from To Tatou Vai repairing the pipeline along our main road. I inquired about the primary water issues affecting our area of Ruaau, and they informed me

MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Member. I see the Honourable Tetangi Matapo; you have the Floor.
MRS T. MATAPO: Kia Orana, Mr Speaker, your parliamentary staff, and all the Honourable Members of this House. I appreciate the valuable insights shared by the speakers who have come before me. However, I would like to take this opportunity to express some of my own concerns that I believe are important to address in this House.
It may seem small, but I want to emphasise that it is both critical and important. First and foremost, I would like to convey my thoughts regarding the appropriation for Education. While I stand in support of this funding, I feel it is insufficient; nonetheless, I will continue to support it. Additionally, I want to highlight the allocation for Internal Affairs, particularly concerning our scholarship students from the Outer Islands who are studying here in Rarotonga. This is especially pertinent for our children who have reached the age of 16 but are still attending school.
Let us consider the situation regarding education in Mangaia. I want to involve the Ministry of Internal Affairs and Education in this discussion, as it pertains to them. Currently, when a child turns 16 years old, they no longer receive the Child Welfare pension. However, in Mangaia, many students continue their education beyond this age, attending Year 12 and Year 13. There may be similar scenarios in other locations, such as Aitutaki and Atiu. The essence of my point is that the government should extend financial support to these students who are still in school, to help them further their education and expand their knowledge.
For those who travel to Rarotonga on scholarships, they receive financial support from the government. However, there are many children who remain on the island because their parents prefer them to stay, often due to housing issues in Rarotonga. These are my thoughts, Mr Speaker, regarding the need for ongoing support for our children in Mangaia.
I would like to acknowledge the allocation made to Infrastructure Cook Islands, as we have been asked to support this appropriation. Therefore, I stand in support of it. However, I must address a concern that was raised earlier in this Sitting regarding where most of the spending for road improvements is taking place, which is primarily in Rarotonga. For Mangaia, the island closest to Rarotonga, this situation is not feasible.
Last year, there was supposed to be an improvement on a particular road; we supplied the metal and tar, yet I am unsure what happened to that appropriation, especially since the 200th anniversary celebrations have already passed.
