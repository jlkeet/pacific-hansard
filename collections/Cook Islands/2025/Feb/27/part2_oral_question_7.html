Oral Question 7

MR T. HEATHER: The question at hand pertains specifically to the contract related to Mr William Doherty and the construction of bridges, and it does not involve T&M.
MR SPEAKER: You may continue, Honourable Minister.
HON. A. NICHOLAS: I apologise, Honourable Member; I was providing a clarification. Moving forward, I would like to emphasise that the government has deemed it appropriate to recognise Mr Doherty's contributions to our country. In fact, the Office of the Prime Minister,

along with the Prime Minister himself, has recommended that an honorary knighthood or award be conferred upon this gentleman in acknowledgment of his efforts.
As many of us know, he received the OBE title as a recognition from the government on behalf of our people, signaling our gratitude for his services. However, we must not overlook the talented and young Cook Islanders who have gone overseas, honed their skills in their respective fields, and returned with a vision to contribute to their homeland.
It is our responsibility, Mr Speaker, to ensure that every individual providing a service is given a fair opportunity to secure contracts through the government. This young man is a Cook Islander who participated in the same procurement process as Landholdings and other tenderers for that specific contract. Furthermore, I want to emphasise that as soon as the Head of the Ministry of Infrastructure Cook Islands became aware of Mr Stan Wright's tender application, she promptly declared her conflict of interest and completely withdrew from the process. Following a thorough vetting by the team at ICI, without the involvement of the Head of Ministry, the Minister of ICI, or any politicians, a recommendation was then made to procurement. It is procurement at MFEM that holds the responsibility for making the final decision.
This is the process, Mr Speaker. It has been in place long before my involvement in politics, and I believe that the current framework was specifically designed to prevent any influence or interference from Ministers. What concerns me, Mr Speaker, is that the Honourable Member seems to imply, based on Mr Doherty's history, which I do not contest, that we should overlook this young Cook Islander. This young man is quite astute in his field, and he deserves our consideration.
Now, in comparison to TRIAD, which the Honourable Member brought up, I need to address this point. After 2019, we did go to market through the procurement process, only to find that there was just one tender submitted. This is not the first time we have encountered such a situation, Mr Speaker. It is understandable, however, since TRIAD is currently the sole provider of this specific service. Given this context, procurement determined that it would likely be a waste of resources to put this company through the entire process when it was clear that no other contenders would be submitting tenders.
To expedite the roading program, Mr Speaker, the procurement team, along with Crown Law, recommended that we fast track the process. This would allow us to award the contract, subject to certain conditions and criteria. Once a local company expresses interest in providing this particular service, the entire procurement process will be restarted and reverted to its original form.
In comparison, Mr Speaker, while the service provided by Landholdings is of superior quality, they are not the only provider of that particular service. This positions us in a situation where we cannot comply with the request made by the Hon. Member from Murienua. It is only when there is a sole service provider that procurement, let me be clear, procurement, will implement any necessary changes, not ICI. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: I see the Honourable Member Teariki Heather; you have the Floor.
