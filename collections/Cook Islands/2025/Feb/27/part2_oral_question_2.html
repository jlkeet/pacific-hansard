Oral Question 2

MR T. HEATHER: Thank you, Mr Speaker, for extending greetings to everyone in the House and to those of our people listening in to our Sitting. I have a brief question, but please allow me some time to provide context for it. This question, Mr Speaker, pertains to a gentleman who has been in our country since 1970 and continues to be here today. I believe we are all familiar with him, he is Mr Doherty, a gentleman involved in various construction projects throughout our country.
Since 1970, he has consistently demonstrated his expertise and commitment through his work and that of his company. His contributions include the construction of our Auditorium and numerous other projects, as well as the cyclone centres in the Outer Islands, extending even to Pukapuka. He has also been involved in the harbour project in Manihiki, and more recently, in the last couple of years, he has worked on the two bridges in Avatiu and the Takuvaine Empire bridge.

He has also made significant investments in the fishing sector, benefiting our people in that area as well. Mr Speaker, he has contributed immensely to our community and our country. Therefore, my question is directed to the Minister of ICI or the Prime Minister, whichever one can address my inquiry. Recently, we have heard about a ‘new kid on the block’ entering the industry. I have no objections to our people returning and getting involved, especially in construction. I am raising this question regarding the bridge in Avatiu specifically to the Minister of ICI. Since 2019, we have been aware of the government’s procedures concerning the tendering processes. Notably, there has not been any tendering for the sealing of our roads since that time, and I would like to extend my congratulations to TRIAD company for their work in that area. It is important to note that this is our own funding, Mr Speaker, not aid from overseas.
Therefore, my question to the government is, why are they not able to award larger projects, such as bridges, to this gentleman who has been a vital part of our community? He has the necessary equipment and skilled workforce to carry out the work. Furthermore, I believe there is enough construction activity on the island to engage our young people who have returned. I applaud those young individuals for returning home and for joining the construction industry.
I recognise the quality of work he has delivered in our country, particularly in constructing bridges and buildings, and he is more than willing to undertake the types of projects that are needed. We all acknowledge the excellent contributions this individual has made. My question is, why is he not granted the same opportunities as TRIAD? Thank you.
