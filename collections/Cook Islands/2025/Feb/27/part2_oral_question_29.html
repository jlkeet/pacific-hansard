Now, I will provide you with the long answer. According to the report from the Ministry of Transport, these are the agencies tasked with managing and addressing this specific issue. I am not an investigator and I do not have a background in shipping, so I rely on the expertise and

opinions of the Heads of Ministries and Chief Executive Officers who are involved in these matters.
Furthermore, the report states that the Opposition's inquiries about the 18 vessels are based on speculation stemming from misinformation. This is crucial to note, as I forwarded a copy of the Hansard containing the Honourable Member's question to the Ministry of Transport.
The Hansard clearly quotes the Honourable Member as stating, "We are aware that 18 Cook Islands flagged ships have been arrested overseas for allegations of participating in the transportation of arms to Russia." Following the receipt of this question from the Hansard, the Ministry initiated inquiries to investigate the claims regarding these 18 vessels. However, the Ministry of Transport and Marine Cook Islands were unable to locate any such vessels. Therefore, I propose this matter needs to be addressed with transparency, as it involves serious allegations, Mr Speaker.
The Honourable Member could have at least provided the names of the 18 Cook Islands flagged ships that are alleged to have participated in transporting arms to Russia and have been arrested overseas. Unfortunately, the question appears to be open-ended and lacks specific details, which would have been useful for the Ministry of Transport to conduct a thorough investigation.
It could have been beneficial for the Ministry to locate these 18 ships, and if there were any merits to the allegations, appropriate actions would have been taken. However, Mr Speaker, neither the Ministry of Transport nor Marine Cook Islands was able to find the 18 ships referenced by the Honourable Member, especially without specific names provided. To clarify, Mr Speaker, there have been claims of 18 ships involved in transporting arms to Russia, which is the basis for the response and report delivered by the Ministry of Transport this morning. Thank you.
Thank you, Honourable Members for your contribution to our Oral Question time and we will leave that as it is.
