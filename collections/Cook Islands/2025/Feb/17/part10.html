ORDERS OF THE DAY
I call the Minister of Finance, the Honourable Mark Browne, for the Second Reading of the Appropriation Amendment Bill 2025.
HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Today, I have the opportunity to highlight further signs of our strength and resilience as we celebrate our 60 years of self-government. And before I do that, I would like to move, Mr Speaker:
That the Appropriation Amendment Bill 2025 be now read a Second time
Mr Speaker, the growth in our economy, and our improving fiscal position which I will outline today are a testament to the steps taken over the past few years to support our economy while balancing our budget realities as well.
Mr Speaker, I present this Amendment Bill with full confidence, as we strive towards delivering on improving the lives of our people. On the economic front, our latest financial year report for the year ending June 2024 shows that our Cook Islands economy has recovered well and is now on a path to continued growth and resilience as well to the future.
Mr Speaker, in the year to June last year, our economy experienced a robust 16 percent in real terms, bringing us closer to pre-pandemic levels, even after adjusting for inflation. Mr Speaker, this strong recover and positive outlook are not just something that we in government believe, but it is also reported in the Asian Development Bank’s Pacific Economic Monitor forecast in August last year that we would grow by another 5.2 percent for the year to June 2025, showing the confidence that one of our key development partners has in our economic trajectory.
Mr Speaker, it is no secret that tourism is the key industry in our economy, and over the 2024 calendar year, we welcomed over 170 thousand visitors to our shores, the second-highest that we have ever seen. This rebound is a testament to the resilience and strength in our tourism industry, as well as the investments government has made to ensure these visitors can make their way to the

Cook Islands. The benefits to this activity are not confined to just the tourism sector, but flows to other sectors as well.
Mr Speaker, these sectors play a vital role in our economy. Our agriculture sector , for example sustains our growing population by supplying fresh local produce – essentially creating a local export market where we bring the demand to our shores rather than sending these goods abroad. The financial services sector supports businesses with essential banking services while the contracting, construction, retail and the wholesale sectors all benefit from a thriving tourism industry. As we know, Mr Speaker when tourism prospers, these other industries flourish, demonstrating the powerful multiplier effect that keeps every dollar circulating within our economy.
Mr Speaker, the strength of our economy has enabled this government to invest in our bright future while also honouring our past. In addition, we have taken proactive steps to work with our creditors to accelerate debt repayment, seizing the opportunity presented by our strong recovery. This early repayment will reduce our annual debt servicing costs, particularly for the loans that were essential during the pandemic that funded the wage subsidies, the business grants and the vital support for our people in the times of need.
Mr Speaker, this proactive approach has allowed the government to invest in vital infrastructure, including road improvements in Rarotonga and measures to ensure reliable shipping for our Pa Enua. At the same time, we have allocated an additional $2 million to celebrate the 60th anniversary of self-government – an occasion of great significance.
Mr Speaker, strong economic management has enabled the government to address the needs of our people while upholding fiscal responsibility, maintaining confidence in our nation’s stability. This confidence extends beyond our borders earning recognition at regional and even global levels, and reinforcing the Cook Islands standing in the Pacific.
Mr Speaker, today, I stand before you to reaffirm our government’s commitment to building a stronger, more resilient Cook Islands. As we look ahead, our focus remains clear – investing in the well-being of our people, strengthening our infrastructure and safeguarding essential services to support the continued development of our country.
Mr Speaker, a key pillar of our development agenda is ensuring safe and efficient infrastructure. To support this $500,000 has been set aside for critical road maintenance, ensuring our roads remain safe and accessible. In addition, $5 million has been allocated to the Rarotonga Roads project, improving the connectivity for both our people and for visitors. These investments will enhance road safety and ensure the long-term sustainability of our road transport networks.
As we strengthen and improve our roads on land, Mr Speaker, we also recognize the vital role that inter-island shipping plays in keeping our Pa Enua connected. To ensure more reliable and efficient transportation, we are investing $3 million towards the procurement of a new shipping vessel. The initiative will facilitate the movement of people and goods between our Pa Enua.

Reliable infrastructure goes beyond roads and shipping, Mr Speaker. It also includes access to clean and safe water. We recognize that this is a fundamental right for all Cook Islanders and to support our communities, we have allocated $500,000 to sustain the household water allowance of 50,000 litres per month, ensuring equitable access to this essential resource. We have allocated 50,000 litres per month ensuring equitable access to this essential resource.
Public safety remains a basis for our national priorities, Mr Speaker. To strengthen law enforcement and community trust, we are allocating $145,000 to help the re-establishment of our community policing, reinforcing the vital partnership between Police Officers and the communities they serve. Additionally, $100,000 has been set aside to support the Police Advisory Committee, further enhancing collaboration and public trust.
To improve the financial oversight within law enforcement, Mr Speaker, a Memorandum of Understanding has been established, enabling the MFEM-Shares Services to provide financial reporting support to the Cook Islands Police Service. As part of this arrangement, certain funds have been allocated from Police to the Ministry of Finance and Economic Management for the remainder of this fiscal year, ensuring accountability and efficiency in managing resources.
Beyond our domestic priorities, Mr Speaker, our government remain committed in strengthening diplomatic ties and supporting our diaspora. To uphold these commitments, we have provided additional funding to ensure continued engagement with Cook Islanders abroad and also with our international partners.
Furthermore, $80,000 has been allocated to support our international subscriptions, ensuring that we meet our international obligations for the fiscal year and maintain our active presence on the global stage.
And as we look ahead, Mr Speaker, we also take time to reflect and celebrate our national identity. This year marks our 60th year of self-governance and in recognition of this historic milestone, we have allocated $2 million towards the 60th Te Maeva Nui celebrations. More than a commemoration, this occasion is an opportunity to honour our journey and reaffirm the strength of our culture, our people and our future.
While we celebrate our achievements, Mr Speaker, we remain equally committed to ensuring responsible financial management. Thanks to our strong economic performance and our prudent fiscal policies, we ae on a solid cash position. As a result, we take this opportunity to pay down some of our existing debt. This will have the effect of reducing our debt servicing burden over the medium to long term, ensuring financial stability while continuing to meet our national commitments.
Mr Speaker, beyond the planned expenditure outlined today, our government has taken decisive action throughout the year to address urgent funding needs. Through Executive Council and Article 70 of the Constitution, we have responded to pressing priorities, including providing cost of living relief to our most vulnerable people by increasing the Christmas Bonus payment and supporting the work of our Select Committees to strengthen our nation’s governance.

Mr Speaker, it is my privilege to share with you today positive news regarding our country’s financial outlook. Our revenue forecasts have seen an encouraging adjustment, with an increase of $9.5 million for this period. Notably, this includes a $10 million in Core Sector Support funding from the Government of New Zealand, a valued partner in our nation’s development.
Furthermore, Mr Speaker, we have observed a modest but welcome increase in tax receipts, particularly from VAT contributions made by visitors who have chosen to experience the beauty of our shores. This growth is a clear reflection of the strength of our tourism sector and more broadly, our economy.
As we look ahead, Mr Speaker, it is important that we allocate these additional funds strategically, ensuring they are invested in infrastructure and essential services that will not only support our people and communities but also sustain the long-term prosperity of our nation. Tourism remains the foundation of our economy, and by making sound investments today, we are securing a stronger future for generations to come.
Finally, Mr Speaker, I would like to provide an overview of our financial position following the recent updates to the 2024/25 Budget. The total Appropriation has increased from$328 million to $396 million – a total increase of nearly $68 million.
Now, this increase may appear alarming at the first glance, but there is a reason for this large increase which I will explain. As government is taking the opportunity to pay down some of its existing debt, to do so we must appropriate for it. This alone increases our appropriation by over $40 million, and combined with expenditure plan for the previous fiscal year which was carried forward into the current financial year of $19 million, these two amounts, account for the vast majority of this increase.
This has been funded, Mr Speaker through a combination of a strong cash position along with additional tax revenues and the generous support from our development partners, for which we are sincerely grateful.
Mr Speaker, this budget increase is dedicated to strengthening the services we provide to our people and enhancing infrastructure across both Rarotonga and the Pa Enua. Specifically, $3.4 million will support various agencies in delivering better public services, while $8 million is allocated to critical infrastructure projects. These adjustments reflect our commitment to improving the quality of life for our people and ensuring the sustainable development of our nation.
Mr Speaker, we have made commitments to the people of the Cook Islands and we have done our best to honour those commitments whilst at the same time upholding fiscal discipline. We have prioritised effectively keeping our eyes on the goal of building a stronger and more thriving society for us all. It is my honour and privilege to table this Appropriation Amendment Bill before the House. Thank you, very much.
