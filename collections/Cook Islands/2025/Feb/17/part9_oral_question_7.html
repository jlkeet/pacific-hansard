Oral Question 7

HON. M. BROWN: Thank you, Mr Speaker and thank you to the Leader of the Opposition for the question. We had a team effort in this morning’s program where we would share the work load. We were advised that there would be no speaking opportunity for the government, therefore part of our team came to Parliament to welcome the protestors and mindful that today was the tabling of our Budget and having just returned last night, Mr Speaker my time was consumed with preparing the Ministerial Statement to address our people as a result of this protest march where I knew I would be afforded appropriate time to be able to speak freely.
In addition, Mr Speaker I was taking the time this morning also to prepare for the Budget Speech that again will be delivered today here in Parliament. But that is not to say that we are dismissing the protest march or disrespecting the protestors. We welcome everybody’s opportunity to place their protest before the Parliament and as a government and as a Parliament, we have heard numerous over the past 15 years various protests that have come before our Parliament and I recall, Mr Speaker the protest march on the purse sein fishing, the taxation protest on the increase of our VAT and taxation of pension, the protest of the Grey Power and also the protest of the helmet legislation and there were people who had very strong views against the decision that the government took on all of these issues. But when carefully analysed, Mr Speaker people began to understand that the decision we made was in the benefit of them and all of our people and I have every confidence, Mr Speaker that when the details of our action plan are provided for open and free discussions, people will see the benefits that clearly will emanate from this.
So, as I said in my Ministerial Statement, Mr Speaker we have seen and we have heard and we have listened to our people and we have understood the need for more consultation that has been asked for and this, Mr Speaker is something that we definitely will be committing to. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Prime Minister. I see the Minister Elikana and you have the floor.
HON. T. ELIKANA: Mr Speaker, I want to clarify that I was fully aware of the absence of the Prime Minister and the Deputy Prime Minister. They informed me yesterday that, due to important commitments, they would not be present when the protestors gathered here at Parliament. As Minister Responsible for Parliamentary Services, I assured them that I would convey their apologies to the protestors who would be attending. I sincerely hope that in the future, we will be given the chance to respond to the statements made by protestors, whether from the government side or the opposing side. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you. I see the Leader of the Opposition.
