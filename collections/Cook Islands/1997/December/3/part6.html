HON.N.PUNA: Thank you, Mr Speaker. On behalf of the Honourable Minister, Tiki Matapo, Minister of Parliamentary Services, I move:
That this Parliament resolves to proceed upon the Copyright Bill in the English language only
Mr Speaker, very briefly on the Motion, I would like to ask Honourable Members of the House to bear with me in this request because firstly, this is a Bill on a subject that is new to our country, plus the fact that it is a very bulky piece of legislation. Therefore, I am asking if we can proceed in the English language only but of course we will be debating it in English and Maori. As we know, the normal practice is, a Bill is always translated into Maori before proceeding. I am asking that we proceed on this Bill because it is in the English language only and we are unable to bring the Maori translation of the Bill to the House. The discussion of the Bill will be in English and Maori but the printing of the Bill is in English only.

The Honourable Member, Dr Terepai Maoate.
HON.DRT.MAOATE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you, to Honourable Members of the House, to your staff and to our people listening in. Kia Orana.
Firstly, I do not see any controversy for me to second this Motion. We have a job to do and there is no point in trying to muck around.
With your indulgence, Mr Speaker, I would like to raise a very important issue right now. Yesterday, just before we adjourned, I made an urgent announcement with regards to the fire that was taking place on the hill at Turangi. I appreciate your support with that announcement because it has taken effect with the young people, the mapu, in Ngatangiia district, to respond to the call. Unfortunately, the people who own that hill and land, not many of them turned up, and the family who caused the fire were not responding to the work that the people around were doing. I am told that the fire has recurred right now and it is extending towards the Avana Valley at this moment.
I am very concerned, Mr Speaker, because we have this crash fire unit that responds to these calls from the community. There is a need for our people to listen to this sort of thing. There is a need for parents to educate their children and teach the children during this time of drought not to light unnecessary fires. Yesterday, there were two

fires, one on the hill at the back of Turangi and Avana, and the other one at Avana behind the old packing shed. The day before that, I understand that there was a fire also at Wigmore’s place in the valley. The expenses incurred through this free service, voluntary service by the Crash Fire, is costing a lot of money, thousands of dollars have gone into that and thousands of litres wasted on that unnecessary happening.
I had a personal experience yesterday, Mr Speaker, with regards to the effort by these young people of the Crash Fire, led by their Director, Nga Jessie, and George Nicholas. I commend them for their job. That is not enough, Mr Speaker, if we are going to thank them for a job like that and our children continue to light fires that cause unnecessary wastage for the Crash Fire people and the equipment they have to spend in order to stop these unnecessary fires. We have to ask our people to stop this and the only way they can do that is to get to our children and others, who are weeding around the back and lighting fires, to stop that practice immediately. We are right in the middle of a drought. We haven’t got enough water for home use and here we are, we have to spend a lot of water, thousands of litres of water was used yesterday, and who knows how much more we are going to have to use while we are still in the drought. Quite often, Mr Speaker, this is taken as an accident and nobody seriously looks at who has done it, why it has happened and what penalties should be imposed on that kind of negligence. Penalties, such as paying for the expense of getting the fire shut down by these Crash Fire people, somebody should pay for that. We are right in the middle of a financial crisis, Mr Speaker. We can’t afford to see such unnecessary spending again during this time. So, I am expressing this concern, Mr Speaker, I thank you for allowing me a bit of time on it and I would like to ask Members of Parliament to go back to your own constituencies and encourage parents to look after their children and make it known to everybody this is a disaster that has happened to us in Ngatangiia, and it has not stopped. It is continuing and I am not sure the Crash Fire people have the effort or the means to go back and stop this fire. It is ongoing at the moment and it really is a concern.
One has to go through the experience, Mr Speaker, to drag these hoses up the hill, through the smoke, through the fire to get to the end of it, to where it is leading, to stop it with that hose of water.
Thank you very much, Mr Speaker. I believe it is worthwhile reminding and telling our people of this concern and hopefully something can be done about it. Thank you.
