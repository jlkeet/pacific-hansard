Mr Speaker, in reference to this report before the House, the Commission of Inquiry was appointed on the 11th February 1997 and was a one man Commission involving Mr Justice Hillyer. There has been a lot of publicity in the media in regard to this crime that took place here on Rarotonga, involving the complainant, in this case Mr Scott of Ngatangiia. The matter was fully investigated by the Police and charges were laid against the offender and the matter was dealt with in the High Court. However, as a result, after the completion of the Court case other matters came to the notice of the Justice Department as well as the Chief Justice, Sir Quilliam. Information which was not known to the Chief Justice at the time when he imposed the sentence on Makitae would probably affect the gravity or the seriousness of the sentence of the trial then. It was as a result of this information, unknown to Sir Quilliam at the time, that warranted the need to set up the Commission of Inquiry to look into these factors. A submission was made to Cabinet and recommended the appointment of a Commission of Inquiry, and on the 11th February an Order in Executive Council promulgated the appointment of Justice Hillyer to head this Commission of Inquiry. The Commission of Inquiry was assisted by Mr Michael Mitchell, a resident lawyer here. And as we can see here today, Mr Speaker, this is the Report of the Commission of Inquiry. The recommendations from this Report have been formulated in discussion with the Ministries concerned, i.e. Justice, Crown Law and Police. The recommendations which have come out of this Report, in discussions between the three Ministries concerned, outline the role and responsibility of each Ministry. In the event of cases of a serious nature like this one in which Ngateina Makitae was charged, or in more serious offences, all the Ministries concerned are happy with the recommendations and have agreed to undertake what has come out of this report, especially their own roles and responsibilities pertaining to such cases.
It is at this point in time, Mr Speaker, that I would like to extend my thanks to Justice Hillyer who headed the Commission of Inquiry. Also to the lawyer, Mr Michael Mitchell, the three Ministries involved, the Solicitor-General and his staff; the Commissioner of Police and his staff, and lastly, the Justice Department headed by Terry Hagan and his staff.
Mr Speaker, I can assure you that the Inquiry did not go smoothly as most people would think, especially in the Ministries concerned. However, the main concern was the result of this Report, the recommendations which have come out of it that would definitely assist the future gravitation of preventing a situation of this kind, ever occurring again. The differences that may have crossed their paths in the past or throughout their own investigations during the time of this case, been sorted out by the Ministries.
At the same time, Mr Speaker, I would like to again extend my word of comfort especially to Mr Scott for what he experienced when this happened and even the publicity that went through the media. I know this will not cover the full trauma of the whole thing he went through. I just hope that this will help not only now but for the future.
