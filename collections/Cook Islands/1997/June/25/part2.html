ORDERS OF THE DAY
Constitution Amendment (No.22) Bill, Third Reading. We will now take the Third Reading. The Honourable Minister of Finance, Hon. Sir Geoffrey Henry, K. B. E.
HON. SIR G.A. HENRY: Thank you Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you, to all Members of Parliament and a very warm Kia Orana to our people who are listening in to our discussion this afternoon.
Mr Speaker, I stand to move:
That the Constitution Amendment (No.22) Bill be now read a Third time.
I am certain, Mr Speaker, that Members of the House will recall our spirited discussion of this Amendment when the Second Reading was taken. I have no wish to traverse the Merits and Principles of the Bill, nor indeed the various important issues raised in connection with it. Suffice to say, that it is one extra aspect of the reform programme that Government is bringing into place. It goes without saying, Mr Speaker, that the reform is an ongoing exercise and may at times necessitate bringing statutory documents for approval of this House. This Constitution Amendment has been laid off for the constitutionally required 90 days and those 90 days are now up. I am therefore pleased to move the Motion for the third reading of the Constitution Amendment Bill. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Mr Prime Minister. May I call for a Seconder please?
Seconded by Mr Thomas Hewett.
Before I put the question before the House, because this is a Constitutional requirement, I would like to ask all Members to rise from your seats when the question is raised.
The question is:
That Constitution Amendment (No.22) Bill be now read a Third time
Those in favour please rise from your seats.
