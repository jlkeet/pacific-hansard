MR SPEAKER took the Chair at 1.00 p.m.
MR SPEAKER: Honourable Members, please be seated. Kia Orana to Honourable Members in the name of our good Lord this afternoon.
Firstly, a word of thank you to our chaplain, Father Jim for his encouraging message to us this afternoon.
Before we go on to our work for today, I have an announcement to make. The message that I am about to read is a reply to a letter to the Clerk of Parliament that I sent back because I did not want to read that letter in this House. I do not want Members of Parliament to write letters to be read in this Parliament, except for letters from that Office.
This is what was written in that letter. This is a letter written by the General Secretary of the Democratic Alliance Party, Mr Aporo Dean.
“The Democratic Alliance Party, Avarua, Rarotonga, Cook Islands.
26 November 1997.
To the Clerk of Parliament, Department of Parliamentary Services, P.O. Box 13, Rarotonga, Cook Islands.
Dear Sir,
It has come to our attention that Norman George M.P. for the electorate of Tengatangi/ Areora/ Ngatiarua has formally requested by letter to you, that he and fellow Atiu M.P., Upoko Simpson, be disassociated from the Opposition Caucus.
The Democratic Alliance Party and Opposition Caucus are saddened by the step taken by Mr George and regard it as childish. We believe he took this action as a response to him being replaced by the Opposition Caucus with Dr Terepai Maoate as Leader of the Opposition.
I make an emphatic distinction between Mr George and Mr Simpson because we understand that Mr Simpson was unaware of Mr George’s action. We wish to warmly invite Mr Upoko Simpson to remain with the Opposition Caucus. The decision by

Caucus to vote Mr George out as Leader of the Opposition was done openly and democratically at a meeting Mr George himself chaired. Therefore, it is hard to understand his motives in disassociating himself from the Opposition Caucus. We are obviously disappointed with Mr George’s latest antics but although reluctant we have no choice but to accept his request and hope that you will accept it accordingly.
Yours sincerely,
Mr Aporo Dean, Democratic Alliance Party”.
I now place it on the Table.
