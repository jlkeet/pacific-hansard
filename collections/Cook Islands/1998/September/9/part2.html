Electoral Bill. Interrupted debate on the Question:
That the Bill be now read a Second Time
I can see the Honourable Minister Tiki Matapo is already on the Floor now, so I call upon the Honourable Minister.
HON. T. MATAPO: Thank you Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and your staff, to Honourable Members in the House, Kia Orana to you all. Kia Orana to our people who are listening in this afternoon.
Mr Speaker, I stand to seek the Leave of this House so that this Sitting may adjourn, to enable Members to have an informal Sitting and to discuss the Bill in-camera. Perhaps I should say, suspend the Sitting, Mr Speaker, to enable Members to discuss the Bill in-camera.
As you may recall during the debate on the principle and merits of the Bill a strong consensus of Members which in fact was brought also to the attention of the both Leaders of the Opposition parties. This suggestion was that we be able to meet and

MR SPEAKER: Thank you Honourable Minister. The Honourable Member Dr Terepai Maoate, Leader of the Democratic Alliance Party.
HON. DR.T. MAOATE: Thank you Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you and your staff, to Honourable Members and a special Kia Orana to our Prime Minister - welcome back to the Chamber. Kia Orana also to our people who are listening in.
Mr Speaker, I stand to support the request by the Minister in charge to discuss the Bill informally. When the Bill was presented, we in the Opposition had a look at it and felt that there is no real need to argue much about it, especially since the whole Bill has been rewritten - it does not include just the amendments that are presented. It is a completely new draft altogether. The benefit of looking through the whole Bill is to both sides of the House. So we have made a suggestion to the Minister in charge that this could be done. I am glad that the Minister has, along with Government, approved to have this discussed informally first. I am sure, Mr Speaker, we should be able to go through the Bill quite fluently and quickly. After we have done that we will have made our final decisions or conclusions on the Bill.
So those are my comments and suggestions, Mr Speaker. But before I sit down, I would like to endorse your welcoming remarks to the Honourable Dr Williams. Welcome back to the Chamber. You will no doubt go through the Hansard and see the comments made to your request to be pardoned for being away from Parliament. It was all to your favour and to the favour of the Honourable Members of the House.
Thank you Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Honourable Member. Now the Honourable Minister is now seeking the Leave of the House to suspend this Sitting. What is the opinion of the Honourable Members?
