HON. DR R. WOONTON: Mr Speaker I move:
That the International Shipping Bill be Read a Second Time
The purpose of this Bill is to make specific statutory provision to preserve and enhance national control of Cook Islands International Shipping services. To ensure that control and regulation of Cook Islands Shipping remains in the hands of the Cook Islands.
Three licences are currently issued to allow the holders to carry on international shipping services to and from the Cook Islands. The purpose of allowing a number of ships to carry on this service is to enhance the service and to promote competition. This Bill addresses the need to ensure that those enjoying a dominant position in regional shipping do not take competitive forces. The Bill aims to prevent the establishment of a monopoly or cartel of Cook Islands international shipping services.
The Bill addresses the activities of foreign interests who do not have a direct presence within the country by giving statutory recognition and encouragement to the use of ships which are genuinely owned and controlled by persons present in the jurisdiction true national control of the country’s shipping requires ships and ship-owners who are Cook Islands based. The Bill outlaws unfair and anti-competitive practices in a number of provisions, which are modelled on similar provisions in New Zealand’s Commerce Act 1986 and Shipping Act 1987.
The Bill recognises that while foreign interests may attempt to manipulate Cook Islands international shipping services without reference to such actions taken will be

The Bill makes provision for such carriers to enter into agreements with the Minister to negate anti- competitive or unfair practices entered into by associated persons where those practices affect Cook Islands ships or Cook Islands international shipping service.
While the Bill provides some protection for shipping licence holders to conduct their businesses in the normal business manner, it does not in any way disadvantage or prevent any licence holder from operating their line by bringing into force some control on the fair trade as far as shipping is concerned in this country.
