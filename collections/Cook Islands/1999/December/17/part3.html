ORDERS OF THE DAY
Rarotonga Environment Amendment Bill. Interrupted debate on the Second Reading.
Our last speaker was the Honourable Deputy Prime Minister Norman George. You have the Floor Sir and you have 18 minutes to go on your speech.
HON. N. GEORGE: Thank you Mr Speaker. May I begin by saying my Greetings from yesterday, continuing today.
Now, I would like to say that the simple reason for this Amendment is to allow the new Government to put their choice of people on the Environment Board. The original Act gives power of removal only when there are faults of one kind or another with council members. Understandably, no provisions were made for the change of Government, you do not see that kind of thing and it was never done.
Mr Speaker, what all Governments in the past have done was, to rely on the protocol or convention of the practice that when one Government leaves the office, those on Government appointed Boards will automatically offer their resignations to the new Government. This practice or convention as we call it is unwritten. Normally those with the right attitude, those with a concern for their standing, would automatically submit their resignation to the new Government. It is up to the incoming Government whether to accept the resignation or to decline it.
In this instance, we struck a slight problem where the Chairman of the Environment Council decided he did not wish to resign. Maybe the former Prime Minister Dr Joseph Williams set an unsavoury precedent where you go to the wire. So this gentleman, the Chairman of the Environment Council decided he would follow a similar course. I will not mention any names at all to save the good reputation of the

individual concerned. This particular person wrote a letter to me saying that under the Act, he had no obligation to step down. Then he also made a counter allegation containing a lot of political and racially biased statements towards the new appointees. It was a very sad situation where a highly educated gentleman writes a letter embarking upon the racial origins of number of members of the council, which the new Government has appointed.
Mr Speaker, this Government’s standard for equality before the law, God, and every citizen of this country regardless of your race and background is very sound. If Government chooses that individual because of his ability, education and interest to protect the environment of the country you will be appointed by this Government. I expect the Chairman to go with grace, to accept our wishes. I have indicated that was not only my wish but also that of Cabinet. He took the extreme interpretation to buck the system whereby we are compelled to amend the law giving the Minister the right to remove any member of the council. This gives the Minister the right to remove any member of the council without any particular fault. But our legal system protects those who feel wrongly removed to remedy or to repair any injury that is likely to be received if an action like this is to be taken.
Mr Speaker, there is no injustice here. If there is a victim in this situation, it is the Government of the day. Somebody has decided because there is no provision to allow Government to replace him that he would victimise Government by forcing this amendment to be made.
If there is any fear, Mr Speaker, that the current and future members of the Board will live under a regime of threats and fear, I want to assure them that that is utter nonsense. Mr Speaker, we are dealing with the Environmental Council, not some highflying confrontational body. These are earth loving people who love their environment, who love to preserve the environment for the future generations and there is no likelihood whatsoever of confrontational administration.
The people that this Government selected to serve on the Environmental Council were appointed due to their expertise, good reputation and devotion to the protection of the environment. These are people who have shown willingness in the past to help secure a good environment in this country.
Some of these people on the Environment Council are people that we have seen time and time again. Actually, they were the ones going out to the beaches, to the parks to get their hands dirty, to collect rubbish, to pick up tin cans, empty bottles. These are the people that we have identified and put on the new Council.
Mr Speaker, I have spent my professional life fighting for people’s rights as a trial lawyer. No one needs to fear having their rights threatened under my stewardship. Therefore, I urge those who wish to enter the debate to stay away from personalities, not to mention names, and not to crucify the reputations of people who are not here to defend themselves. I undertake to be a gentleman and not bring up unsavoury matters that are likely to embarrass the personalities concerned.
I urge Honourable Members to please respect the privacy of the individuals involved in these discussions. Therefore, Mr Speaker, in conclusion, I look forward to hearing
