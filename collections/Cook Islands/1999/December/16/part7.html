MR SPEAKER: Excuse me Prime Minister? Can you move the Motion for the Second Reading?
HON. DR T. MAOATE: Thank you Mr Speaker. I have the pleasure to move:
That the Public Holidays Bill be now read a Second time
As I said earlier, Mr Speaker, this Bill repeals and replaces the Public Holidays Ordinance 1949 as well as re-enacting the same days which the Ordinance sets out the days following New Year’s Day as new public holidays. Under the 1949 Ordinance only the Constitution Day and the Cook Islands Gospel Days were carried forward to the following Monday if it falls on the week ends. This coming Christmas and New Year’s Day will fall on Saturday and therefore it was necessary for this to be brought into the House for amendment. We were not only concerned with Christmas and New Year holidays, we have also considered the other holidays that have not been considered in the Act which falls on Saturdays and Sundays. These should be taken in lieu on Monday, similar to Christmas and New Year’s days. A few other comments on this Bill.
Clause 1 is the Short Title, there is no Commencement provisions. Therefore, the Bill will take effect as soon as it is assented to by the Queen’s Representative.
Clause 2 of course is Interpretation and defines the various public holidays as I have mentioned earlier.
Under Clause 3, it provides National Public Holidays and those days are set out in the First Schedule to the Bill.
Clause 4 provides for Island Gospel Days and those days are defined by Orders in Executive Council.
Clause 5, the Orders in Executive Council made under the 1949 Ordinance will continue to be in effect as if made by this Bill.
Clause 6 repeals the 1949 Public Holidays Ordinance, which this bill replaces. And the Schedules are listed in the First Schedule which itemizes the various National Public Holidays. The Second Schedule lists Orders in Executive Council which were made under the 1949 Ordinance. These shall continue to be in force as if they were made under this Bill.
