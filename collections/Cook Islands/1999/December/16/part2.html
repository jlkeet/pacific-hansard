PRAYER
MR SPEAKER took the Chair at 1.00 p.m.
MR SPEAKER: Please be seated Honourable Members. First, I would like to welcome all Honourable Members back to Parliament this afternoon. I would like to welcome the new executive arm of Parliament, the new Government and our new Opposition. I do pray that we will be able to conduct the affairs of our country as we used to before, and that God’s blessings will be upon each Honourable Member.
I have a few announcements. First, I would like on your behalf Honourable Members, to acknowledge the passing away of Mrs Kopu Brown former Secretary of the Ministry of Internal Affairs. She passed away at the age of 54 in Auckland New Zealand on Saturday the 11th of December this year and she was laid to rest on Tuesday the 14th of December at her home in Karekare, Tupapa. She is survived by her husband Henry Keta Brown, the former Ombudsman, her children and grandchildren. I am sure that the Honourable Members will join with me in extending our sincere condolences to Henry Brown, his family, children, grandchildren and the family on both sides. In particular, to the Honourable Sir Geoffrey Henry an uncle of the late Kopu Brown.
Secondly, we acknowledge the visit of the Australian Members of Parliament led by the Hon. Gary Neale, Deputy Speaker of the House of Representatives of Australia. They visited us on the 20th of November through to the 4th of December. The other members of the delegation were the Hon. Frank Mossfield, the Hon. Jim Lloyd and Michael Mclean, Acting High Commissioner of Australia to New Zealand. They were accompanied by Mrs Mossfield. Those members who came to the lunch our branch hosted for these friends from Australia, I wish to thank you very much for your presence. There is one matter that was conveyed to our Branch through this delegation but that will be discussed with our Executive tomorrow.
Thirdly, I would like to thank the following Honourable Members. The Hon. Tapi Taio, Minister of Works and the Hon. Vaevae Pare for their kind donation to make the bridge at the back of the Parliament grounds. Now, you can see that we have a beautiful bridge over the drainage. The labour was provided by the Parliament staff.
And fourthly, Honourable Members, I know that we feel very happy this afternoon coming into a cool Chamber – for the first time. We thank God for this. It will make our work for our country more bearable and more pleasant. And special thanks to the Southcold Company of Tere Tai and his team for installing an excellent air conditioning plant.
Last but not least, I wish to inform the Honourable Members of the appointment of our new Sub-Editor of Debates, Mrs Tutai Taia Manavaroa as of the 18th November this year. Already she has made a very good impression on the family of the Legislative Service and we are very happy to have her on board.
