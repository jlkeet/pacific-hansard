MR SPEAKER: Please be seated Honourable Members. Parliament is now resumed. The Honourable Member Tupou Faireka was the last speaker before we went for a break, please carry on Honourable Member.
MR. T. FAIREKA: Thank you Mr Speaker. I should not be long now. I was just talking about the performance of the audit department. The only disadvantage as I see the performance of the audit service is that there is not enough funds to send members of the audit team to the outer islands to continue audit reporting on the accounts of the outer islands. I think if the auditing of Government services or departments had been regular as is the situation on Rarotonga, a lot of mishandling of funds of the outer islands would have been detected earlier than they have been. As an example Mr Speaker the situation in Mitiaro recently where one of the public servant employees was convicted of mishandling some of those finances. As a result, we lost some $68,000 of public funds and this staff member has been sent to gaol. Therefore, I will urge the Government to please look into the problem, especially in assisting audit staff to make regular visits to the outer islands and to sit down with the Mayors, chief executive officers and public servants of that particular island. Most of them would not have the experience to do accounting and management so they should go over to assist with the accounting of public finance and accounts.
Lastly, I would like to give my appreciation to the members of the PERCA team. Since their existence, they have been working very hard, they have not been deterred from reporting to Parliament. They were not even deterred to give a report concerning Government, Cabinet Members and public servants. To prove what I am saying there are some reports following this report. We have read some of the reports tabled in this House concerning some services performed by Cabinet and Government. It is good because it is reported whether their views are right or wrong and whether it is in line with the statutory requirements of MFEM and other statutes. However, I support the views aired previously that there is some amendments required to be put in place so there may be some redress for people who are being accused in a report against those accusations. With those comments, I give my full support to this report.
