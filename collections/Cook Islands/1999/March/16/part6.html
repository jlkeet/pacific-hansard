Companies Amendment Bill. The Honourable Minister Tiki Matapo.
HON. T. MATAPO: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Mr Speaker I move:
That the Companies Amendment Bill be Read a Second time
Greetings to you Mr Speaker and to all the staff Members of Parliament. Greetings to all the Honourable Members in the House and to our people listening to the debate. To those Members who are still on the way, the Prime Minister, Kia Orana to them too.
Mr Speaker the Companies Amendment Bill serves to address several areas found lacking in our existing legislation today. In fact this is to bring the Companies legislation more in line with current practices today. The first concern is the recouping of costs by reviewing costs and fees, something that has not been done

since 1971. Other concerns dealt with in the Amendment include better management and control of Companies’ records and affairs and defining of offences. This Amendment Bill serves a number of functions.
Firstly, this is the first review of Companies fees since 1971. The first Schedule, which forms part of the Bill, establishes equality with other charges and fees currently levied by the Ministry of Justice. My colleagues in Cabinet will recall in 1997 there was an Amendment to Court costs fees allowances and charges as negotiated with the Rules Committee. The Rules Committee comprises of the Chief Justice, the Solicitor General, and the President of the Law Society. Secondly, it will empower the Registrar to inspect Company records and act to ensure compliance with the Act. This Amendment therefore Mr Speaker brings our law in line with the New Zealand legislation. Thirdly, it amends the principal Act by requiring Companies to submit an annual return, at the same time pay an annual return fee. The annual return fee will replace the annual licence, which has been paid by Companies up to now. This will enable a much improved administration of Companies and again is based on existing New Zealand legislation. Further, the amendment introduces new offences widening the policing powers of the Act which under existing law is limited to that of making false statements. The amendment will cover the making of false statements, the fraudulent use of property, its destruction or concealment. And the concealment of property, debts, books, payments of any Company under liquidation. Including the altering or falsifying of books, records or documents of any Company. Finally the offences include obtaining transfer credit or property by fraud with the intent to defraud creditors. This also increases fines payable by officers or Companies in default. Until now, this has been $10 and today this amendment seeks to increase it to the amount of $100. The total Companies resident within the Companies Division of the Justice Department are 760 to 800 Companies. There has not been a review of this particular Act since 1971 and some of these amendments which I have highlighted were problems which have been encountered by the Justice Department in implementing some of the requirements of the Act. One of the significant amendments is the requirement on the part of Companies to furnish an annual return to the Justice Department.
The first draft of the Bill goes back to 1995. But I am pleased to say that I am able to bring this before the House to be enacted. Undoubtedly it will help the policing of the Act and hopefully a better response from the Companies in their requirements under the Act.
Those are my comments I wish to make Mr Speaker, thank you very much.
