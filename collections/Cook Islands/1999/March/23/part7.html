That Commission represented the view of the people. This Parliament agreed to pick up that recommendation, conscious of the comments and recommendations of the Political Review Commission. It is not this Parliament nor Government that restricted the choice to 4 or 5. By that I meant it was not Government or it was not Parliament who is by its own volition, in favour of that choice. Constitution Amendment (No.25) Bill, Mr Speaker, refers to the ability of the new Parliament to pass an enabling legislation in respect of three matters. Firstly, the question of Crossing the Floor. The intention is for an Act to be put in place to manage that process so that it is done in a reasonable fashion, a clear transparent fashion that takes into account the interests and rights of both the elected Member and his electors.
The second issue concerns the appointment of a National Appointments Council. Such a council, once the enabling Act is in place, would have the authority to make recommendations for all of the judicial and quasi-judicial positions in Government. The third is that the ability that would be created for the new Parliament to pass an Act to establish a Leadership Code that would affect all Members of Parliament, all senior positions in Government in every sphere of Government activity.
Those are the three issues of the Constitution Amendment (No.25) Bill that we are seeking to have the opportunity to be brought up as live issues by the incoming Parliament. Thank you, Mr Speaker.
