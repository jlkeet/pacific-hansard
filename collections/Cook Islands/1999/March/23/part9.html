As a Party contesting the 1989 general elections, the odds were against us winning Government, which is always the case when you are in Opposition, especially moreso when a Government has some good programmes in place. However, I must say, Mr Speaker, now that it is history, but for some extravagances, such as the infamous travels of Government Members at the time, it might have been difficult for us to topple the Government of the day. But under the good leadership and a co-operative team we were able to topple the Government of the day despite all the good things they had going for them. Our candidates, the personalities were important at the last elections. The quality of each candidate was foremost in the minds of the executive of the Cook Islands Party. That is how the Honourable Member Mataio Aperau came into the fold. We will not traverse the ins and outs, the pros and cons of the Honourable Member because that is water under the bridge now.
When I took the position as Minister of Works in late 1991, I was charged with the responsibility of setting up the infrastructure in readiness for the 1992 South Pacific Festival of Arts. The Prime Minister could not have given me a better Second in charge. He could not have given me a better man. He gave me the Honourable Member Mataio Aperau to assist. We quickly realised there were three main areas that needed addressing. We, in Government, realised the need for good clean water supply and adequate water supply. Government needed good facilities in order that we could invite the people who were coming to the festival and give them good facilities to perform. Government needed to ensure that there were good roads. In the Honourable Mataio, I put my whole trust. What he did is now history.
Allow me to highlight a few of the projects supervised by this Honourable Member. He was responsible for the tidying up and revamping of the National Stadium. He was responsible for monitoring the progress of building the National Auditorium. He was required to reseal all the main roads including the main crossroads in Rarotonga. He was required to reclaim and prepare the Avana Harbour site for activities in that area. I picked up the tail end, previously under the direction and supervision of the Prime Minister himself and the Honourable Mataio Aperau, the main second road, ‘Ara Maire Nui’ in Central Avarua. I carried on the job originally started by the Honourable Mataio Aperau and the Prime Minister in the area now known as the Punanga Nui.
