HON SIR G.A. HENRY: I listened very carefully to the tribute paid by the Honourable Member Terepai Maoate, the doctor and the Leader of the Opposition. I was waiting and waiting for him to announce his retirement from politics, for he had indicated to some of his friends in Aitutaki and to some of his friends in Ngatangiia that he was planning to retire before the next election. If he so wishes, I am prepared to write his retirement speech for him. He won’t need to consult his two wizards of Oz from America.
I want to make it clear Mr Speaker, that I accept the arrangement whereby, if it becomes my job to do so, I will make an amendment to Amendment No. 23 to make provision to ensure that there is integrity in the selection of a Minister who is not a Member of Parliament. I want to make it clear, Mr Speaker, that I am saying that, and I would like the Member for the Overseas seat to convey this. I am saying this not because Joseph Ka has called for an irrevocable commitment from me. I am saying this because I agree with the principles enunciated by Professor Hodges so that there is a provision in the Constitution that ensures the integrity of the appointment of somebody from outside of Parliament. So that in the highly unlikely event that the Honourable Member Dr Terepai Maoate will become the Prime Minister, he will not be able to appoint one of his wizards from America. I want to make it clear that I have a different view about the appointment of a Minister from outside of Parliament from that expressed by the Leader of the New Alliance Party and the Leader of the Democratic Alliance Party. I support the views expressed by the Commission on Political Review and I support the rationale that supports those views. I am happy to endorse the motion that Constitution Amendment (No. 23) be now read a Third time.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you, Honourable Prime Minister.
