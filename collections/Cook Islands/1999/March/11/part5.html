Marine Resources: The Question is:
That the sum of $723,000 for Marine Resources stands part of the Schedule?
The Honourable Minister, Tepure Tapaitau.
HON. T. TAPAITAU: Thank you, Mr Chairman. Greetings to you and all the staff members this afternoon. Kia Orana also to Honourable Members in the House. Kia Orana also to our people listening to our debate this afternoon.
I have pleasure, Mr Chairman, in presenting to this House the Supplementary Appropriation for the Ministry of Marine Resources for 1998-99. The allocation, Mr Chairman, for Marine Resources for this financial year that has been requested totals $723,800 nett. Mr Chairman, I believe, this figure is justified considering the important functions carried out by the Ministry of Marine Resources. May I remind the House, Mr Chairman, that this is to achieve the sustainable development of our Marine Resources for the benefit of the people of the Cook Islands.
The appropriation in the booklet before us Mr Chairman, seeks to support three main outputs. The areas of Policy, Commercial Development Assistance and Research.
The Policy Output will continue to set and review guidelines for the Marine Sector. These policies are means to stimulate our economic growth to provide employment and ensure that the utilisation of our important resources is sustainable. Concurrent with the development policy is the ongoing restructuring of the role and the functions of the Ministry. Services pertaining to the relevant legislation and Ministerial requests are also provided under this Output. This Output asks for an appropriation of $17,000.
Mr Chairman, in the second area, Commercial Development Assistance, its aim is to promote the development of commercially viable marine resources for both inshore and offshore marine resources. It is important under this Output, Mr Chairman, that the commercial development has to be ecologically sustainable. Technical advice, of course, to this industry, with limited involvement, is also covered.
Now under that Output in the Ministry, it sets out some of the sub-Outputs for the benefit of the Output itself. Now there is the area of the Commercial Species Development. An example of that, Mr Chairman, is the recent trochus harvest which was done on the island of Aitutaki worth over $100,000 to the community. Further work in this area includes the transfer of trochus and the giant clam to other lagoons in the country. The first program, Mr Chairman, and the other extension services have been placed around the island on Rarotonga and also on the other Outer Islands. This includes the workshops conducted throughout the Outer Islands on Aitutaki, Atiu, Mauke, Mitiaro and Penrhyn. It also includes provision of support to our thriving cultured pearl industry. It provides advice to the respective Island Councils on the various management issues and of course pursuing further means to expand this very lucrative industry.

The licensing and the surveillance of our offshore tuna industries has resulted in fifteen fishing vessels being issued licences, which brings in a total of $385,000 into the country. In addition, this Output asks for $488,000 in the Appropriation.
Now, Mr Chairman, we look at the third Output that the Ministry has engaged itself in which is Research. It is indeed a very important function that the Ministry addresses. Now the Research Division of the Ministry has the aim of developing and implementing research programs that are focused on responsible and sustainable management of our marine reserves. There is the development of Fisheries Management. There is also the continuous monitoring of subsistence fisheries in our islands.
We have two research stations in the country. These include laboratories and hatcheries. The first is the giant clam hatchery, which is located on the island of Aitutaki, and the pearl oyster hatchery is on the island of Tongareva. Whilst monitoring is continuously done by the Ministry in other lagoons, it is very important, Mr Chairman, that the health or ecology of those lagoons be continuously monitored. Particularly, those lagoons responsible for the cultured pearl industry which require clean water and disease-free conditions.
Another activity Mr Chairman, in which the Ministry is extensively involved, is the well-publicised and highly successful marine Ra’ui program which has been implemented on Rarotonga. The Ministry Personnel inspect the Ra’ui areas on a regular basis. This is important so that rejuvenation effects can be monitored, as well as raising awareness of the ra’ui through school visits and visits to the community. The research output requested for, in the appropriation, Mr Chairman, is $218,300.00.
Mr Chairman, before I sit down I would like to make an announcement to the people of the Northern Group. I want my people in the Northern Group to know that there are discussions finalised between representatives from the United States of America and representatives in the Cook Islands, to form a joint fishing venture between the people of the North and investors from outside. The venture is to set up a commercial fishing company in the Cook Islands to fish in our Cook Islands EEZ. The initial task, Mr Chairman, is to establish a sustainable operation in the Northern Group, and to send fresh fish to international markets in Hawaii, United States, by airfreight on a weekly basis from the island of Tongareva. About the setup, Mr Chairman, a representative will visit the island of Tongareva next week. They will be seeking permission with the Island Council to establish this fishing business for the Northern Group. They will also seek permission to utilise the runway for the purpose of air-shipping seafood to Honolulu and then onwards. They will also seek permission to make certain improvements to the airport and the runway, as required to meet international regulations for flight operations for the appropriate aircraft. They will seek permission from the Island Council and the landowners to utilise and upgrade the older wharf to dock its processing vessel.
