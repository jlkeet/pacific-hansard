HON. DR T. MAOATE:Thank you, Mr Chairman. I won’t be long on this. I just want to go back and reiterate what I said about the people who have listed in this Clause. I gave my strong support to this. I do not want any appointments to be made politically. Because today, Mr Chairman, there are a lot of Boards and Councils

appointed which have hindered the progress of this country. These Boards have become such a hindrance that they are actually delaying or hindering the growth of this country. The people are accusing the Board of the Cook Islands Investment Corporation. They are spending so much money and not putting money back into the Public Account to help the Government. And when you listen to the comments of the people you cannot believe the comments that they are making. You have to listen to what they are saying. This is why I support the establishment of a National Appointments Council so that the Chief Justice or Judge of the High Court in his absence, the Prime Minister and the Leader of the Opposition can make the decision collectively. The main aim is to achieve some balance. The way I see it, there are too many of these Boards and they are getting out of hand.
The only area I don't really agree with is the last part of the Clause where it says, “such other officers as maybe prescribed by the Act”. We do not know the limits or the extent of this part. Thank you, Mr Chairman.
