Mr Speaker, I have great pleasure to welcome Teina Bishop and Mike Henry to the Chamber. Kia Orana.
Thank you very much for your indulgence, Mr Speaker. I now turn to Constitution Amendment (No. 23) Bill. For the record, Mr Speaker, the New Alliance Party supported the First Reading and the Second Reading of this Bill and we intend to support the Third and final reading of it. The most important grounds for our support are the reduction of Ministers from nine to seven. I also want to thank the Prime Minister and the Cook Islands Party Government for carrying out this Amendment on behalf of the New Alliance Party, because it was in our 1994 Manifesto. It was also supported by our August 1998 Party Conference well before a Political Reform Commission came on the scene. Well before. We are confident that the Public Review Commission copied this recommendation straight from our written submissions.
Talking about written submissions, I warn you, lady and gentlemen of this House, to make written submissions because people like Iaveta Short will quote things outside those submissions which will suit his argument of the day or the minute. Surely, when an organisation like a political party commits itself to a written submission, that - and that only - is the official position of the party. You don’t quote all the discussional materials you discuss because you are likely to talk about anything. You are likely to go all over the place before you arrive at that written submission. There goes Mr Short, the Chairman of this ill-fated Public Review Commission, misquoting me from discussional materials.
I just want to record that, because I have quoted in this House before that that Commission was quoting gossipy, discussional materials, which weren’t the true position of organisations. But they used it and latched on to it to support their arguments. So, the golden rule there, from this experience, is to stick to written submissions because you are likely to be misquoted by people like Iaveta Short.
Mr Speaker, I would like also to take this opportunity to place on record that while the Second Reading of Constitution Amendment No. 24 was held yesterday, my Deputy and I were on a holy crusade to the island of Araura Enua. While we were on this holy crusade this House discussed and passed the Second Reading of Constitution Amendment No. 24 reducing the term of Parliament to four years. Mr Speaker, I want to place on record the total and absolute support of the New Alliance Party for that Amendment reducing the term of Parliament to four years. Also, I thank this Cook

We did not support the appointment of an outsider into Cabinet, we never will. But because it is optional, the Prime Minister may do that, it is not compulsory. Because that option is available, the New Alliance Party sensibly supported this Amendment right from the beginning. Because we do not support the concept of this outsider being brought into Cabinet, I want to state in this House that a New Alliance Party Government will never bring anyone from outside. We have enough talented people within our own team to take care of all Cabinet posts. In the unlikely event another party becomes Government and decides to bring an outside Minister into this House, we will vigorously fight for it to be subjected to parliamentary approval.
Mr Speaker, the New Alliance Party is committed to implementing this Amendment when it becomes the next Government. However, it will not appoint an outsider so we are not going to wait or be subject to any arrangements made between the Member for the Overseas and his Party. However, I don’t think any of us should hold our breath because you wouldn’t need to worry about another Cook Islands Party Government for at least another ten years from now.
Now with such a pleasant comment like that I don’t wish to upset anybody and continue. Finally, Mr Speaker, we want to reiterate our support for this Amendment. Thank you very much.
