ORDERS OF THE DAY
Appropriation Amendment Bill, Committee of Supply.
The Honourable Minister for Legislative Services, Vaine Tairea.
HON. V. TAIREA: Mr Speaker, I wish to seek leave of the House to allow me to move an adjournment Motion of Parliament. Mr Speaker I move:
That this Parliament do Adjourn now until Tuesday the 16th of March at 1.00 p.m.
I do not think it is necessary for me to explain. However in view of the storm warning surrounding us at this time, the special requirement of the staff of Parliament is to move everything movable in this House to the containers which you will see on the side of the road in the event of high seas coming through, then we will be ready for it. Of course the extra day on Monday is to allow them to put every facility back into place to be ready for Tuesday. Honourable Members will have a weather bulletin on your desks. That is an earlier report. Now it has been upgraded to a fullscale cyclone. Thank you.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Mr Minister. May I call for a Seconder please?

Seconded by the Honourable Member Mii Parima.
The Question is:
That the Motion be Agreed to?
The Honourable Member, Dr Terepai Maoate.
HON. DR T. MAOATE: Thank you, Mr Speaker. Kia Orana to you, to the staff and to all Honourable Members of the House and our people listening in on this windy afternoon.
Special greetings to the islands of the southern group who are being battered by the storm as we heard over the radio. The only thing we need to do is be prepared, get ready as we go through this time. I rise to give my support to the Motion, as we are already conscious of the fact that this hurricane is right amongst us.
I just wanted to raise the question, whether today we will form part of the days for the debate on the Appropriation Bill. The Minister explained that this would not be part of the total number of days for the debate in Committee on the Appropriation Bill. We the Opposition Members would like to go through each item of the Appropriation Bill and to speak on those items. This is the first time the Minister has done something good therefore, I support what he has done. We will not be debating any further.
HON. V. TAIREA: Thank you, Honourable Members. Please clean your desks to allow the staff to move. Yes Honourable Member the Appropriation Bill supersedes any other business in the House.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you.
The Question is:
That the Motion be Agreed to?
Motion Agreed to
MR SPEAKER: This Parliament is adjourned until Tuesday the 16th of March 1999 at 1.00 p.m.
Sitting Adjourned at 1.13 p.m.
