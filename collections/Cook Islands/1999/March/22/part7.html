The Honourable Deputy Prime Minister.
HON. I. AKARURU: Mr Speaker, I move:
That the Ports Authority Amendment Bill be now Read a Second time
I want to give a very brief explanation on the principle and merits of the Bill for the benefit of all. This is the first of four Bills dealing with statutory corporations. The proposed amendments to the Ports Authority Act are designed to align it with the Cook Islands Investment Corporation Act. Under the Cook Islands Investment Corporation Act the Board of the Investment Corporation appoint directors of the Ports Authority. The Ports Authority is a subsidiary of the Investment Corporation. A number of provisions in the Ports Authority Act are added or removed to accommodate this change and in particular -

Mr Speaker the amending legislation does not completely remove the authority of the Minister. It is this Government’s view that the Minister still has a role to play. This amendment provides that the draft statement of corporate intent must be sent to not only the Investment Corporation but to the Minister of Shipping as well. The reason why it is being delivered to the Minister of Shipping is that the Minister has the job of keeping an eye on the Ports Authority to make sure it is giving effect to the social policies of Government where those are relevant and the laws allow. With the concurrence of Cabinet, the Minister may require the Ports Authority to place greater emphasis on Government’s social policy. However where this occurs the cost of doing so is to be identified and either deducted from the dividend Government receives from the Investment Corporation or appropriated by Parliament. In this way the cost to Government policies will be clearly identified in a transparent way. With those few explanations, I hope it will help us to understand the reason behind the amendment before the House. Thank you Mr Speaker.
