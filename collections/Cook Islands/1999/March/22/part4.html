Cook Islands Investment Corporation (Amendment) Bill: Motion on Second Reading: Interrupted Debate on adjournment of Second Reading. The Question is:

Are there any speakers? The Honourable Member, Dr Terepai Maoate.
HON. DR T. MAOATE: Nobody has explained this Bill that we are amending.
Greetings to you Mr Speaker. I rise to explain the amendment in this Bill. That is the Minister’s responsibility but the Minister is saying that he has done that last Friday.
We know Mr Speaker that the principal Act was completed in 1998. However, the question is why is it necessary to create this body to carry out these responsibilities. Obviously experts have looked into this matter and feel that Government is not able to carry out their responsibilities, hence the Amendment before the House. Government also knows that the landowners of the Airport are angry. Government is very uneasy. Therefore, they created this authority. This body will then be responsible for making decisions, even to the extent of selling this property.
In terms of the Airport, I know that Tinomana is angry. The landowners are angry because the Airport was proposed for sale to foreigners. The Ui Ariki of Te-Au-O- Tonga was also angry. The Ngati Makea did not like what was happening. Now Government is dodging its responsibility and requiring instead the Corporation to make those decisions. The Government is aware that it needs to form a body of business minded people because such a class of people will not be bothered with trivial matters. Their objective is to ensure the success of the business. In most cases they will not concern themselves with moral issues. I believe that this is Government’s aim for creating this body. This is no longer their concern. A body such as this Corporation can handle these public businesses. I am glad to say that while this Corporation may be heavy-handed the landowners were not scared at all. They want to oppose the move to sell this property to foreigners. Likewise the Port Authority. The respective landowners of the Port are also concerned. This includes the landowners of the Aitutaki Airport.
The landowners of Aitutaki got together and formed an organisation for them to counter any move by the Corporation against them. The formation of this body derived from a report by the ADAB. It was also concerned about the undertakings of Government. We seem to be geared for astronomical projects and high costs of living rather than controlling the mass debts of our country. These are the symptoms of a

DR P. ROBATI: Mr Speaker last year the Minister of Outer Island Development said there would be one more amendment to this Act. It would give power to the outer island local governments to take over the assets of Government, such as Crown lands. The Mayors of the outer islands were also told they would be given the authority over any Crown lands. This amendment before us does not go along with what was explained by the Honourable Minister and is not in line with what he told the Mayors of the outer islands. What this amendment is doing is giving all the power to the Cook Islands Investment Corporation. We see that their power is nearly the same as that of Cabinet. I see in some areas the Minister is removed and his powers transferred to the Cook Islands Investment Corporation. This is an area I am not very happy with and I would like to ask why the Minister is deprived of these responsibilities and why

The Island Councils are elected representatives of the people. The Cook Islands Investment Corporation is not the representatives of our people. Cabinet has put them in place. Who are they? Who elected them? Our people did not elect them. How is it that the local governments of the outer islands the Mayors and the Island Councillors have to bow down before the Cook Islands Investment Corporation and beg to have the power to do something? I do not know exactly who these people are on this board but they are people who do not know our Maori way of life. This is why I would be a lot happier if the responsibility is with Cabinet. I am certain that with Cabinet their intentions and principles, the spirit behind the decision making would be for our people because they are elected by the people. Or if it would be a committee and the chairman is a Government Member I would be a lot happier, rather than having a committee elected by Cabinet which has nearly the same powers as that of Cabinet. I can see that the Cook Islands Investment Corporation is replacing the powers of the Minister. This is why I am afraid because the power is being given to people other than Members of Cabinet.
Also we have heard in this House mention of the large amounts of money spent on these people. I would be a lot happier if a Member of Government was put in charge and not somebody else. I am very worried about this power given to a separate group of people and we do not even know whether our people’s need will be a priority to them. I would rather that the money spent on these people be diverted to our people and the Investment Corporation Board be disbanded, and a committee of Members of Parliament set up to do the work that is set out to be done by the Cook Islands Investment Corporation. Otherwise give it back to Cabinet. I believe this is one of those areas that should be taken care of by Cabinet but what has happened is Cabinet is getting somebody else to do their work.
So what is Cabinet going to be doing? Cabinet will sit around with nothing to do and pay other people to do what was expected of them to do. I think this responsibility should stay with the Cabinet. Or if Cabinet thinks that they have too much to do they should give the responsibility to a Member of Government and the Member of Government will form a committee from Members of Parliament. At least the responsibility will be with Members of Government. Those are my views and I strongly oppose this amendment Bill.
