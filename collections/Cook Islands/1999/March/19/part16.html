Cook Islands Investment Corporation Amendment Bill - Second Reading. The Honourable Deputy Prime Minister.
HON. I. AKARURU: Mr Speaker and Honourable Members, I move:
That the Cook Islands Investment Corporation Amendment (No.2) Bill be Read a Second Time
Mr Speaker, today marks the First Anniversary of the passage of the Cook Islands Investment Corporation Act. The invention of the Investment Corporation was an important innovation in the economic reform programme, which our Government has been pursuing during its term of office. Mr Speaker, the objectives behind the establishment of the Corporation was to bring professional management of Government assets by establishing a board of private sector persons with substantial business experience to oversee those assets of Government. The second objective, Mr Speaker, was to foster the privatisation process and ensure Government moved out of those activities which are best left to private enterprises to provide. Mr Speaker, the Investment Corporation Act is sweeping in its progression. The management of nearly every conceivable Government asset was given to the Corporation. It includes

real estate and cars in the Outer Islands. It also includes the desks in schools and all other assets, which Ministries manage. It includes management of Government shareholdings in various companies. It includes the appointment of Directors and the giving of directions to statutory Corporations, such as Te Aponga Uira, the Airport Authority, the Port Authority and the Cook Islands Development Bank. However, Mr Speaker, the ownership of the Crown Assets remains with the Crown. Therefore, in the disposal of an asset or the privatisation of any function, the approval of the Cabinet is required under the Act. The aim, Mr Speaker, was to create a balance between the Government’s obligation to govern, and Government’s desire to inject expertise from the private sector in the professional administration of its assets.
Mr Speaker, it is now one year from the passing of the Act, an ideal opportunity to review the legislation under which the Investment Corporation operates. A number of difficulties have been identified, Mr Speaker, which this Amendment Bill is trying to address. First, one of the objectives behind the establishment of the Investment Corporation was to streamline the acquisition, administration and disposal of Government assets. However, in some respects, Mr Speaker, the amount of bureaucracy has increased. For example, the disposal of the assets requires Cabinet’s approval. Therefore, the disposal of a car or the sale of a desk technically requires Cabinet’s approval. This Amendment, Mr Speaker, will create a new definition of minor assets where Cabinet approval is not required. Second, Mr Speaker, it is inconsistent with Government policy of Devolution, to have such a highly centralised management structure. Government has the view that the best people to manage assets, such as tools, and vehicles are the people who have the day to day use of them. It is for this reason, Mr Speaker, that this Amendment removes most moveable assets from the direct management and control of the Investment Corporation and which will allow Government to vest management in the body which has direct control of them, whether they be the Outer Islands Councils, Vaka Councils, Ministries, or Crown agencies. They will still be subject to a power of direction from the Investment Corporation, which can only be exercised after consultation with it. The aim is to introduce good techniques of management on the one hand, but develop day to day control on these assets on the other hand.
Thirdly, Mr Speaker, at present the Investment Corporation has power to delegate any of its functions. In respect to the assets which remains with the Investment Corporation, which includes interests in land, this Amendment will allow the Investment Corporation, to delegate its authority to Outer Islands, Outer Islands Council, Ministries and Crown Agencies. Fourthly, I will be moving a resolution that Clause 10 of the Bill be deleted and replaced by a new Clause 10, which allow Cabinet and the Corporation, by consent to modify the statement of Corporation during the year. Again, Mr Speaker, this is aimed at bringing greater flexibility to the legislation. Mr Speaker, the Investment Corporation administers nearly $150 million worth of assets. There is a need to have a more flexible system as the Corporation does not have the resources or the staff to manage directly all of the country’s property. The islands and the Ministry wish to take a management role. Mr Speaker, I know of no one who does not support these Amendments. These Amendments will bring more flexibility into the legislation and further the goals and objectives behind the establishment of the Investment Corporation.
