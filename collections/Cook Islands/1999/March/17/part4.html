When the pearls were harvested and because there were fewer pearls which survived the seeding, and therefore less pearl was reaped from the harvest, the turnover for the pearls was the less than anticipated. As a result they weren’t able to pay off the loan. Between the Ministry of Marine Resources and the Cook Islands Development Bank, when the loan arrangements was reached, there was a provision which stipulated that if the 30% set aside was not able to repay the loan obtained from the Cook Islands Development Bank, then the onus will be upon the Ministry of Marine Resources to pay the balance of the loan. And because this loan was not paid off entirely, the Ministry of Marine Resources took over the seeding of the outstanding pearls on the ropes.
During the seeding process Mr Chairman there were around about 100 smaller pearl farmers who just started. They were not fully equipped with the pearl farming skills. And before the seeding process, the Minister of Marine Resources and some officials met with the people and assured the people that if the 30% was not sufficient to pay off the loan then the Ministry of Marine Resources would meet the shortfall.
