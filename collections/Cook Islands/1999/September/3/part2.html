Before I call on the Deputy Prime Minister and the Prime Minister to present their Ministerial Statements I would like at this time to take this opportunity to offer words of condolence to the family whose daughter Teau Samuel passed away yesterday as a result of a motor accident. We pass on to the parents and grandparents our deepest condolences and may the Lord bless you.
Honourable Deputy Prime Minister, you have the floor.
HON. N. GEORGE: Mr Speaker, I seek leave to make a Ministerial Statement.
MR SPEAKER: Leave is granted Deputy Prime Minister, please carry on.
HON. N. GEORGE: To all Honourable Members of the House, to the Prime Minister, to Sir Geoffrey Henry, all Cabinet Ministers and to the Leader of the Opposition the Honourable Dr Maoate and his Caucus members, Kia Orana. To the Honourable Member Tom Marsters and the Honourable Member Tangata Vavia, Kia Orana. To the people of the Cook Islands, Kia Orana and to the bereaved family of Teau Samuel, Kia Orana. As said by the Speaker, we also endorse his statement of condolences to the family. To the publisher of the Cook Islands News, Kia Orana. To the reporter Florence Syme Buchanan and your dear husband Tim Buchanan “Kata,” Kia Orana. I hope you are having a wonderful time this morning.
Mr Speaker in this morning’s newspaper on the front page - appeared an article released about my groundsman and housegirl. Since the Civil List Act was passed in 1984 the Deputy Prime Minister has been entitled to employ a groundsman and a housekeeper.
The Civil List clearly gave the Deputy Prime Minister and his wife the absolute right to employ whoever they want. It does not spell out limitations as to age, relationships or any descriptions whatsoever. In the period immediately after the coalition Government was formed, I did not make use of this provision. About five weeks ago - not two months ago as Florence Syme Buchanan published, I advised the Clerk that my wife and I wanted to award the house keeping duties for a temporary period to our three teen age children. These children aged between 15, 16 and 17 have been doing house work and ground work for many years and in the period that we are waiting to employ a suitable groundsman and house girl we decided to offer the opportunity as job training discipline for them. This is particularly at the request of my wife who is very meticulous about who does the housework as she has suffered abuses from house girls in the past. We have also

suffered abuse from different groundsmen whom we have employed in the past. Sometimes they do not show up or they go to where the pig pens are and sleep. There has been a lot of stealing from the household. Five weeks ago, I advised the Clerk to employ these three teenage children of ours on a contract basis. During school holidays they will be able to work full time and during school they are able to work before school, four hours after school and on weekends - Saturdays and Sundays.
In arriving at this recommendation Mr Speaker, we decided that there are a number of reasons for doing so and I would like to itemise these. Firstly the political situation. We have a by election in Pukapuka which could turn the Government around. We could see a DAP Government in power and therefore immediately that happens I lose this privilege, which means we do not have to send our groundsman and house girl packing. We do not upset them immediately the result comes out. With this arrangement they simply come off the payroll because they are already at home.
And I have explained to you our past problems with thieving, unreliability and also the fluctuation of workers where they don’t seem to last very long. And quite clearly, Mr Speaker, this arrangement is temporary because we are waiting firstly for the Pukapuka By-election to be concluded. We are also waiting for this Budget to be finished before I plan to approach Cabinet about a review of this situation.
This Statement has compelled me to advise this House that recently there has been a threat against my life and that of the Prime Minister. I received a number of reports and the most compelling one was one person who was approached to source a killer from Australia to come and shoot me.
Mr Speaker, to describe a feeling where you no longer feel safe in your home is something that is very difficult to explain. So, we have a security scare at my home and it is my proposal that if we continue to be the Government, I would like to approach Cabinet and the Executive Council to see about security measures.
It could well be that a future groundsman would also act as a security guard for my property in which case the pay will have to be a lot better than what we are paying our present staff at home.
So that is purely one reason why I feel that what we have done at the present time is just a temporary measure to cover the services. Perhaps with the intention to change it later on. And of course, Mr Speaker, there is no better person that you can trust than your own family. So in arriving at this decision, Mr Speaker, I was well aware that there will be leaks from Parliament, from the Parliament staff. Indeed one of the staff went and talked about it openly immediately when she found out. I knew that was to be expected but nevertheless I decided that the Civil List was quite clear about the choice. So it didn’t bother us that this would happen and maybe I am glad it is out because there is nothing to hide. And Mr Speaker, we feel that for this one position, for these two positions, this is a household staff. For this particular appointment I am sure that Honourable Members of this House will understand for that to be left entirely to the individuals concerned. It

Mr Speaker, I feel that this is a personal area. The law says you can have this and it is up to you to decide whom it is you wish to do your grounds and cleaning. I feel the humble groundsman and the humble house girl should not become part of the State appointments. What do we do? Get a Higher Services Commission to appoint it? For goodness sake, let’s draw the line.
Of course, Mr Speaker, if there was going to be major difficulties about it we would simply forget about it. Therefore, Mr Speaker, I mentioned this to the news reporter, Florence Syme Buchanan “Kata” that she might very well want us to set very high standards. But let me state this. She can’t even meet all the standards she expects us to set. So Mr Speaker, that is the reason for this. The explanations are there. I am hoping that there will be more developments in this area after the by-election and when the Budget is finished. This situation must be looked at. All I ask is for this matter to be left to my wife and I to decide. It would appear Mr Speaker, that at every indication, particularly in my personal situation, the State is granting me a small privilege. There are people out there who will not allow me to have it. It is selfish, it is mean, it is unreasonable and to a certain extent it is cruel because it hurts my wife all the time.
We Cook Islanders have a habit of trying to drag each other down and this is just another example but regardless of this, Mr Speaker, I intend to serve this country, regardless of detractors like this. I intend to serve my country faithfully until my time is up.
Thank you Mr Speaker and thank you Honourable Members for your indulgence.
