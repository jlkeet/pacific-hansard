HON. DR J. WILLIAMS: Thank you Mr Speaker. I will continue. My name was stated in the article this morning. I will repeat that because my microphone wasn’t turned on. My name was mentioned with the implication of nepotism. I said I am happy that with this article, that Florence Syme has published this article this morning. Nothing must be hidden from the public. They are entitled to know what the entitlements are of the Prime Minister and the Deputy Prime Minister and also the Leader of the Opposition who is entitled to the same provisions as the Deputy Prime Minister. That is the provision for the Prime Minister, the Deputy Prime Minister and the Leader of the Opposition to receive support staff. The Civil List Act does not stipulate who should be employed by the Prime Minister, by the Deputy Prime Minister or by the Leader of the Opposition. This has been in place for a long time. The fact that I am Prime Minister, and the Honourable Norman George is Deputy Prime Minister, and the Leader of the Opposition is Dr Maoate, does not change the provisions of the Civil List.
What I did not like in the article is the citing of the names of people without asking those people for their names to be published in the paper. That is an infringement of the privacy of the individuals. My brother’s name has been mentioned together with his family.
