HON. DR T. MAOATE: Thank you Mr Chairman. I am sure my wife would be happy to hear you say that, Mr Chairman. I’m only 65.
To you, your staff and Honourable Members and the people listening in – Kia Orana.
Mr Chairman, this is one aspect, which is unique in our society and that is our culture, our identity. We value this so much and it becomes a part of us to show to the world what we really are. Mr Chairman with the birth of a new born child comes the realisation of a culture in which the child is nurtured. It might be difficult for us to accept this, yet, if we recall the growth of our children, they are still small and yet they know how to dance. This is one thing which capture the imaginations of the tourists who come to the Cook Islands. Often we are referred to as a naturally talented race. I trust, Mr Chairman, that when our children are born, regardless how much money we appropriate for this Ministry to enhance our culture, our children are already inbred with certain cultural attributes which in later years will become more and more pronounced.
I want to offer my support to the documentation of our history, recording events which made land marks in the progress of our country, compiling information relating to our customs and traditions and collecting artifacts and handcrafts for archival purposes. We want to safe guard these things to ensure that there is no repeat of the devastation which occurred at the old administration block. As mentioned by the previous speaker, we should be grateful to the Mormons for microfilming a lot of our history.
In terms of our language the Maori Language, I know that we are all destroying our original mother tongue. Even in this House, Members tend to mix their speeches to simplify what they want to say. Although there are complaints about the mixing of the language in our speeches, it does not really concern me because the receiver of the message still understands what you want to convey. The unfortunate thing is that our own Maori Language is disappearing. I have no difficulty with the mixing of the languages because these words are coined from English like the word “time” and frequently used by us. I support that money should be put aside for the purpose of preserving our Language.
The other issue I would like to address is the Library and Museum. In our country today, there are two main public Libraries and Museum. One is operated privately and the other require appropriations from this House to operate it. I am particularly concerned about the Library at the Culture Center (Tauranga Vananga). I would prefer to see Government either run both complexes, hence provide funding for the both or they both operate privately.
As we all know this is one area which I used to complain a lot. The Auditorium has always been a sore point for me whenever I debate in this House. When it was started we predicted that this was going to be a major problem, a major concern to this country. I have been advised that when these buildings were constructed the design and construction work was not the best. I think there has been a lot of corrupt work

The question has been raised whether the Culture allocation in regards to the requirements and needs of the Outer Islands is sufficient. I think this is a very good question Mr Chairman. It should not only be confined to Culture, the question should be whether the $9 Million Budget for the Outer Islands is sufficient to look after the affairs of the Outer Islands. This is one area I am really concerned, Mr Chairman. What is in this Budget? What can this Budget do for the needs of the people of the Outer Islands? That is the saddest part in my view and I think Members from the Outer Islands should be looking at this matter. They should be duty bound to raise those matters while we are talking about the Appropriation Bill. And if I have any qualm, it is the allocation for the Outer Islands and how that is going to benefit the people out there. Would this fund be enough to cover the needs of the Outer Islands? If we are to concentrate on the restoration of the customs and the traditions of our islands, would this money be sufficient? And if we need to document the history of the Outer Islands then the Ministry should bring the Orators or the Tumu-Korero to Rarotonga where they can record their chants and songs, the legends and myths of the people. Do not send the officers in the Ministry because they know nothing about the customs in the Outer Islands. That is what we have been doing over the years. That is all I have to say on this Vote Item.
