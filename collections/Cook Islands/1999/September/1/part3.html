The Honourable Member for Nikao Panama was the last Member speaking. Do you wish to continue with your second speech? Meitaki. Please carry on.
MRS N. MUNOKOA: Greetings to you Mr Speaker, to the Clerk and to all the staff members. To the Prime Minister and the Deputy Prime Minister, greetings to you all. Greetings to the leader of the Opposition and the Deputy Leader and all the Honourable Members in the House. Greetings also to our people who are listening in this afternoon. A word of greeting to those who are grieving over the loss of their loved ones, may God comfort you all.
Mr Chairman, yesterday I was talking about those carvers who are making and selling their crafts. I was speaking on this matter yesterday so I would like to encourage all our womenfolk in the country to go back to the things that we used to do including patchwork. We are used to these kinds of activities. The only ladies I see carrying out this work nowadays are in Arorangi and Matavera. Maybe I should start this kind of project in my constituency Nikao. There are a lot of women who are hand-sewing tivaevae while others use the sewing machine. I know we used to sew another type of tivaevae called taorei. When we went to campaign in Aitutaki the women folk who used to look after the Women’s affairs in Aitutaki requested me to see their art work. I discovered that the women of Aitutaki were trying to revive old skills possessed by women in the past. They told me that they are trying to teach the young girls how to do patchwork as was done in the olden days rather than concentrating on sewing machines. I will encourage the Ministry of Culture to see how we could get women to

I now call upon the Honourable Member for Akaoa.
MR T. TAIO: Mr Speaker, Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister Greetings. Greetings also to the Leader of the Opposition and the Deputy Leader. I do not have much to say. I just rise to give my support to the Vote Item for the Culture Division. We all realise that this is one of those important areas in our lives.
We are lucky nowadays that there is a written record of our history from 1906 but there are others who have records prior to 1906. Maybe some people lost theirs due to the big fire to the old administration building. That was most unfortunate. So I wish to encourage the Minister responsible to take good care of all the archaic and old documents recording our history. Some still need to be compiled by his staff members. We all know the fire which gutted the old building in Avarua. Now, we should be more prepared. A proper chamber or a vault will be required to store these national treasures and protect them from any fire. There are all kinds of priceless documents here and in debating the appropriation for this Ministry, I want to make sure that if a fire erupts the information we are talking about will be safe. I don’t want to be part of that problem. So we must ensure the protection of these documents.
I would like to thank the LDS Mission because they have records, even tapes, containing information as far back as 1906. These were micro-filmed and some people are lucky because the information that was collected protected their interests. If it wasn’t for those existing documents they could have lost their lands. We the Members of Parliament should look at how we can utilise this appropriation to receive the best value – most especially to ensure that the documents that we are talking about are preserved for the next 100 years or more. If we are not prepared to do this then we are only wasting our time. This budget will also affect the people of the Outer Islands. They may have their own archival storage for documents on the history, the developments and the work on their respective islands. I support them. The same emphasis should also be placed on the Marae - our traditional meeting places. Mr Chairman, some people are using this as a rubbish dump. There are tourists coming into our country who would love to see these sites. I would like to encourage the Minister responsible to put signs on these areas to show visitors that these are land marks of the history of these islands. Sign used to mark these places but because of the ruthlessness or the irresponsible attitude of our young people they destroyed this.
