CLERK OF PARLIAMENT: Greetings to all Honourable Members of Parliament and to all those in the public gallery.
Firstly I would like to read to you the Summons.
“Appointment of the 43rd Session of the Parliament of the Cook Islands.
To the Members elected to serve in the Parliament, Greetings.
Pursuant to Article 29 of the Constitution, I Apenera Pera Short K.B.E.,Queen’s Representative, do hereby appoint Thursday the 29th day of July 1999 at 10 o’clock in the morning as the day at which and the hour at which the 43rd Session of Parliament shall commence and the Parliament Chamber in Nikao as the place of such meeting.
You are now hereby summoned to appear on the day appointed for the dispatch of the business of the House and to take into consideration the state of the Cook Islands and therein to do as may seem necessary.
Given under my hand and issued under the seal of the Cook Islands this 22nd day of July 1999.
Signed: Queen’s Representative, Sir A.P. Short
Sealed:
”

CLERK-AT-THE-TABLE: The Honourable Member Dr Maoate has the Floor.
HON. N. GEORGE: Excuse me, I’m the first on the Floor. Mr Clerk, I’m in support of the Motion that we rise or have a show of hands. I think you could test that by asking the majority. This is a voting procedure, we are not satisfied with a secret ballot and if it is a Standing Order which requires us to do as demanded by the Opposition then an indication from us by voting in the affirmative shows that we must suspend the Standing Order. It is our wish to have the election procedures by rising to our feet or by a show of hands and quite clearly you should put that to a vote.
CLERK-AT-THE-TABLE: The Honourable Dr Woonton will be the last person to speak on this.
DR R. WOONTON: Mr Chairman, Clerk of the House, it is not within your powers to suspend any Standing Orders of this Parliament. Unless we vote for the Speaker of this House only then, we would be able to change or suspend any Standing Orders of this House. There is no Constitutional Power vested in you Mr Chairman. As Clerk of the House you do not take any amendments or suspend any Standing Order of this House. So therefore, I will insist for you as Chairman of this meeting this morning to follow the Standing Order No. 10 of the Parliament of the Cook Islands. This stipulates the election of the Speaker of this House to be conducted through a secret ballot. Thank you.
MR T. MARSTERS: Mr Clerk, could you allow me, just a short moment please.
CLERK-AT-THE-TABLE: I have indicated that the Honourable Dr Woonton will be the last speaker.
