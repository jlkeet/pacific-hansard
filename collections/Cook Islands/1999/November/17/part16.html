(MUCH APPLAUSE)
The Honourable Norman George, are you moving the Motion? Before you do I would like to say a few words. So please excuse me. Thank you.
As the President of our CPA Branch, I want to say that I am very proud of you Parliamentarians in the way that Government has changed so peacefully. Other Members of the Commonwealth – indeed other countries of this world can well take the Cook Islands as an example in peaceful transition of Governments, of power.
To you, the incoming Prime Minister and your incoming Cabinet – God bless you, and your spouses as well as your team.
To the outgoing Prime Minister and your outgoing Cabinet and your team, thank you very much for your services of love.
I didn’t want to close this meeting without me saying this but I have one request. When we sit again, can I please have a Deputy Speaker? I am only human. I have urges like the rest of you.
Thank you very much. I now call on the Honourable Member, Norman George to move the Motion for adjournment.
MR N. GEORGE: I move:
That this House do now adjourn until Thursday, 16th December 1999 at 1.00 p.m.
May I also, Mr Speaker, take this opportunity to express our deep gratitude to you for the exemplary conduct of the Chair in today’s Session. I also want to express my congratulations to the Prime Minister and also to thank the new guns of the Democratic Alliance Party and the New Alliance Party for your loyalty, hard work, dedication and support to crown our efforts today with the success that we are now about to enjoy. To my colleague next to me, the future Leader of the House, the Honourable Dr Pupuke Robati, thank you very much for your able guidance and assistance.
To the former Prime Minister, the Honourable Dr Joseph Williams. Thank you for a job well done. It’s a pity that the country did not discover your talents earlier but you displayed during the brief time that you were Prime Minister, especially with me holding the rope around your neck. You were outstanding.
