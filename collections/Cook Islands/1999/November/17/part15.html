I just want to thank all the Honourable Members who have expressed their views on this Motion. It is essential again for me to highlight the importance of following the rules in the Constitution.
This particular Motion came about as a result of Article 13 (2)(a) of our Constitution which reads: “The Prime Minister shall be appointed as follows:
if the appointment is to be made while the Parliament is in session, the Queen’s Representative shall appoint as Prime Minister a Member of Parliament who commands the confidence of the majority of the Members of Parliament.”
Therefore in order to appoint a new Prime Minister, you have to express a Motion of No Confidence in the incumbent Cabinet of the sitting Prime Minister in order to get him moved aside and create a vacancy which then requires you to move this Motion to confirm the new Prime Minister. The first action was under Article 14 (3) (b), this is under 13 (2) (a). I would like to ask the next Opposition to please do some homework before you come back into Government as you would have saved a lot of time today if you had studied the Constitution. Indeed we do expect a quality Opposition from you gentlemen and we look forward to that.
Thank you very much, Mr Speaker and please put the question on this Motion.
