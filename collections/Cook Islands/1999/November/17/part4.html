Mr Speaker, thank you for giving me the opportunity to make this Ministerial Statement.
Yesterday the Deputy Prime Minister and I had a meeting with the Honourable Dr Maoate and the Honourable Member Norman George. The purpose of the meeting was to forge a smooth transition in case there is a change of Government today. We did talk about the possibility of my resigning. The discussion was very good. I was very happy with the outcome of the discussion and it was done in the spirit of consultation and cooperation and that is how it should be. We parted in the spirit of friendship and camaraderie, with the understanding that I may be stepping down.
Yesterday Mr Speaker, I played golf with the Honourable Tom Marsters and he knew very well how terribly I played. You see, when I looked at the golf ball, I saw the Honourable Norman George’s face on that golf ball and I duffed shot after shot after shot because I was worried. I was concerned. There was something bothering me. Last night, I could not sleep for the same reason. I was worried and I was bothered. This morning, I asked the Leader of the Opposition if he would be good enough for us to have a last discussion before the Sitting of this Parliament today. Knowing fully well, Mr Speaker and Members of this House what was on this Order Paper, right at the back, I thought we should have another talk. This is the basis of my Ministerial Statement today.
I informed the Honourable Dr Maoate that I have changed my mind that I will not resign. I informed him that if I did, I would be breaking my word to the people of this country. I have repeated time and time again on Frontline and in the News that I will not resign and that, I will take it right to the 17th November, to this sitting of Parliament. We talked about it in the spirit of co-operation. He understood. Thank you for your understanding.
You see, Mr Speaker, Members of this House, if I resigned I would be letting the people of this country down. The people who believed my word that I would take it to today. If I broke that promise, it would be injustice and I do not support injustice. If I broke that promise, my integrity will hit rock bottom. If I broke that promise, I would not be able to look at myself in the mirror each morning and feel proud of what I had done. I am making this statement Mr Speaker, with the full knowledge that the outcome today would be inevitable. Nevertheless, I must make this statement. I hope that the Leader of the Opposition is still of the same understanding following our discussions this morning. Somebody once said Mr speaker, “If one is prepared to take high office then that person must face the good and the bad with the full knowledge that no one individual will hang on to high office forever.” Should the outcome of today’s deliberation see a change in Government, I want to wish the Honourable Dr Maoate well together with the Members of your incoming Cabinet. If there is an incoming Cabinet, I wish you all God’s blessings. I would ask that you uphold the spirit of consultation and uphold the principles of justice. That justice I have been mentioning time and time again on Political Frontline. I am sorry Mr Speaker that I have not a prepared speech, but I will take it that you will have a prepared text of what I have said for distribution to the Members of this House. Thank you Mr Speaker.
MR SPEAKER: Thank you Prime Minister.
