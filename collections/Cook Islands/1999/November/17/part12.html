(MUCH APPLAUSE)
But allow me, Mr Speaker, to say one or two more words. Because I rise, Mr Speaker as a final expression of goodwill and support to the Honourable Member, Dr Joseph Williams. This is speaking in the light of what has transpired over the last few months and no one is ignorant of what happened politically, involving myself. My wish at the outset is that the two major political parties should coalesce in order to seek stability for this country. Whilst the rest of my colleagues had elected to coalesce with the New Alliance Party and the Honourable Norman George, I honestly saw the opportunity through my association with the Democratic Party as a way forward for all of us. Especially now, a way forward into the new Christmas, the new

Millennium as one people. So I rise, Mr Speaker, to say that I don’t dispute any of the arrangements made because I think the intention was for the good interests of all our people in the Cook Islands. Whilst one Coalition had failed and the other have picked up, I honestly believed, Mr Speaker, that all is not lost. We can still get together as one happy family going into the new Millennium.
I call on the three Leaders of the three different groups to please look at this one very carefully. After all, the Democratic Party’s logo was “People first”. I would like to believe that that logo, that objective will come to the fore now. After all, when you said that I believed you did not mean it to be the Democratic Alliance Party supporters alone. You meant it to be every single person in this country. That is why I called on the leaders of the three groups to please consider our situation properly. Never in the history of the Cook Islands can you give a better Christmas present or a new Millennium celebrations present to our people but now. We are going into the new Millennium. We would like to see all our people in this country happy. To go and celebrate and not be deceived again in our political differences. I thank Dr Maoate and my colleagues in the Democratic Party camp for accommodating me for the time that we were together, due to my strong belief that that was one way of getting our people together. I also thank you for seeing the way clear to release me from the commitment to the Democratic Alliance Party. In order that I could now go back and honour my promise to my Party that I will come back and help rebuild. After all I do believe in my heart that I have fully completed my commitment to you and I once again plead on the three leaders to get together over the next three days –it’s still not too late and look at the possibility of going together into the new Millennium.
In the meantime, Dr Maoate, you have my full support and confidence and wish Marito and Nane all the best. And I believe you both deserve it.
