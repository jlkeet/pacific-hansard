ensure that we can supply the market when they want our produce. With regard to quality, I think that should be left to the growers themselves. I remember a few years ago, Mr Chairman, when we were dealing through Turners & Growers as our market outlet for our produce. The growers were required to have a number as they sent their produce on an individual basis and our each grower was responsible for the quality of his produce. Because, Mr Chairman, your reward was based on the quality of your produce and you were paid accordingly. People did not know who were sending inferior quality produce and who were sending the best quality and those who sent the best quality obviously got the best reward.
And after a while the auctioneers used to know who were the culprits who sold inferior quality produce. They used to make it known amongst the growers that so and so were sending beans with fruit flies in them or pawpaws with holes in them. But after the demise of Turners & Growers, I don’t know which is which. Then the growers used to send their produce overseas en masse. That means everybody received the same rewards whatever the pay became. So if there were some inferior qualities nobody would know who the culprits were and if the reward is low, well everybody received a low grading regardless of the quality of your export.
I think the only way for the growers to be able to get back to the old standard is for them to have their own market and send their produce overseas through a system of collective export. But with a global mark to say so and so sent this quality product overseas and so and so sent this inferior product.
Since I have got a couple of minutes left, Mr Chairman, I would like to say a few words about nono because we have established a big planting scheme of nono in Mangaia. The last time I was there they have propagated over 200,000 shoots and up and above that some shoots that propagated last year have already been transplanted and the people over there are very enthusiastic about the nono because it is a crop you don’t look after. All you do is plant it and wait for it to bear fruit. Apart from the cultivating of nono, Mr Chairman, there are also those that grow wildly in the bush and the people are now receiving income from harvesting the wild nono that is prevalent in the bush in Mangaia.
