SITTING RESUMED
MR CHAIRMAN: Be seated please Honourable Members. The Sitting is resumed. The Honourable Sir Geoffrey Henry was the last speaker and he is not in the House. I call for the next speaker? Oh, here he is. Sir Geoffrey, you have the floor.
HON. SIR G.A. HENRY: Thank you Mr Chairman. I was talking about one of the fundamental principles involved in the construction of PERCA, Public Expenditure Review Committee and Audit. Mainly, the principle of independence. Mr Chairman, as rightly pointed out by the Deputy Leader of the Opposition, PERCA is responsible directly to Parliament. And that in fact the appointments of members of that Board is so convoluted that the dismissal of the Chairman of the Board is very difficult to achieve. We borrowed that principle from the Constitution that established the position of the Speaker where the Speaker cannot be whimsically removed from office except by Parliament. We know that it is important that the independence of the Chair is seen to be maintained and therefore the Constitution was amended to ensure that there will have to be very strong feelings against the Speaker before he can be removed. That removal has to have a two-thirds majority support.
MR CHAIRMAN: Please carry on Honourable for your second speech.
HON. SIR G.A. HENRY: Thank you Mr Chairman. And I think to a larger than a lesser extent, the current Act has succeeded in preserving that particular principle. I believe to a larger than a lesser extent PERCA has worked very well in carrying out their responsibility under their Act. They have written many reports about certain aspects of Government administration and Government management of finance. Many of these have come through to us and many in fact have become public documents. I believe, Mr Chairman, the Committee together with the staff of Audit over the last year have done a very good job in carrying out their watchdog responsibility and I think they deserve our congratulations.
We may need to look at some of the procedures of the investigations conducted by PERCA. For example, Mr Chairman, the question concerning the sale of the Rarotongan Hotel. The Report on that activity was tabled in Parliament and yet at no time did PERCA meet with the main players of that activity. Such as myself, Lloyd Powell, the members of the Board of Directors of the Rarotongan Hotel and the purchaser, Mr Tata Crocombe. I find it incredible to believe that a Report at that time could be completed without a proper interview with the main players involved. So maybe we need to look at that and maybe we need to specify in the Act how the procedures of investigation have to be carried out. And that with as much information as they have must be fully presented to Parliament.
Some have commented on what they see as a toothless watchdog. In other words even after investigations, when they find something is not quite right they are not in a position to effect a prosecution. I think we need to have a clear understanding of the role of PERCA. PERCA is an investigative body. It researches carefully the manner in which Government finances are spent and how Government assets are used. If there is some wrong doings and they have found some incriminating acts or some acts of corruptions, it is not for them to prosecute. It is clear perhaps that a further change

to the Act could be contemplated because the PERCA Act does not specifically tell PERCA what to do when they find some act of corruption or some criminal behaviour. What it says is for them to write a Report and in that Report may be some mention of wrong behaviours. Then they must present that Report to the Speaker.
Perhaps we might consider making a provision whereby if they saw fit they should refer their Report to the Commissioner of Police as well. I am aware too Mr Chairman, there has been some delay in the presentation of Reports. Some Reports have come to Parliament and the delay is when these Reports are tabled. We could give some thought to that problem. In our Standing Orders we make sure that the Audit Report must be tabled in Parliament. This House could give some consideration to the thought that the Standing Order specifically also require that this House debate the Reports of PERCA.
We also need to be careful about the content or composition of the Committee. We do not have such a large pool of professionals from which to draw the appropriate skills for this Committee without conflict of interests. Many of the professionals in our country, accountants, lawyers are working either for themselves or for one company or another. Or the legal advisors for companies or people or several people in the country. And ours is a very small community, Mr Chairman.
MR CHAIRMAN: Your last speech, Sir Geoffrey.
HON. SIR G.A. HENRY: Thank you Mr Chairman.
So it is therefore difficult to put together a body of professionals without a situation of conflicts of interests arising. I know, however, Mr Chairman that the Members of this Committee are very very careful about that issue. And I know that they absent themselves from any discussions where there is a conflict of interest but that is not the only problem. For the simple reason, Mr Chairman, ours being a small community, we know everybody. And while we seek to preserve the independence of PERCA it is impossible to stop them from being friendly or having friendship with people outside of their own sphere. Our House, this House, has very diligently preserved the independence of this Committee. There is no way, however, Mr Chairman, we can stop them being subjected to commercial influence. Therefore, Mr Chairman, I believe that this House should give careful thought to the question of the composition of this Committee so that it achieves as far as it is possible that principle of independence from politics, independence from social and community influences. And that they be guided and they be strictly guided by the responsibilities and the functions prescribed for them in their Act.
Mr Chairman, I stand in support of the allocation for PERCA. Thank you.
MR CHAIRMAN: Thank you Honourable Member. Are there any further speakers? I call on the Leader of the Opposition.
