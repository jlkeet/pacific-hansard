Appropriation Bill –– Interrupted Debate on the Question:
That the sum of $705,576 for Agriculture stands part of the Schedule?
MR CHAIRMAN: The Minister of Agriculture was speaking when we adjourned.
HON. T. FAIREKA: Thank you Mr Chairman. Greetings to you, the Clerk and all staff members, greetings to you all. Greetings to all Honourable Members in the House. The Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, Sir Geoffrey, Members of Cabinet and Members of Government. The Leader of the Opposition, Deputy Leader, and Members of the “Corrective side” –I like the name “Corrective”. Greetings to our people who are listening in to our debate this afternoon, the people on Rarotonga and the Outer Islands, greetings to you all in the Love of God.
I would like to extend a very special greeting to our senior citizens in our country. As Minister responsible for our senior citizens, our elderly, I would like to make this statement. Rest assured that the problem some of you may have encountered when you returned from New Zealand and have to wait for three months to collect your pensions, will very soon be solved. May I just give a word of caution to you, the elderly. Do not spend too much time just eating and sitting around because this is the time in life that one is prone to strokes, heart attacks and other illnesses. So, my advice is for you to go for a walk, visit your children and grandchildren, even your in- laws, do some exercises.
Because we are on the Budget for Agriculture, I would like to encourage the elderly not to eat too much red meat, as this is sometimes the cause of strokes and other complications. But eat lots of vegetables and fruits.

I would like at this time to send a very special greeting to a former Pastor, Papa Enua Totini, who is sick. I extend to him a special greetings this afternoon.
Before I go on, I would like to advise our people with regard to the export of Pawpaw. As from yesterday we have designed a new package for exporting our pawpaw and I am sure this new packaging will improve our pawpaw exports. I have also requested the Secretary of Agriculture, Mark Brown, to go to New Zealand and Australia to seek markets for our pawpaws. We have exported a shipment of fifty cartons of chilli and we will be sending eggplants overseas as well. I have asked the Secretary of Agriculture to see Turners & Growers with regard to getting the best price for our produce. I appreciated the comments made by the Deputy Prime Minister when he was in New Zealand that he had met a group of Cook Islanders from Nga-Pu-Toru and the Northern Group who are prepared to buy our produce, and to develop the best way that we can style our products for the people who are handling the marketing. In the past, people who sold our products on our behalf in New Zealand get the money and slip it into their pockets.
This is all I have to say, Mr Chairman and I give my support to the appropriation of $608,576 for the Ministry of Agriculture.
