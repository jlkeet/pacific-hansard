Regarding the Vote of PERCA, Mr Chairman, I don’t really want to speak about the Vote. I just want to speak about the Office.
The PERCA Act brought into the House was enacted in 1996 as part of the reform package. There were three laws introduced to effect that reform. The other two laws were the FEM Act and the Public Service Act. Although I was partly responsible for the introduction of the PERCA Act, I would like to point out that there are a lot of anomalies associated with that Act. But firstly let me explain what PERCA stands for.
PERCA stands for Public Expenditure Review Committee and Audit. Usually, Mr Chairman – usually, not much debate is conducted over this Vote Item because to everybody it is a very uninteresting Office. But nevertheless, it performs a very very important function. Before I go into what the functions of the Office are, let me go through those anomalies I mentioned earlier.
Firstly, Mr Chairman, the Minister of Finance is responsible for this Office but this Office is responsible to Parliament. Furthermore, the Minister appoints the Chairman of this Committee but if he is to be dismissed for various reasons, the Minister cannot dismiss him. The Queen’s Representative on the recommendation of Parliament is the body to dismiss him. At the same time the Queen’s Representative on the advice of Cabinet can only appoint the rest of the Committee Members. So the only connection the Minister of Finance has with this Committee is as Minister responsible for the Committee. That’s all. No, that’s not quite right Mr Chairman. The Minister is in charge of this Committee. At the same time his other responsibility is the power to appoint the Chairman, full-stop. But the Committee is responsible directly to Parliament. So I don’t really understand even though I admit to being part of the formulation of this Act, what the position of the Minister of Finance is with regard to this Committee. However, the Minister of Finance is also responsible for the Office of Audit and the Audit Office assists the PERCA Committee with its responsibilities.
The present Members of this Committee are: Chairman, Mike Mitchell; Members: Mr John Kenning, Mr Trevor Simmonds and Mrs Tina Browne. In fact, Mr Chairman, if you look at this Part II of the Budget Estimates, under the Vote Item of the Public Expenditure Review Committee and Audit on page 12, you will note that the goals of this Committee are:
Goal 1: to ensure that the Cook Islands General Annual Accounts are true and fair and comply with the Act.
