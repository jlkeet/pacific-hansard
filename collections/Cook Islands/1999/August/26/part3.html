ORDERS OF THE DAY
Appropriation Bill. Interrupted Debate on the question that the Bill be now read a second time. The last Member speaking was the Honourable Member for Manihiki. You have about 15 minutes to go.
DR R. WOONTON: Thank you Mr Speaker. Yesterday before you interrupted me I was giving my Aroa to the nation so I want to continue that today.
I would like to extend my Aroa to our new colleagues on the Opposition side, that is the Honourable Tangata Vavia and the Honourable Tom Marsters. Even though you don’t have any degrees, we will accept you onto the Opposition side.
Yesterday, Mr Speaker, we were talking about the Budget of this nation. Today we have the honey pot of the country in our hands and we are sharing out food to the different Government Ministries so that we can achieve the social equity we so much desire.
Before I go on Mr Speaker, I would like to thank the former Prime Minister of this country for achieving a 2.5 million surplus in his last Budget. Thank you also to the Minister of Finance for his eloquent delivery of the financial situation of the country. I just don’t want our people to misunderstand that this is a surplus of this present Coalition Government, its not, it’s a surplus from the CIP Government previously in control of the country. While this Budget says, we have a crown revenue of just over $48 million likewise our expenditure has increase by $6 million to that figure over last year’s one. I would ask the Minister of Finance whether this is an actual increase in

If we look at the honey pot of the country, $48 million, beautiful honey pot. But inside this honey pot Mr Speaker are flies. Because in it is the biggest fly - the Civil List. I’m afraid my friend the Honourable Member from Nikaupara didn’t see this. One whole million increase over the last year’s expenditure to maintain our political status. For the benefit of our people, I will explain what this $1 million increase is. Just over a half a million increase in the Civil List from last year’s expense.
If you look at the Ministerial Support, we have just under $400,000 and the MP Superannuation by $195,000, a total of $1.06 million. If you calculate this increase it will work out to be about 30% of the increase in our expenditure this year. The Civil List should be less because there is now not 9 Ministers but 6 Ministers. So the Civil List should be less not more. What is this half million increase in the Civil List? Is it to increase our MP’s pay or is it to pay for Assistant Ministers that are in the pipe line. Of course the new Members of Parliament would know the difference in their pay. But I will tell you Mr Speaker there is no increase in the MP’s pay, so why do we need this half million increase? Why is it necessary to give half a million increase? For the last 5 years the Ministerial Support had been $70,000 per year per Minister. Now, suddenly we have a $400,000 - close to $400,000 increase in their support, a total of $739,000 to support our Ministers.
I don’t mind if there is an increase and likewise an equivalence in the output of the Ministers. But I will tell you Mr Speaker, there has not been any increase in the Outputs of the Ministers this year. If I may use or quote the infamous Pauline Hudson, MP of Australia, quote, “Please explain”. I know the Minister of Finance said that we all suffered from the Reform. I take my hat off to that, because that is true. The pain our people went through in accepting the Reform was so extreme. We often say in this House that we should have a private sector driven economy but you look at how much we are giving the economic generators of this country. Less than $700,000 increase was ear marked for Agriculture, Tourism and Marine Resources. I ask where is the economic drive in our reforms, $600,00 is that it? Yesterday, the Honourable Wigmore referred to the Agriculture of this country as the sick man of the economy. I think, he’s being polite. I would go further and say the Agriculture of this country is dead. Because the Agriculture was increased from $600,00 last year to $700,000. Simple arithmetic tells me that it is only $100,000 we have increased for the vote in Agriculture. Look at Marine Resources, the second highest revenue earner of this country to Tourism. It received a measly $35,000 increase. The Minister of Finance, has given Tourism a half million increase.
In this morning’s Cook Islands News, it quoted the Prime Minister of the country Dr Williams saying that we are not deviating from the Reforms. We are speaking about the economic growth and the social equity of this country. I dare say Mr Speaker, that this Coalition Government is feathering its nest by giving itself $1 million increase. I ask where is the social equity? We have people dying in this country, dying to be sent to a better facility overseas to treat their cancers. Senior citizens of this country can’t even afford their medicine. Our children are forced to pay their school fares. The majority of our parents are forced to go and play housie to supplement their low incomes. So don’t hide the $500 increase in the Civil List behind our backs. Come
