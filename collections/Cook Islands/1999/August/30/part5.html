Let me assure you Mr Chairman, that we are very much interested in Agriculture developments including any new ideas that the Department of Agriculture comes up with. We have many of our young men well trained in Agricultural practices and we would gladly invite them to the North to hold workshops to establish small market gardening for subsistence. The Honourable Ngamau Munokoa mentioned that many of the trained agriculturists are no longer with the Department. I also remind the House we only have one Doctorate in Agriculture and that is Matairangi Purea who is now resident in Samoa. I had the privilege of meeting with Dr Purea two weeks’ ago and I voiced my concern about skilled people leaving our country. He was very modest. He would not state categorically why he left the country but I rather suspected that his skills was not wanted in this country. I am certain if the current Minister of Agriculture asked Dr Purea to come back he would willingly come back to work for his country.
